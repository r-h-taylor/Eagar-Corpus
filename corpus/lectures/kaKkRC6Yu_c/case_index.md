# Case Index — Lecture `kaKkRC6Yu_c` (3.371, Fall 2016, 11/12)

*Citation format: layer 3 paragraph anchor (e.g. `§3.p4`). Click-through reveals the corresponding layer 2 passage with timestamp range.*

The lecture's structural spine is the Chaparral Steel case as a worked example of disruptive mini-mill productivity, contrasted with the Bethlehem Steel integrated-producer culture. It closes with reinforced concrete and Corning Willow Glass as additional structural-materials case studies.

## Cases developed in this lecture

### Chaparral Steel mini mill — net-shape casting and four-strand rolling
- **Anchors:** `§3.p1`–`§3.p3`, `§4.p1`–`§4.p3`, `§5.p1`–`§5.p4`, `§6.p1`–`§6.p3`, `§7.p1`–`§7.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Chaparral Steel"
- **Frame in this lecture:** central worked example of mini-mill productivity disruption. Covers: Canadian founders (Gordon Forward), Midlothian Texas plant, rebar starting market, 0.8 person-hours per ton, net-shape casting of I-beams, four-strand rolling for #3 rebar, $450,000 sales per employee, profit sharing, "what have you done for me lately" innovation culture.
- **Materials/systems:** carbon steel rebar, structural I-beams, 36/50 ksi steel.
- **Era:** mid-1970s founding, 1990s described.

### Corning Willow Glass development
- **Anchors:** `§9.p2`, `§9.p6`–`§9.p7`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Corning Willow Glass development"
- **Frame in this lecture:** 100–200 micron alkali-free borosilicate glass sheet, ~2014 release. Tom's inference: must be a laminated three-layer structure (like Corning Ware) with mismatched-CTE middle layer keeping surfaces in compression. Architectural backsplash application.

### Bob Rose light bulb classroom demonstration
- **Anchors:** `§9.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Bob Rose light bulb classroom demonstration"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Rose's 3.091 demonstration — fresh light bulb from Sylvania Danvers plant bounces off wall; day-old light bulb fractures. The point: sodium/potassium oxide corrosion by atmospheric humidity within hours creates fracture-mechanics-relevant surface flaws.
- **Materials/systems:** soda-lime glass envelope.
- **Era:** Rose's teaching era, ~1970s–1980s.

### Glass fiber humidity degradation in fiberglass manufacturing
- **Anchors:** `§9.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Glass fiber humidity degradation in fiberglass manufacturing"
- **Frame in this lecture:** fresh glass fibers as strong as steel; must be plastic-coated within one to two hours or humidity attack creates surface flaws and ruins strength.

## Figures referenced (not cases)

- **400-years-ago wrought-iron labor input:** one person-year per ton. (`§2.p1`)
- **Modern steel labor input:** twenty minutes per ton. (`§2.p1`)
- **Steel scrap price (thirty-year flat):** ~$100/ton, ranging $90–$120. (`§2.p2`)
- **Blast furnace cast iron cost:** $180/ton. (`§2.p2`)
- **Mini-mill capital reduction vs. integrated:** ~20×. Integrated steel mill cost ~$20 billion. (`§2.p6`)
- **Chaparral productivity (1990):** 1.0 person-hour per ton at start; 0.8 after new facility. (`§3.p1`, `§4.p3`)
- **Chaparral sales per employee (1990):** $450,000. Rule-of-thumb baseline: $100,000. (`§4.p4`)
- **Boeing door rule-of-thumb cost:** $50,000 in mid-1990s; ~$100,000 today. (`§4.p6`)
- **Chaparral $30/ton Japanese shipping benchmark:** target labor cost for transpacific competitive parity. (`§4.p3`)
- **Mini-mill US share:** >50% as of lecture date. (`§7.p7`)
- **Rolling pass reduction (Chaparral net-shape):** 28 passes → 11. 75% energy savings. (`§5.p3`–`§5.p4`)
- **Rebar import displacement:** 41% imports before Chaparral's new mill → 0% after, with 20% exports. (`§5.p1`)
- **Reinforced concrete tonnage:** 4.2 billion tons/year, 2.5–3× all metals combined. (`§8.p3`)
- **Concrete vs. steel strength:** ~3,000 psi tensile after 30 days vs. 30–60,000 psi for steel. (`§8.p2`)
- **Glass strength decay:** 14,000 psi → ~4,700 psi within an hour → ~1,000 psi by a month. (`§9.p3`)
- **Willow Glass thickness:** 100–200 microns (4–8 thousandths of an inch). (`§9.p2`)
