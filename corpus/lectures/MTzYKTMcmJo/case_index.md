# Case Index — The Story of Arc Welding (`MTzYKTMcmJo`)

*This is an archival promotional film shown in class, not a Tom Eagar lecture. It does not generate cases in the corpus sense (named industrial-historical or forensic teaching units Tom returns to). It does, however, contain historical claims and figures that intersect with the corpus canon. This index records those intersections.*

## Status note

The aggregate v2 cluster list catalogs **Tom's** cases. The film is not Tom speaking, so nothing in it can be a canonical Tom-case. What we record below are:

1. **Topical adjacencies** — places where the film treats a subject Tom also teaches, so the corpus search can surface the film when a user is looking up the related Tom-case.
2. **Historical figures** — numeric anchors in the film (5,000 PSI pressure vessels, 25 million American cars in 1935, 200,000-pound diesel locomotive weight reduction, etc.) that may be useful as period context for Tom's own historical arguments.

## Topical adjacencies to canonical clusters

### WWI Army truck and vehicle repair
- **Anchor:** `§1.p2–p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `WWI Army truck and vehicle repair`
- **Frame in the film:** Dramatized vignette of soldiers admiring an arc-welded repair on an Army vehicle. The film cites Army adoption of arc welding for vehicle repair as the process's first significant institutional foothold.
- **Note:** This is the film's framing of a topic Tom treats independently in his own teaching. The film is not the source of Tom's case but may be useful as period context.

### WWI Navy ship reconditioning
- **Anchor:** `§1.p4–p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `WWI Navy ship reconditioning`
- **Frame in the film:** Dramatized vignette of sailors repairing a ship's pump that had been sabotaged. The film cites this as the Navy's parallel adoption of arc welding.

### High-pressure refinery vessels with shielded arc welding
- **Anchor:** `§7.p1`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `High-pressure refinery vessels with shielded arc welding`
- **Frame in the film:** "In 1930, with the introduction of shielded arc welding, the oil men turned to welded construction for refinery pressure vessels, by which in some cases they were able to maintain pressures up to 5,000 pounds per square inch." This is a period-contemporary statement of a historical transition Tom teaches.
- **Materials/systems:** shielded-metal-arc welding, refinery pressure vessels.
- **Era:** 1930 onward.

### Gasoline yield improvement via welded refinery towers
- **Anchor:** `§7.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Gasoline yield improvement via welded refinery towers`
- **Frame in the film:** Welded refinery towers doubled gasoline yield per barrel and improved octane. The film's contemporaneous economic argument connecting welded refining to the cost and performance of motor fuel.

## Open questions for the case index methodology

- The film is the first non-Tom artifact in the corpus. The case-index format assumed all sources are Tom teaching cases. For artifacts like this film, a clearer distinction between (a) cases Tom teaches and (b) topical materials Tom presents may be warranted. The pragmatic solution here was to record only topical adjacencies, not to claim the film's vignettes as Tom-cases.
- The film's dramatized vignettes (the Robert/Boss dialogue in §2–§3) are pedagogically powerful arguments for arc welding over cast iron and riveting. Tom presumably used them as set-up for his own historical lectures on welding's industrial adoption. Worth cross-referencing against his lectures on the same period.
