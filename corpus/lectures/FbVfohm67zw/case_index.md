## Cases referenced

### AT&T mechanical switch reliability crisis and transistor development
- **Anchor:** `§3.p2`–`§3.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "AT&T telephone network mechanical switch failure risk" + "Bell Labs transistor development"
- **Frame in this section:** Tom's lead case for the argument that breakthrough technology comes from solving a defined human/industrial need, not from open-ended basic science. Bell Labs in the 1920s projected mechanical-switch failure rates would collapse the telephone network as it scaled; the transistor (Bardeen, Shockley, Brattain) was the targeted-research response.
- **Era:** 1920s reliability studies; 1947 transistor demonstration.

### Honeywell New Jersey plastic-sheet plant turnaround
- **Anchor:** `§13.p1`–`§13.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Honeywell New Jersey facility morale and management crisis"
- **Frame in this section:** LGO/LFM student internship site visit. Morale crisis: tool theft among workers, mutual hatred between management and union, anonymous excrement-smearing in the men's room ("the Mad"). Tom's intervention: instruct the student to buy donuts weekly and listen to workers' grievances. Within two weeks the student had access to the floor and could identify fixable problems.
- **Frame for the lecture's argument:** the paradigm case for "respect-as-leadership-tool." One leader (the LGO student, acting on Tom's coaching) turned the plant around culturally even though it remained unprofitable.
- **Era:** unspecified, likely late 1980s or 1990s based on LGO/LFM naming.
