# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture FbVfohm67zw (3.371 What is Engineering, Fall 2015, session 7/12). This register records every editorial judgment made in lifting the cleanup edit to the readable edition.*

## Sectioning rationale

The lecture is structurally two halves: (1) a paperclips/modulus opener that wraps up the prior session's materials-selection thread (§§1–2), and (2) a long argument about science vs. engineering and leadership vs. management (§§3–13). The second half follows a Nibley essay closely; sections are broken at the natural transitions in that essay plus the case-narratives Tom embeds. Thirteen sections of roughly 2–5 minutes each. The case-narratives (Hartzell, custodians, Honeywell) each get their own section per spec §11.

- `§1.p3` opens with Tom's "Flimsy" — his own correction/answer to the student's tentative guess. Preserved as Tom's voice continuing.

- "I might have shown" (false start) at head of p2/p3 cut — Tom restarts and the corrected sentence stands.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
