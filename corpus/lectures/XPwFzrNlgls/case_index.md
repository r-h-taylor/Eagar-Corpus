### Pilkington float glass process
- **Anchor:** `§3.p1`, `§3.p2`, `§4` (entire section)
- **canonical_cluster_id:** Corning float glass dual-surface manufacturing (closest existing match — but Pilkington, not Corning, is the inventor). Better fit: PROPOSED: Pilkington float glass process (1953–1957)
- **Frame in this lecture:** Sir Alastair Pilkington and Kenneth Bickerstaff invented the float glass process between 1953 and 1957. Molten glass floats on a tin bath, producing uniform thickness and surface finish without polishing. Pilkington licensed the process widely and became wealthy; later family lawsuits. Float glass production: ~53 million tons/year.
- **Materials/systems:** soda-lime glass, molten tin bath, hydrogen atmosphere
- **Era:** 1953–1957 invention; ongoing

### Nickel sulfide inclusion spontaneous glass breakage
- **Anchor:** `§7.p1`, `§7.p2`, `§7.p3`, `§7.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** Nickel sulfide inclusion spontaneous glass breakage
- **Frame in this lecture:** Tempered glass with nickel sulfide inclusions trapped mid-thickness can fracture spontaneously after years in service. The α-to-β phase change in nickel sulfide produces a volume expansion that imposes tensile stress on the surrounding glass, eventually exceeding its tensile strength. Diagnostic signature: figure-8 / butterfly fracture pattern; spherical inclusion confirmed by stereo microscopy and SEM/EDS (with gold sputter coating).
- **Materials/systems:** tempered soda-lime glass, NiS inclusions
- **Era:** general phenomenon

### Bulletproof glass site investigation (consulting case)
- **Anchor:** `§9.p1`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** PROPOSED: Bullet-resistant glass site nick investigation
- **Frame in this lecture:** Site visit where the client wanted to know whether nicks and impact indications in glass-clad polycarbonate panels meant their bullet-resistant glass was defective. Substitute used this to make the distinction: bullet-resistant ≠ impervious; a nick does not indicate manufacturing failure if the glazing still meets penetration-resistance and no-fragment criteria.
- **Materials/systems:** glass-clad polycarbonate
- **Era:** several years before 2016

### Cell phone glass and Gorilla Glass
- **Anchor:** `§9.p2`, `§9.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** PROPOSED: Cell phone Gorilla Glass and screen repair economics
- **Frame in this lecture:** Broken cell phone screens cost Americans $23.5 billion to repair or replace from 2007–2014. Corning's Gorilla Glass (alkali-free formulation related to Willow Glass) developed for impact resistance. More than 25% of iPhone users have had a cracked screen.
- **Materials/systems:** alkali-aluminosilicate glass (Gorilla Glass), Willow Glass
- **Era:** 2007–2016

### Wire rope tensile test demonstration
- **Anchor:** `§1.p2`, `§1.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** PROPOSED: Wire rope tensile test demonstration (1.5-inch diameter, 300,000+ lb)
- **Frame in this lecture:** Substitute shows a video of a full-scale tensile test of 1.5-inch diameter braided steel wire rope in a three-story test frame. Tensile strength over 300,000 lb.
