## Flags for editor review

1. **§4 arc physics omission.** L2 p26–p33 contain substantive arc physics teaching that has been deferred from this layer 3 on the reading that Tom intended to defer arcs to Thursday. Re-reading suggests this is wrong: Tom did teach arc physics in this session before detouring to flame cutting. Adding an interstitial section (§4a) to restore that content is recommended.

2. **Wöhler attribution.** Tom credits Wöhler with the 1862 calcium-carbide-from-water reaction. Historically, calcium carbide acetylene was discovered by Edmund Davy in 1836 (which Tom states correctly) but the industrial calcium carbide process is associated with Wilson and Moissan/Willson in the 1890s, not Wöhler. Preserved as Tom said it per the no-correction-of-Tom's-factual-errors constraint.

3. **"P. Smith thing"** (§1.p4). Captioner transcription of a name Tom mentions casually. Possible: USS Smith fire? Pat Smith? Cannot resolve from context. Preserved as transcribed.

4. **Simmons Hall dedication date.** Simmons Hall dedicated 2002–2003; Dick Simmons (Allegheny Ludlum) connection is real. No issues, just noting the case is verifiable.

5. **Negroponte "Italian royalty"** (§5.p11). Nicholas Negroponte's family is of Greek/Venetian origin with claims to nobility; Tom says "Italian royalty." Preserved per constraint.
