# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 1, §1. This register records every editorial judgment made in lifting the cleanup edit to the readable edition.*

## Conventions established

- **Speaker attribution.** Student turns get a `**Student:**` label.
- **Quotations.** Where Tom reads or recites a quoted passage (Hutchins twice in p19), layer 3 renders as a proper quotation in quotation marks.
- **Numerals.** "0.03%" preserved as a precise statistic. Years as digits (1856, 1990, 2001). "120 years" spelled-out compound preserved.
- **Filler removed.** "Um," "uh," "you know," "I mean," "okay" as discourse fillers cut per the convention catalog.
- **Backward-references.** None in this section; this is the opening lecture.

## Flags for human review

- **p1 opening fragment** ("is a freshman in 1968"): cut from layer 3 but this is Tom's actual first recorded words. The mid-sentence start suggests the recording captured Tom mid-introduction to the class. The fragment is preserved in layer 2 but not in layer 3 reading. Editor should confirm this is the right call for the corpus.
- **p18 "gentile" / "genteel"**: rendered as "genteel" in layer 3 on the assumption Tom meant the politeness adjective, not the religious term. If audio review confirms Tom said "gentile" deliberately as a malapropism, the layer 3 rendering should be reverted.
- **p24 adiabatic flame temperature fix**: aggressive captioner garble repair. Audio review recommended to confirm.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice.
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases that Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
