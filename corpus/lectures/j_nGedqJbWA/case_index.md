## Cases developed in §1

### World Trade Center collapse (Eagar's 2001 metallurgical article)
- **Anchor:** `§1.p23`–`§1.p24`, `§1.p26`–`§1.p27`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "World Trade Center collapse"
- **Frame in this section:** Tom's own forensic-metallurgical article, written in three hours in late September 2001 after the editor of a metallurgical journal commissioned it via Professor Clark. The article became the top Google hit for "WTC collapse" for seven or eight years, established Tom as the Washington DC expert on the collapse within months, and is the reason Tom is on German Wikipedia. Used here as the exemplar for two pedagogical points: (a) you can become a world expert on a topic in 8 hours of focused study; (b) communication for the audience matters — Tom wrote it for a high-school science student and that is why it had outsized reach.
- **Materials/systems:** structural steel, fire temperatures, adiabatic flame temperature of hydrocarbon fuels, austenite transformation
- **Era:** 2001
- **Related figures in this section:** steel melting point 1500°C; typical fire temperature ~1000°C; austenite transformation above 900°C; Oklahoma gas well derrick fire at 10,000 PSI, 300-foot flames, three days, no austenite transformation.
