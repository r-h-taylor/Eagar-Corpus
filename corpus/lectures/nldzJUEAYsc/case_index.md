### Soviet titanium electro-slag welding technology
- **Anchor:** `§1.p1`–`§1.p3`, `§2.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Soviet titanium electro-slag welding technology"
- **Frame in this lecture:** The technical content Tom was after on the exchange. ### Deep TIG (Deep Gas Tungsten Arc) development for thick-section horizontal welding
- **Anchor:** `§2.p1`–`§2.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Deep TIG (Deep Gas Tungsten Arc) development for thick-section horizontal welding"
- **Frame in this lecture:** The Soviets' "semi-submerged arc" was what the US now calls deep TIG. With a thin fluoride flux (calcium / strontium / sodium fluoride), gas tungsten arc at 1,000–2,000 amps with a quarter-inch tungsten electrode can weld 1.5–2 inch titanium in horizontal position in four passes. Navy funded EWI / Ohio State; sort-of works in aluminum and steel; really works in titanium.
- **Materials/systems:** titanium, calcium/strontium/sodium fluoride fluxes, quarter-inch tungsten electrodes, 1,000–2,000 amps.
- **Era:** development from the 1960s (Gurevich) onward; declassified US program from approximately 1980s.
