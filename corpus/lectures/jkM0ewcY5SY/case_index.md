# Case Index — Lecture jkM0ewcY5SY (Fall 2016, session 7/12)

*Guest lecture by Brian Holman. Cases below are Holman's consulting and research examples, not Eagar's. They are still indexed against the corpus canon because several touch on cases Eagar himself has taught.*

## Cases referenced in this lecture

### Refrigerator plastic shelf cracking
- **Anchor:** `§1.p3` – `§1.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `1980s refrigerator shelf failures`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Used as opening example of how naïve material substitution (metal → plastic shelving) creates new stress concentrations at dissimilar-material joints. Holman teaches it as a thermal-expansion-mismatch failure.
- **Materials/systems:** plastic shelving, sheet metal carrier rails, dissimilar-material joints
- **Era:** historical (referenced as years ago)

### Historical water conveyance — clay to ductile iron
- **Anchor:** `§2.p6` – `§2.p9`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Historical water conveyance material evolution`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Survey of water pipe material substitutions from 4000 BC clay pipes through Roman aqueducts, wood, cast iron / ductile iron, and into polymer transitions. Used to set up the embrittlement issue with century-old cast iron mains.
- **Materials/systems:** clay, concrete aggregate, wood, cast iron, ductile iron, polymer
- **Era:** 4000 BC – present

### Wooden gas distribution pipes (1800s)
- **Anchor:** `§2.p10`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Wooden gas distribution pipes`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Brief reference to the historical practice of conveying gas through hollowed wood trunks. Used to set up the combustible-material-in-permeable-container problem.
- **Materials/systems:** wood
- **Era:** 1800s

### Century-old carbon steel gas pipe fractures
- **Anchor:** `§2.p11`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Century-old carbon steel gas pipe fractures`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Generic reference to news-cycle reports of buried hundred-year-old gas pipes fracturing. Used to motivate the inspection-after-substitution problem.
- **Materials/systems:** carbon steel
- **Era:** late 19th–20th century pipe in present service

### HDPE gas pipe fracture on Hudson River barge
- **Anchor:** `§3.p2` – `§3.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `HDPE pipe fracture on Hudson River barge`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Holman's own consulting case. Thousands of feet of HDPE pipe fusion-welded on a barge and pulled under the Hudson; one fracture investigated. Used to introduce fusion-welded polymer pipe and the open question of century-scale durability.
- **Materials/systems:** HDPE, fusion-welded joint
- **Era:** Holman's consulting work (within last several years of 2016)

### Lead-tin solder ban and plumbing transitions
- **Anchor:** `§3.p4` – `§3.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Lead-tin solder ban and plumbing failures (1978)`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Sequence of plumbing material substitutions: lead-tin solder, post-ban replacement solders with worse joints, current swage-style mechanical fittings. Holman attributes the example to Eagar's home renovation.
- **Materials/systems:** lead-tin solder, replacement solder fluxes, swage fittings
- **Era:** 1978 ban → present

### Flint Michigan lead in drinking water
- **Anchor:** `§3.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Flint Michigan lead in drinking water`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Passing reference (Holman alludes to "all the people up in arms over the lead in school drinking water") to motivate the historical lead-pipe legacy.
- **Era:** 2014–ongoing

### Boston hot-work permit / fire watch regulation
- **Anchor:** `§3.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Boston hot-work fire-watch regulation and building cost impact`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Specifically Belmont, MA, where Eagar lives. Used as an externality argument for why swage fittings displaced solder fittings even where the joint quality might be inferior.
- **Era:** present

### Corrugated stainless steel tubing lightning fires
- **Anchor:** `§4.p1` – `§4.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Corrugated stainless steel gas pipe lightning strike fires`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Substitution from black iron pipe to CSST for residential gas supply; consequent lightning-strike puncture fires. Holman shows a fire-site photograph.
- **Materials/systems:** corrugated stainless steel, polymer sheath, black iron pipe baseline
- **Era:** 1990s–present

### Skyscraper IGU thermal stress glass breakage
- **Anchor:** `§4.p9` – `§4.p11`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Skyscraper glass curtain wall panel breakage`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Holman's consulting work on structural glass failures in curtain walls. IGU optimization shrunk the gap layer, raised internal temperatures under morning sun, produced thermal stress breakage. Notes the LEED-credit trade-off.
- **Materials/systems:** insulated glass unit, polymer interlayer, curtain wall mounting
- **Era:** Holman's recent consulting

### Crane rail forging burst
- **Anchor:** `§5.p4` – `§5.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Crane rail forging burst failure`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Holman's consulting case. Crane rail on a dredging barge turntable fractured; sectioning revealed an internal forging burst from refractory inclusion during manufacture. Should have been caught by pre-service ultrasonic inspection. Holman passes the sectioned sample around the class.
- **Materials/systems:** rail steel, hot rolled, refractory contamination
- **Era:** Holman's recent consulting

### Container ship wire rope fracture
- **Anchor:** `§6.p3` – `§6.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Container ship wire rope fracture`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Holman's consulting case on a fractured cargo-container lifting rope. Full-scale tensile test in a three-story vertical machine; broke above 300,000 lb (Holman conflates with ksi in delivery — see editorial register flag).
- **Materials/systems:** braided steel wire rope
- **Era:** Holman's recent consulting

### Lehigh University full-scale bridge test facility

- **Era:** present

### Navy topside weight reduction — aluminum foam sandwich structures
- **Anchor:** `§9.p1` – `§9.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Navy topside weight reduction composite structures study`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Holman's master's thesis work. Open- and closed-cell aluminum foam sandwich structures, dip-brazed in molten sodium fluoride, evaluated as topside-weight alternatives to monolithic aluminum. Holman shows samples. Discusses maintainability barrier to field deployment.
- **Materials/systems:** open-cell aluminum foam, closed-cell aluminum foam, dip-braze in sodium fluoride flux
- **Era:** Holman's MS work (pre-2011)
