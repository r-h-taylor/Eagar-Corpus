## Sectioning rationale

Eight sections at natural teaching transitions:

- **§1** Factor-of-safety definition and history (West Point, Norwich, Galileo, 1850 commission, Tacoma Narrows). Ends at the Tacoma Narrows aside, before the pivot to ASME.
- **§2** Boiler/pressure vessel code (Brockton boiler, steamboat, A36 numbers, fatigue justification). The code/scope discussion closes the section.
- **§3** Other codes — AWS structural, OSHA man-rated, tree stands. The tree-stand digression is treated as a single thematic block within §3 rather than split.
- **§4** X-33 spaceplane case + grandfather levee + helicopter aside. The unifying topic is "what happens when safety factors get adjusted or are uncertain."
- **§5** Pivot to bolting; sets up via BSEE committee and National Academies framing.
- **§6** Bolting mechanics proper — shear/tension ratios, undersea platforms, blowout preventer, springs model.
- **§7** Torque control limitations, Bickford, threads, lubrication.
- **§8** Fracture-reading cases — propeller bolts, nuclear reactor bolt, sports complex anchor bolts, 50,000-ton forging press bolts.
