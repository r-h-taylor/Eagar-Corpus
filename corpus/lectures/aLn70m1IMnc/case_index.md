# Case Index — Recitation 2018-09-10

*Citation format: layer 3 paragraph anchor (e.g. `§4.p1`). The recitation is built around factors of safety and bolting; cases are short illustrative units rather than fully developed teaching cases. Several recurring framing figures (1850 commission, ASME founding) appear.*

## Cases referenced

### Tacoma Narrows Bridge
- **Anchor:** `§1.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Tacoma Narrows Bridge`
- **Frame in this section:** Slenderness and wind — example of factor-of-safety failure when designers push toward lighter structures. Tom says 1942; actual collapse was November 1940 (bracketed).
- **Era:** 1940

### Brockton Massachusetts steam boiler explosion
- **Anchor:** `§2.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Brockton Massachusetts steam boiler explosion`
- **Frame in this section:** Shoe factory boiler explosion (~1905) prompting ASME to develop the boiler and pressure vessel code.
- **Materials/systems:** Pressurized steam boiler
- **Era:** ~1905

### Ohio River steamboat boiler explosion
- **Anchor:** `§2.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Ohio River steamboat boiler explosion`
- **Frame in this section:** First US government research contract (~1836) appropriated by Congress to investigate steamboat boiler explosions.
- **Era:** 1830s

### 1849-1850 Royal Commission bridge safety factor study
- **Anchor:** `§1.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `1849-1850 Royal Commission bridge safety factor study`
- **Frame in this section:** British commission in 1850 establishing the factor of safety of six for bridge design.
- **Era:** 1850

### World Trade Center collapse
- **Anchor:** `§3.p1`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `World Trade Center collapse`
- **Frame in this section:** Built to AWS structural welding code at static factor 5/3. Reference to Tom's published article using this principle.
- **Era:** 2001

### X-33 space plane
- **Anchor:** `§4.p1`, `§4.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `X-33 space plane`
- **Frame in this section:** $1.3 billion rapid-prototyping program. Liquid hydrogen tank with carbon-fiber/Nomex composite, originally designed to 2.0 safety factor, recalculated to 5% margin after bonding problems. Tank split during warm-up after liquid hydrogen test at Huntsville. NASA cancelled the program.
- **Materials/systems:** Carbon fiber composite + Nomex honeycomb + adhesive; liquid hydrogen tank
- **Era:** early 1990s

### New Orleans Canal Levee Failure
- **Anchor:** `§4.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `New Orleans Canal Levee Failure`
- **Frame in this section:** Tom's grandfather designed 1880 New Orleans levees with safety factor of 1.3. Used to illustrate historical limits of calculation capability.
- **Era:** 1880

### Deepwater Horizon / BP Macondo well blowout
- **Anchor:** `§5.p1`, `§6.p2`, `§6.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Deepwater Horizon / BP Macondo well blowout`
- **Frame in this section:** Drove BSEE rename and reorganization at Department of Interior. Bolting failures featured in subsequent near-misses. Shear ram failure on the blowout preventer.
- **Materials/systems:** Subsea blowout preventer, shear rams, hydraulic actuation
- **Era:** 2010

### Nuclear reactor bolt failures
- **Anchor:** `§8.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Nuclear reactor bolt failures`
- **Frame in this section:** Early-1980s test lab case. Tom diagnosed low-cycle fatigue, moderate nominal stress, variable amplitude loading, 600°F service temperature from blue oxide coloring — without being told anything about the part.

### Sports complex building collapse during erection
- **Anchor:** `§8.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Sports complex building collapse during erection`

### 50,000-ton Wyman-Gordon forging press structural crack
- **Anchor:** `§8.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `50,000-ton Wyman-Gordon forging press structural crack`
- **Frame in this section:** Press post bolts — four bolts about a meter across. Steel substitution to higher strength didn't help; fracture mechanics governed and the bolts still failed.
- **Materials/systems:** Large-diameter high-strength bolt steel

### Aircraft crankshaft bolt tensioning
- **Anchor:** `§7.p1`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Aircraft crankshaft bolt tensioning`
- **Frame in this section:** Tom's first exposure to ground-tip stretch-control bolting — aircraft engine crankshaft bolts ground to exact dimension and measured before/after tensioning to get ±10% stress control.

## Figures referenced

- **Factor of safety of six** — 1850 British bridge commission (`§1.p5`).
- **Factor of safety of two for steel** — modern ASME boiler/pressure vessel code (`§2.p3`).
- **Factor of safety of five for ductile iron castings** — ASME code (`§2.p3`).
- **5/3 static factor for building structural welds** — AWS code (`§3.p1`).
- **4.0 OSHA man-rated** — ladders/scaffolding/lifts above six feet (`§3.p2`).
- **2.0 → 5% X-33 safety factor adjustment** (`§4.p1`).
- **1.3 levee safety factor (1880)** — New Orleans (`§4.p3`).
- **~10 helicopter safety factor** — most parts (`§4.p4`).
- **Torque wrench ±30%, hydraulic tensioner ±10%, operator feel ±100-200%** — Bickford/NRC bolting study (`§7.p3`).
- **Fatigue limit at 45-50% of yield for steel** — S-N curve (`§2.p4`).
- **70-90% yield bolt tension for fatigue resistance** (`§7.p1`).
