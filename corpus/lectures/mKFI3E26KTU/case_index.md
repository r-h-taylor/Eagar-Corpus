# Case Index — Lecture 13 (Guest: Adam Powell, Infinium)

*Citation format: layer 3 paragraph anchor (e.g. `§4.p1`). This is a guest lecture; most "cases" are aspects of Powell's own company's technology and its commercial context rather than Tom's forensic teaching cases.*

## Cases referenced

### 2010 China rare earth embargo on Japan
- **Anchor:** `§2.p8`, `§2.p9`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "2010 China rare earth embargo on Japan"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Powell's motivating case for rare earth supply chain risk. Notes Toru Okabe (postdoc in Sadoway's lab, later University of Tokyo) had sounded the alarm in 2006, allowing Japan to stockpile 2-3 years of supply ahead of the two-month embargo. Prices rose tenfold, now down to about 2× pre-embargo.
- **Materials:** neodymium, dysprosium
- **Era:** 2006–2010

### Mountain Pass rare earth mine closure
- **Anchor:** `§2.p8`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Mountain Pass rare earth mine closure"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Best light rare earth mine in the world (southern California), shut down in 2002 due to Chinese price competition.
- **Era:** 2002

### Hall-Héroult cell anode effect and perfluorocarbon emissions
- **Anchor:** `§4.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Hall-Héroult cell anode effect and perfluorocarbon emissions"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Powell explains the anode effect as a key advantage that Infinium's separated-anode design eliminates. CF4 has 6,500× the GWP of CO2; C2F6 about 9,000×.
- **Materials:** cryolite fluoride salts, graphite anodes

### Corvette magnesium engine cradle corrosion study
- **Anchor:** `§3.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Corvette magnesium engine cradle corrosion study"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Powell's counter to the conventional wisdom that magnesium corrodes badly. Cradle is right next to the engine, getting salt water from the road, but hasn't failed since 2006. Part consolidation: ~10 steel stampings replaced by a single magnesium die-casting.
- **Materials:** magnesium die-casting
- **Era:** 2006–present

### Magnesium automobile wheels corrosion in road salt
- **Anchor:** `§3.p3` (implicit, in the context of the magnesium-corrosion qualifier in §3.p2)
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Magnesium automobile wheels corrosion in road salt"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Referenced indirectly as the source of magnesium's bad corrosion reputation that Powell is countering.

### Don Swaay lawsuit against US magnesium producer
- **Anchor:** `§3.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Don Swaay lawsuit against US magnesium producer"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Sadoway involved in a suit against US Magnesium (the surviving US producer) over chlorine emissions. The plant is on the Superfund list and was called the nation's worst polluter; it survives via a 111% tariff on Chinese magnesium and a Clean Air Act exemption.
- **Note on cluster name:** the canonical name has "Swaay" which appears to be a phonetic capture of "Sadoway." Editor may wish to merge or rename.

### Infinium magnesium extraction process development
- **Anchor:** `§4.p1` through `§7.p7` (entire body of the talk)
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Infinium magnesium extraction process development"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Core subject. Founded 2008 by Powell and Steve Derezinski (MechE '90). 22 employees in Natick. Solid zirconia oxygen-ion conductor between molten salt and liquid metal anode. Invented and patented by U.D. Pal (BU). Magnesium fluoride-calcium fluoride eutectic doesn't attack zirconia — the enabling breakthrough.
- **Materials:** yttria-stabilized zirconia, MgF2-CaF2 eutectic, liquid silver/natural gas anode, steel vessels
- **Era:** 2008–present (lecture date 2013)

### Zirconia tube durability breakthrough in molten salt cells
- **Anchor:** `§5.p1`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Zirconia tube durability breakthrough in molten salt cells"
- **Frame in this lecture:** U.D. Pal's discovery that MgF2-CaF2 eutectic gives ~100 microns corrosion in 500 hours, enabling 5,000-hour tube lifetime targets and viable cost economics. Idea dates from late 1960s; Swiss Aluminium had early-1970s patents but couldn't make tubes last.
- **Era:** late 1960s (concept), 2000s (Pal's molten salt breakthrough)

### Silicothermic reduction (Pidgeon process)
- **Anchor:** `§3.p5`, `§6.p1`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** PROPOSED: "Pidgeon silicothermic magnesium reduction process"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Powell's comparison case for the China magnesium production method. 100 kWh/kg energy use; carbon payback 275,000 km of driving. Pidgeon was a Canadian professor. Chinese siting next to coal mines makes the energy nearly free.
- **Note:** not in aggregate; proposed addition.

### Pittsburgh Reduction Company / Alcoa environmental pollution
- **Anchor:** `§4.p3`, `§4.p4` (referenced generally as "graphite anode plant" and the messy carbon side of conventional aluminum)
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Pittsburgh Reduction Company / Alcoa environmental pollution"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Powell references the dirty graphite anode production process as one of the externalities Infinium's approach eliminates. Not developed as a discrete case in this lecture.

## Figures referenced

- **Magnesium density:** 1.7 vs aluminum 2.7. Stiffness-limited weight savings 20–30% vs aluminum; ~50% vs steel.
- **Pidgeon process energy:** 100 kWh/kg magnesium.
- **Aluminum primary production:** 24 kWh/kg, 12 kg CO2/kg metal.
- **Perfluorocarbon GWP:** CF4 ≈ 6,500× CO2; C2F6 ≈ 9,000× CO2.
- **Zirconia corrosion rate (Infinium):** 100 microns in 500 hours; target 5,000-hour tube life.
- **World magnesium production:** ~250,000 tons/year. MgO production capacity: ~1 million tons/year.
- **Ocean magnesium reserve:** ~2 quadrillion tons.
- **Magnesium price:** ~$2.25/lb (US, tariff-protected); world price ~$1.40/lb.
- **Infinium 111% tariff** on Chinese magnesium imports into US.
- **Argon recovery:** >99.99%.
- **Magnesium product purity:** 99.9% from 98% MgO feedstock.
- **Steel productivity context** (carries over from prior lecture): not surfaced here.
