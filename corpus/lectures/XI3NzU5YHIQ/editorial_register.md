# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 28 (Welding Metallurgy, Summer 2014). This register records editorial judgments made in lifting the cleanup edit to the readable edition.*

## Sectioning

The lecture as presented in layer 2 is a single thematic arc — the Blackhawk tail rotor washer failure — but it has clear internal teaching transitions. Five sections imposed:

- **§1** opens with the physical sample and the residual stress measurement.
- **§2** pivots into a generic aluminum heat-treatment tutorial (the T6/T7 explanation), prompted by Sikorsky's claimed prior fix.
- **§3** returns to narrative with the Arkansas crash and the replacement order.
- **§4** is the legal/forensic core: the government contractor defense and the stress-relief failure analysis.
- **§5** is a short coda on modern 7050 wing alloys before Tom cuts off for the next class.

These match Tom's natural teaching pivots in the layer 2 text (tutorial digression at p6, narrative resumption at p10, forensic shift at p12, alloy coda at p17).

## Per-paragraph decisions

### §1.p1 — Stage direction inferred
- Layer 2 p1–p2 describe Tom showing a cut washer and referring to "this part." The physical-object teaching move is rendered as `*[Tom passes around a tail rotor washer cut in cross-section.]*`. The "maybe I'll pass around tomorrow, well I can't probably pass around now" pocket-business is cut per convention 2.
- "Uh so this one we cut. We cut it." preserved as a single statement "This one we cut" — the repetition is purely a real-time speaking artifact.
- "[Pratt & Whitney / Sikorsky]" bracket carried over from layer 2; Tom's "Pratt Whitney" slip identifies Sikorsky context.

### §1.p2 — Self-correction smoothed
- "stre— stress concentration" → "stress concentration." Mid-word self-correction.
- "and that creates the downward force" → "and the washer creates the downward force" — clarifies referent. **Soft clarification** of pronoun; no new content. Flagged here.
- Final "Friction. Friction." repetition (Tom emphasizing) dropped to single occurrence at sentence end.

### §1.p3 — Filler cut
- "And it turns out uh, um, they started seeing the cracking" → "They started seeing cracking." Filler removed.
- "The gray ones —" cut. This appears to be Tom pointing to specific samples; without the visual it's unparseable, and the next clause ("that one hadn't cracked") carries the substantive content.

### §1.p4 — Technical clarification
- "stress corrosion cracking toughness — or his toughness — of 5 ksi" → "stress corrosion cracking threshold of 5 ksi." "His toughness" is a captioner garble of "K_ISCC" or similar; the corrected technical reading is "threshold." **Flagged for review** — alternative readings ("K-threshold," "susceptibility threshold") are possible.

### §2.p1 — Quotation framing
- Sikorsky's reported speech rendered as direct quotation, since Tom is performing both sides of the dialogue.
- "and so of course he says oh, you know, of course he keeps their Blackhawk program separate" — the doubled "of course" and "you know" pruned.

### §2.p2 — Tutorial cleanup
- "I might as well do everything here, it's kind of the next stuff anyway" cut — this is a teaching-flow aside referring to upcoming material, not content.
- "I should say Fahrenheit yet" cut — Tom self-correcting which scale he's using; the corrected reading ("Fahrenheit") stands.

### §2.p3 — Schedule reading
- "they quench in cold weather, so the solution heat for one hour at 940" — "cold weather" is a captioner error for "cold water"; corrected silently per convention 10.

### §2.p4 — Filler cut
- "Uh, you probably — they're probably using — well, I don't know what they're using, anyway. They're gonna —" cut entirely. Tom abandons the thought and proceeds; the abandoned fragment carries no content.

### §2.p5 — Slip bracketed
- Layer 2 has "if I'm at 374 [340], I'm doing my tempering." 374 is Tom's slip; 340 is the figure he's been using and returns to. Layer 3 reads "at 340" with the slip absorbed (not bracketed in §2.p5 because the bracketing convention is per-section per-occurrence; this is the first occurrence and the corrected number is what Tom meant). **Note:** strict application of convention 4 (bracket slips) versus convention 4 (smooth self-corrections) is ambiguous here. Chose smoothing because the figure is repeated correctly throughout §2 and the slip is a single misspoken number, not a sustained misnaming. Flagged.

### §3.p1 — Self-correction smoothed
- "killed six — uh, killed eight soldiers" → "killed eight soldiers." Mid-sentence number correction.
- "three thousand of those — not three thousand, thirteen hundred of those" → "thirteen hundred of those." Self-correction smoothed.
- "from T6 to C7 [T7]" → quoted as "from T6 to T7." Captioner slip on Tom's spoken "T7"; corrected within the quotation.

### §4.p1 — Backward reference preserved
- "We talked about the great big Alcoa plates" preserved per convention 5 exception — this carries actual content (pointing to a specific prior case: the Davenport stretch stress relief) and is needed to anchor the analogy.

### §4.p4 — Technical slip preserved with hedge
- Layer 2 p15 ends with Tom saying "So what they should have had was a T662." This appears to be Tom mis-stating the designation (T7-something-62 would be the consistent reading — over-aged plus compression stress relieved). Layer 3 reads "a T-7-something-62" to preserve Tom's evident intent without inventing a specific designation he didn't say. **Flagged.**
- "how do you do a 5-1, how do you pull to stretch that washer" smoothed to "How do you stretch a washer."

### §5.p1 — Reading order
- "I'm actually — I can show you this page first, at the bottom. Um, you can buy this as T7651, 7050, or 7075" — the "page first, at the bottom" pointing is cut; the alloy reading is preserved. Tom's "T7651, 7050" was likely meant as "7050-T7651" (alloy then temper); layer 3 renders the standard form.

### §5.p3 — Cut-off preserved
- The trailing "Who's presenting to—" preserved as the literal cut-off, since this is the class transition Tom is moving into. Em-dash signals incompleteness.

## Soft reordering log

No cross-paragraph reordering performed. Within §3.p1, Tom's narrative jumps forward to the Arkansas crash and then back to the Navy ordering replacements; layer 3 preserves Tom's order (Arkansas mention → Navy story → replacement order → fleet swap) because reordering would obscure that Tom is foreshadowing the crash before telling it properly in §3.p2.

## Flags for editor review

1. **§1.p4** — "stress corrosion cracking threshold" reading of "his toughness" captioner garble. Alternative: "K_ISCC threshold," "susceptibility threshold."
2. **§2.p5** — Smoothing the "374" slip rather than bracketing it. Decision could go either way.
3. **§4.p4** — Rendering "T662" as "T-7-something-62." Tom's evident intent is the over-aged + compression-stress-relieved designation, but the specific number system isn't fully consistent in his telling.
4. **§1.p1 stage direction** — Inferred from context that Tom is showing/passing a physical sample. The "you can see the saw cut right down there" language strongly implies it, but no explicit "here, look at this" handoff.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could do this because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases that Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
