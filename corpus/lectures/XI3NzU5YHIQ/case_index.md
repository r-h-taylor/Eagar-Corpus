# Case Index — Lecture 28 (Welding Metallurgy, Summer 2014)

*Citation format: layer 3 paragraph anchor (e.g. `§4.p2`).*

This lecture is built around one central case — the Blackhawk tail rotor washer stress corrosion cracking failure — developed across all five sections. Several adjacent cases surface as cross-references.

## Cases developed in this lecture

### Blackhawk tail rotor washer stress corrosion cracking
- **Anchors:** `§1.p1`–`§1.p4`, `§2.p1`, `§3.p1`–`§3.p2`, `§4.p1`–`§4.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Black Hawk helicopter tail rotor washer failure"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Tom's central forensic case for the lecture. 6061-T6 aluminum washer cracking on the shelf due to residual stresses (8 ksi) exceeding the alloy's stress corrosion cracking threshold (5 ksi) in marine atmosphere. Sikorsky had previously seen this on CH-53/MH-53 and switched to T7 over-aged heat treatment but failed to apply the fix to the Blackhawk part. Bars purchased as T-651 (stretched, stress relieved) were sliced, machined, and reheat-treated, wiping out the stretch stress relief. Government contractor defense raised by Sikorsky in subsequent litigation.
- **Materials/systems:** 6061-T6, T-651, T-62, T-7 aluminum heat treatments; aluminum washer; helicopter tail rotor friction-fit assembly.
- **Era:** Early 1990s consulting work; Arkansas Army crash subsequent.

### Arkansas Blackhawk crash (eight fatalities)
- **Anchors:** `§3.p1`, `§3.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "PROPOSED: Arkansas Blackhawk night-vision-goggle exercise crash (eight fatalities)"
- **Frame in this lecture:** The fatal-failure realization of the same washer defect Tom was investigating for the Navy. Night vision goggle training exercise, cracked washer with T6 heat treatment, eight soldiers killed and one in a coma. Triggered the litigation in which Sikorsky raised the government contractor defense.
- **Materials/systems:** Same washer; T6 heat treatment.
- **Era:** Early-to-mid 1990s.
- **Note:** This may be the same event as or distinct from "CH-53/MH-53 helicopter washer corrosion problem" in the canon. Tom treats it as a separate Blackhawk-specific incident downstream of the CH-53/MH-53 fix.

- **Anchor:** `§4.p1`, also referenced in `§5.p1`

- **Frame in this lecture:** Cross-reference for the mechanical (T-51) stress relief mechanism. Tom invokes the Davenport six-inch-thick plate stretching as the canonical example of the operation Sikorsky's washer process destroyed.
- **Note:** Also tied to "Boeing aluminum plate residual stress relief (Davenport Iowa)" and "Alcoa Davenport aluminum rolling mill thickness variation" — these may be one cluster or related clusters.
