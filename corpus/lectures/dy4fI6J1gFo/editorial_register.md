### §4 — Compressing a cracked pipe

This section is heavy Q&A. The fragmentary back-and-forth (layer 2 `p17`) is the hardest editorial challenge in the lecture. Tom is interrupting and being interrupted, and the student's voice and Tom's voice braid together.

- **Speaker attribution.** The exchange is broken into discrete turns. Where a student speaks, **Student:** label is added. Where Tom asks a clarifying question, his turn is kept in plain text. This is editorial inference from context — the raw transcript has no speaker tags.
- **§4.p5.** Student answer compressed. Layer 2: "Uh no, I think there was like maybe sixty percent. Oh okay... Because what you — from what you're telling me I would assume they're on the outside." Layer 3 splits this: student's "sixty percent / outside" answer in §4.p5, Tom's response in §4.p6. This is *not* reordering — it's disentangling overlapping turns.
- **§4.p8.** Tom's "you know, the thing is going to — the weld is going to" sequence smoothed; the "this is, there's a center line through both of these right, this is just one half of the pipe, right, twelve o'clock position or whatever" mid-paragraph orientation aside cut. It refers to a diagram Tom is drawing that the reader can't see; the operative claim (compressive stresses at the crack) is preserved.
- **§4.p10.** "All codes say if you find a crack you got to grind it out. Whether you just — if you can grind it out to a certain depth and you don't even have to repair it, you can just, you know, grind out the crack, get rid of the thing." Smoothed to "Sometimes you can grind it out to a certain depth and you don't even have to repair it — just grind out the crack, get rid of the thing."
- **§4.p11.** "Yeah, did you have a comment? No, okay" cut — this is a brief checkin with a student that goes nowhere. The "stop drilling — stop drilling holes, stop drilling cracks" mid-sentence pivot smoothed to "Stop-drilling cracks."

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
