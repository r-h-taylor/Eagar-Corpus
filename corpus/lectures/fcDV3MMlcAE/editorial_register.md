# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 22 (Summer 2014, Welding Metallurgy, session 22/35). This register records the editorial judgments made in lifting the cleanup edit to the readable edition.*

## Sectioning

The lecture opens mid-thought (the tail of a hydrogen-cracking case from the previous session). I gave that tail its own short §1 rather than folding it into §2, because the topic — hydrogen cracking and a forty-million-dollar engine plant — is unrelated to the weld-microstructure material that begins at 00:35. Seven sections total for ~18 minutes of audio; section breaks fall at clear topic pivots (microstructure → LNG case → Charpy regulation history → productivity → multi-electrode physics → panel line).

## Per-section / per-paragraph decisions

- Filler trimmed: "okay" cut where mid-clause; preserved where it closes a thought ("Hey, but anyway" → cut).
- "Um, only costs forty million dollars" → "Only cost forty million dollars" (past tense; "um" cut).

## Soft reordering log

No reordering across paragraphs within any section. The only structural choice was merging l2 `p5` and `p6` into §3.p3 (both treat the same machining-shop anecdote with no topic break between them).

## Flags for editor review

- **§7.p5 "I'm not supposed to say with thickness"** — possible classified-information self-stop. May warrant a footnote; left as Tom delivered it.
- **§3.p5 Burns/Sparrows slip** — Burns Harbor and Sparrows Point are both Bethlehem Steel plants; the slip is between two real plants, not a misnaming. The bracket form `Burns [Sparrows]` flags this but the editor may want a footnote distinguishing the two plants for a reader who doesn't know the difference.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could do this because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases that Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
