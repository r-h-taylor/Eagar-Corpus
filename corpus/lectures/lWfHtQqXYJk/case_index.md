# Case Index — Lecture 18 (Welding Metallurgy, Summer 2015)

*Citation format: layer 3 paragraph anchor (e.g. `§4.p2`). Click-through in the static site reveals the corresponding layer 2 passage with timestamp range.*

This lecture is the corrosion capstone for the Summer 2015 Welding Metallurgy course. Tom moves from Pourbaix thermodynamics through kinetics, the eight forms of corrosion, four case studies (Brazilian helicopter rotor, Mass General subway, Canadian helicopter spline, warehouse sprinkler), hot corrosion of turbine blades, and closes with a Tufte/PowerPoint coda.

## Cases referenced

### Iridium engagement ring (Tom's wife)
- **Anchor:** `§2.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** *PROPOSED: Iridium engagement ring (Tom's wife, electron-beam melted)*
- **Frame in this lecture:** Tom electron-beam melted an iridium alloy for the ring because of its corrosion immunity. Sets up the Cannon-connector electrocution story.
- **Materials:** Iridium alloy, electron beam melting
- **Era:** 1973

### Electron beam melter Cannon connector arc tracking
- **Anchor:** `§2.p5`, `§2.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Cannon connector arc tracking fault in electron beam melter"
- **Frame in this lecture:** While melting platinum-iridium in 1973, Tom heard repeated arcing inside a vacuum chamber for three weeks. Eventually traced to arc tracking inside an Army-surplus Cannon connector. Paired with the 1962 fatality on the same equipment.
- **Materials:** Platinum-iridium, electron beam melter, Cannon connector
- **Era:** 1962 (fatality), 1973 (Tom's experience)

### Electron beam melter fatal electrocution (1962)
- **Anchor:** `§2.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Electron beam melter fatal electrocution"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Christmas Eve 1962, a researcher at the same lab where Tom later worked was electrocuted reaching to pull a sparking wire. 6,000 volts; wet shoe soles from rain.
- **Era:** Christmas Eve 1962

### Johnson & Johnson electric cautery instrument corrosion
- **Anchor:** `§2.p8`, `§2.p9`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Johnson & Johnson electrocauterization device corrosion failure"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Cardiac surgery cautery probes corroded under 100V across all metal candidates (stainless, nickel alloys, even platinum and gold). Tom's recommendation: make the tip disposable.
- **Materials:** Stainless steel, nickel alloys, platinum, gold; aqueous chloride environment (blood)
- **Era:** 1980s

### 1948 seaplane exfoliation failure (Miami)
- **Anchor:** `§3.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "1948 seaplane exfoliation failure"
- **Frame in this lecture:** One of only two exfoliation corrosion cases Tom has personally seen. Crashed near Tom's Miami apartment.
- **Materials:** Aluminum alloy (exfoliation)
- **Era:** 1948 build, ~2010 failure

### Homemade aluminum water-jet fishing boat exfoliation (Maine)
- **Anchor:** `§3.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Homemade aluminum water-jet fishing boat exfoliation failure"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Maine builder constructed a water-jet-propelled aluminum fishing boat; seawater velocity-driven exfoliation destroyed the hull within six months. The second of Tom's two personal exfoliation cases.
- **Materials:** Aluminum (incorrect alloy choice for seawater service)

### Dedham, Massachusetts 30-year brass plumbing valve dealloying
- **Anchor:** `§3.p7`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Dedham, Massachusetts 30-year-old brass plumbing valve failure"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Dealloying (dezincification) of a brass valve over 30 years caused a flood with half a million dollars damage.
- **Materials:** Brass (Cu-Zn), dezincification
- **Era:** ~30 years prior to consultation

### Brazilian helicopter rotor blade corrosion-fatigue failure
- **Anchor:** `§4.p1`, `§4.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Bell Helicopter rotor blade corrosion fatigue failure (Brazil)"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Operators avoiding Bell-authorized service center costs ground down rotor dings without proper repainting. Stress-corrosion-assisted fatigue from poor paint coverage propagated cracks until the blade flew off, killing several. Tom's fracture-mechanics analysis showed the initial flaw was too small for the proposed pitting-only mechanism; stress-corrosion fatigue was required.
- **Materials:** Aluminum rotor blade, paint coating, seawater/humid environment
- **Era:** Date not specified in this lecture

### Mass General subway station rust removal consult
- **Anchor:** `§5.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Mass General subway stop welding investigation" / "Mass General subway station rust removal"
- **Frame in this lecture:** 1910-vintage subway station; engineering firm chipped half-inch-thick rust off structural beams down to white metal. Tom's recommendation: plug it back up — the rust was structural and stopping further corrosion.
- **Era:** 1910 build, consultation ~18 years prior to lecture (~1997)

### Northeast pier high-strength bolt hydrogen embrittlement
- **Anchor:** `§5.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "High-strength bridge bolts hydrogen embrittlement in Northeast"
- **Frame in this lecture:** 140 ksi bolts on a pier in New Jersey or Connecticut; no cathodic protection or inadequate zinc coating; hydrogen embrittlement failures within one to two years.
- **Materials:** 140 ksi high-strength steel bolts, marine environment

### Canadian aerospace helicopter spline nitriding hydrogen embrittlement
- **Anchor:** `§6.p1`, `§6.p2`, `§6.p3`, `§6.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Helicopter engine peashooter nitriding failure and lawsuit"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Three-quarter-inch high-speed transmission spline, nitrided for surface hardness. The 24-hour hydrogen bake-out was being performed *after* a three-day destructive case-thickness inspection, by which time delayed hydrogen cracking had already taken hold. Engine power loss in flight; people killed. ITAR-classified, so Tom (as US citizen) read the documents while the Canadian investigator could not. Tom invoked his professional duty to public safety to demand the manufacturer be notified outside the litigation timeline.
- **Materials:** High-strength steel spline, nitriding bath
- **Era:** 1980s/1990s
- **Note:** This is a major case for the professional-duty thread in Tom's teaching.

### Warehouse fire sprinkler pipe corrosion (leak-driven oxygen makeup)
- **Anchor:** `§7.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Boston warehouse fire protection system corrosion" / "Warehouse fire protection system piping corrosion"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Large warehouse's entire fire sprinkler system was fully occluded with rust. Root cause: a tolerated leak introduced makeup water (and fresh oxygen) to what should have been a closed system. All piping required replacement.

### Mexican airline jet turbine blade hot corrosion
- **Anchor:** `§8.p1`, `§8.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Mexican airline jet turbine blade sulfidation" / "Mexican airline hot corrosion engine failure"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Hot corrosion by sulfur/oxygen occurs in a narrow 100°C window where the kinetics peak and the sulfide condenses. Mexican airline practiced no-warmup full-power takeoffs to save fuel, dwelling in the corrosion-sensitive temperature band for the critical minutes that they should have spent above it.
- **Materials:** Nickel-base turbine blade superalloy, sulfide hot corrosion
