# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 1 (njIMQR4hBOU), Fall 2016, 3.371 Materials Selection. This register records the editorial judgments made in lifting Tom's first-lecture-of-the-semester logistics-and-externalities discussion to a readable edition.*

## Sectioning

Seven sections. Lecture 1 of a semester is unusual in shape — heavy on logistics and on theme-setting rather than on developed cases. Sections fall at:
- §1 → §2: Pivot from course logistics to substantive teaching ("I sometimes use this class to try to figure out…")
- §2 → §3: Pivot from Bell Labs / industry research arc to the WTC paper handout ("I handed out a paper on the World Trade Center")
- §3 → §4: Pivot from writing advice to structural vs. functional materials framing ("The paper you probably should read out of this is called 'Socioengineering'")
- §4 → §5: Pivot from Augustine's framing to the operational definition of externality ("Anybody taking an economics course?")
- §5 → §6: Pivot from environmental/legislative externalities (Pennzoil) to social/cultural/military ones ("Another externality is social. Conflict diamonds")
- §6 → §7: Student question opens new arc on positive externalities and renewables

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could do this because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases that Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
