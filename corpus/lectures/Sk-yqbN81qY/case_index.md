## Named failure mechanisms developed in this lecture

These are not cases (no specific failure incident is named), but the lecture treats them as teachable mechanisms with structural depth comparable to a case.

### Hydrogen-induced cracking in martensitic stainless steels
- **Anchor:** `§2.p1`, `§2.p3`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Primary cracking concern in welding martensitic alloys. Addressed via preheat (400–600°F), low-hydrogen practice, and post-weld heat treatment.

### Reheat cracking / strain age cracking
- **Anchor:** `§2.p3`, `§5.p4`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Post-weld aging promotes carbide precipitation along grain boundaries, leading to further cracking. Strain age cracking in nickel alloys is the analog.

### Solidification cracking in nickel-based alloys
- **Anchor:** `§6.p1`–`§6.p6`, `§7.p1`–`§7.p5`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Driven by liquid film wetting along solidification grain boundaries with low-melting eutectic phases (Laves phase, sulfur/phosphorus impurities). Mitigated via solution-annealed welding, joint design (convex fillets, stringer beads), multiple-pass welds, and resistant filler choice.

### HAZ liquation cracking
- **Anchor:** `§7.p6`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Lecture cut off mid-introduction. Mechanism: partial melting of low-temperature phases in the heat affected zone.

### Ductility dip cracking
- **Anchor:** `§5.p4`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Mentioned in passing as a fourth weldability issue, deferred to the textbook.
