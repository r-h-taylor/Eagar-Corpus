# Case Index — Lecture 11 (Solid State Welding, Spring 2013)

*Citation format: layer 3 paragraph anchor (e.g. `§2.p5`).*

This lecture is primarily a technical exposition of soldering science and microelectronics interconnect history. Cases appear as illustrative asides and historical reference points rather than as developed forensic teaching units.

## Cases referenced

### Russian tin stockpile failure (WWII)
- **Anchor:** `§2.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Russian tin stockpile failure (WWII)"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Illustration of tin pest (white-tin to gray-tin transformation at -18°C). Used to explain why antimony and lead inhibitors are specified in solder, though Tom notes the historical rationale turned out not to be the operative reason.
- **Materials/systems:** pure tin; allotropic transformation
- **Era:** WWII

### Bell Labs transistor development
- **Anchor:** `§8.p2`, `§8.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Bell Labs transistor development"
- **Frame in this lecture:** AT&T's 1925 reliability study of mechanical switches led to the projection that the telephone system would collapse around 1960. Tom uses this to argue the transistor was *applied* research, not basic — invented to replace mechanical switches whose failure-in-time rate was unacceptable for a growing network.
- **Materials/systems:** mechanical relay switches; semiconductors
- **Era:** 1925 study → 1947 invention

### AT&T telephone network mechanical switch failure risk
- **Anchor:** `§8.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "AT&T telephone network mechanical switch failure risk"
- **Frame in this lecture:** The 1 in 10,000 per 10 years FIT rate that drove the projection of system collapse. Origin of the "FITs" (failures in time) reliability terminology now used across semiconductors.
- **Era:** 1925–1960

### IBM copper interconnect diffusion barrier innovation
- **Anchor:** `§4.p2`, `§4.p3`, `§7.p1`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "IBM copper interconnect diffusion barrier innovation"
- **Frame in this lecture:** IBM's late-1990s announcement of a copper-on-chip process with a tungsten diffusion barrier, replacing aluminum interconnects. Tom uses it to explain why 3 GHz processor clocks became achievable.
- **Materials/systems:** copper interconnect; tungsten diffusion barrier; silicon
- **Era:** late 1990s

### IBM interconnect density prediction and C4 development
- **Anchor:** `§8.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "IBM interconnect density prediction and C4 development"
- **Frame in this lecture:** IBM developed controlled-collapse chip connection (C4) area-array bonding in the 1960s in anticipation of needs that wouldn't arise for decades. Tom uses it to illustrate forward-looking applied research.
- **Era:** 1960s

### Intel chip-on-board packaging transition
- **Anchor:** `§6.p1`, `§6.p2`, `§6.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Intel chip-on-board packaging transition"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Tab-bonded tape (~$50/piece in 1995) as the precursor to chip-on-board technology that eliminated the aluminum oxide package. Includes Tom's anecdote of meeting Craig Barrett at Intel Chandler, Arizona, and the cubicle-culture aside.
- **Materials/systems:** tab tape; aluminum oxide packages
- **Era:** mid-1990s

### Cisco Systems PCB manufacturing quality control
- **Anchor:** `§6.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Cisco Systems PCB manufacturing quality control"
- **Frame in this lecture:** A Cisco PCB from circa 1997 used to illustrate the via-yield problem in multilayer board manufacture. Tom mentions student work at the New Hampshire plant.
- **Era:** late 1990s

### Motorola Defense Division classified large-format chip bonding
- **Anchor:** `§6.p7`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Motorola Defense Division classified large-format chip bonding"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Early-1990s DoD program at Motorola making 1–2 inch chips with integrated functions to circumvent the gigahertz signal-distance limit. Tom uses it to illustrate the cost of staying in a small-chip regime once parasitic delays become limiting.
- **Era:** early 1990s

### Pentium 6 / Early 1990s large-format chip bonding
- **Anchor:** `§4.p7`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Pentium 6 / Early 1990s large-format chip bonding"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Two-chip tab tape (1 cm² processor + ~0.6 cm² memory) circa 2000, used to show the intermediate state before everything moved on-chip.
- **Era:** circa 2000

### Doctoral exam vacuum reverberatory furnace design error
