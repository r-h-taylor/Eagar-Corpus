## Decisions that would benefit from editor (Taylor) review

1. **§1.p7 lead-paint aside cut.** The strongest editorial intervention in this lecture. Tom delivers a sustained rhetorical aside about lead paint not causing cognitive impairment ("it has nothing to do with the lead paint. It has to do with genetics. That's why your child is stupid"). This contradicts established medical evidence on lead neurotoxicity. Cut from layer 3 because it actively misleads. Preserved verbatim in layer 2. Taylor should confirm this is the right call — alternative would be to footnote it.

2. **§6.p11–p13 Georgia-nuclear-strike exchange compression.** Compressed substantially; lost some of the rambling texture of the live exchange. The student's "if we launch… Georgia and Bangor are going to get bombed" passage was kept because it's substantive; the surrounding banter was cut.

3. **§4.p13–p15 BMW/Toyota import exchange.** The student turns are short and the speakers are not clearly distinguished in the captions. Multiple student voices may be present. Flagged for confirmation.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3 (constitutional constraint)

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
