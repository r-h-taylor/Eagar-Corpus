# Case Index — Lecture 9 (3.371 Welding Metallurgy, Spring 2014)

*Citation format: layer 3 paragraph anchor (e.g. `§3.p2`). Click-through reveals the corresponding layer 2 passage with timestamp range.*

The lecture's spine is hydrogen control in welding. Cases are deployed as teaching units to illustrate (a) extreme industrial discipline around hydrogen (Bell mast), (b) what goes wrong when nominal specs are gamed (peashooter), (c) why thick sections are hard (forging press), and (d) why we lost a national capability (battleship turret).

## Cases in §3 — Helicopter engine peashooter

### Helicopter engine peashooter nitriding failure and lawsuit
- **Anchor:** `§3.p1`–`§3.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Helicopter engine peashooter nitriding failure and lawsuit"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Engine manufacturer's nitrided spline-shaft drive coupling cracking in service, causing helicopter crashes. The spec required hydrogen bake-out within one to four hours of nitriding; the process sheet started the clock when the metallurgical lab returned the coating-thickness reading two to three days later. Tom's case study in professional engineering duty: he reported the defective process through the attorney chain when he discovered it during litigation.
- **Materials/systems:** Nitrided spline shaft drive coupling ("peashooter"); high-strength steel; hydrogen embrittlement.
- **Era:** Undated consulting case; company unnamed.

## Cases in §4 — Forging press repair

### 7500-ton forging press failure
- **Anchor:** `§4.p2`–`§4.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "7500-ton forging press failure"
- **Frame in this lecture:** 1949-vintage 7,500-ton forging press used for forging aluminum truck wheels; cracked through a 14-inch section of cast steel cap (~60-ton, ~10 inches thick). Repair welded with 300–400 stick-electrode passes. First repair cracked before reinstallation; second repair cracked on first forging stroke; both failures attributed to hydrogen buried under successive passes without adequate post-heat. Tom uses it to teach why diffusivity (10⁻⁶ cm²/s) makes thick-section hydrogen extraction nearly impossible without staged post-heating.
- **Materials/systems:** Cast steel forging press cap; stick-electrode multi-pass weld; hydrogen diffusion in thick sections.
- **Era:** Press built 1949; failure and repair undated.

## Cases in §5 — Battleship turret

### USS Battleship turret hydrogen cracking repair (Puerto Rico, ~1980)
- **Anchor:** `§5.p1`–`§5.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "USS Battleship turret hydrogen cracking repair (Puerto Rico, ~1980)"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Battleship 16-inch turret explosion (Tom dates ~1980, Puerto Rico). Navy wanted to repair the 14-inch armor plate but had lost the WWII-era welding procedure — last used circa 1947, all the procedure-holders dead. Tom's case study in *technology loss*: a national-defense capability that decayed because nobody had welded that thick in 35 years.
- **Materials/systems:** 14-inch armor plate; stick-electrode multi-pass weld; WWII-era procedure.
- **Era:** Welding procedure last used ~1947; turret incident ~1980. (Editor's note: Tom's framing of the incident's cause aligns with the disputed 1989 USS Iowa turret explosion narrative; preserved per constraint.)
