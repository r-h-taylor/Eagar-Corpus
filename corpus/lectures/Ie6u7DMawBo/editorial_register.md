## Conventions applied (per catalog)

- Student turns labeled `**Student:**`.
- Stage directions in italicized brackets where Tom produces a physical object: tensile specimen (§5.p3), forged insert (§5.p4), Backofen candy dish sample (§6.p9), and the recapped forging sample (§6.p15).
- Numerals: monetary values as `$3.35`, `$10,000`, `$90,000`, `$2,000`. Years as digits. Rhetorical counts spelled out (e.g., "twenty percent," "ninety percent," "eleven steps"). Technical decimals preserved (`0.2`, `0.9`, `K·c₀`).
- Filler ("um," "uh," "you know," "I mean") cut except where it carries shared-context weight.
- "Okay" preserved where it closes a thought; cut where mid-clause.
- Self-corrections smoothed to the corrected form.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
