# Case Index — Lecture 1 (Codes and Standards, Summer 2012)

*Citation format: layer 3 paragraph anchor.*

## Cases referenced

### Shoe factory boiler explosion (Brockton, Massachusetts)
- **Anchor:** `§1.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Shoe factory boiler explosion (Brockton, Massachusetts)"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Origin story for the ASME Boiler and Pressure Vessel Code; Tom uses it to establish that codes are written in the aftermath of disasters.
- **Era:** circa early 1900s (Tom dates the code at "about 105 years old" in 2012)

### Ohio River steamboat boiler explosion
- **Anchor:** `§1.p7`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Ohio River steamboat boiler explosion"
- **Frame in this lecture:** First federal research contract (1830s), Franklin Institute grant; used to extend the "codes follow disasters" arc backward in time.
- **Era:** 1830s

### USS Seawolf submarine hull cracking
- **Anchor:** `§2.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "USS Sea Wolf submarine hull cracking" (variant: "SSN-21 Sea Wolf hull cracking problem")
- **Frame in this lecture:** Tom's personal arc with Millard Firebaugh through the Seawolf program — funding zeroed when Soviets fielded titanium submarine; consulting role after 18% of ship had to be torn apart and rebuilt.
- **Era:** early 1990s

### Soviet titanium submarine (Alpha-class)
- **Anchor:** `§2.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Soviet Alpha-class submarine"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Background to Seawolf budget zeroing — congressional perception that Soviets had leapfrogged US submarine technology.

### LIGO construction and welding
- **Anchors:** `§6.p1`–`§6.p9`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "LIGO construction and welding"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Most precise measurement ever attempted (10^-18 cm target). Tom's consulting role 15 years prior (~1997) on stainless steel welding for the 4-km vacuum tubes; surface treatments to prevent hydrogen outgassing; wave GPS surveying for mirror alignment.
- **Materials/systems:** 304 stainless steel vacuum tubes, tungsten test masses, high-power lasers
- **Era:** mid-1990s design phase; operational 2000s+

### Race-car wave-GPS spin-out monitoring (John Hansman)
- **Anchor:** `§6.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** PROPOSED: "John Hansman wave-GPS race car fishtail monitoring"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Precision GPS application; Hansman (MIT Aero/Astro) using wave GPS to monitor tail-end movement of race cars to control brakes and prevent spin-out.
- **Note:** New cluster proposed; not in aggregate.

### Tar Sands Pipeline Failure (Canada)
- **Anchors:** `§9.p2`–`§9.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Tar Sands Pipeline Failure (Canada)"
- **Frame in this lecture:** PMI failure case — plain carbon steel substituted for 1% chrome-moly during pipe repair; wall thinned from quarter-inch to 0.007 inches over three years from sulfur corrosion; rupture, fire, billion-dollar loss. Welder blamed.
- **Materials/systems:** carbon steel vs. 1% Cr-Mo pipe, sulfur corrosion environment
- **Era:** ~1987 (Tom describes it in 2012 as "25 years ago")

### Steel yard identification via spark testing
- **Anchor:** `§9.p4`–`§9.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Steel yard identification via spark testing (1900s-1960s)"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Historical PMI technique used in steel yards 50–70 years ago; demonstrated live on the grinder with chrome-moly, carbon steel, 440C, and 304 stainless samples.

### US Navy Destroyer brass fitting failure (Second Gulf War)
- **Anchors:** `§10.p1`–`§10.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "US Navy Destroyer brass fitting failure (Second Gulf War)" (variant: "USS cruiser brass airline failure (Second Gulf War)" / "Persian Gulf destroyer 3000 psi air system failure")
- **Frame in this lecture:** Capstone PMI case. Destroyer lost 3,000 psi air system in opening hours of second Gulf War; stress corrosion cracking of wrong brass alloy; Philadelphia Supply Depot found 80% of stock was wrong alloy.
- **Materials/systems:** brass fitting, 3,000 psi pneumatic system, NAVSEA-specified alloy
- **Era:** 2003 (second Gulf War opening days)

### Farberware stainless steel cookware (304 SS magnetism demo)
- **Anchor:** `§8.p8`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Farberware stainless steel cookware pot cracking"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Used as a physical demonstration of deformation-induced ferromagnetism in 304 stainless — not as a failure case in this lecture. The pot itself appears across the corpus as a cracking case; here it's the magnetism teaching prop.

### Stainless steel weld ferrite/austenite gauge
- **Anchor:** `§8.p10`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** PROPOSED: "Stainless steel weld ferrite-content magnetic gauge"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Magnetic lift-off gauge used in welding QC to measure ferrite vs. austenite percentage in stainless steel welds.
- **Note:** Tom mentions in passing as illustration; may recur across lectures.
