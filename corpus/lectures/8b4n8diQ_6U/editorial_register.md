# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 1 (Codes and Standards, Summer 2012). This register records editorial judgments in lifting the cleanup edit to the readable edition.*

## Conventions applied

Per Convention Catalog v1: student attribution, stage directions in italicized brackets, numerals per §3, self-corrections smoothed, filler removed, "okay" and "you know" handled per §6 and §7, soft reordering only within sections.

## Sectioning rationale

Ten sections, marking natural teaching transitions:
- §1 → §2: pivot from boiler & pressure vessel code to motivation for the course (NAVSEA students).
- §2 → §3: pivot from course rationale to the substantive measurement topic ("we're going to talk about codes... but today I want to get into [observables]").
- §3 → §4: pivot from Lord Kelvin / motivation to precision vs. accuracy.
- §4 → §5: pivot from "seven quantities" general framing to detailed discussion of the standards for time, length, mass.
- §5 → §6: pivot from SI standards to the LIGO precision case.
- §6 → §7: pivot from LIGO to triple point of water (temperature standard).
- §7 → §8: pivot from precision metrology to the classroom hands-on materials ID exercise.
- §8 → §9: pivot from exercise to industrial PMI case (Canadian tar sands).
- §9 → §10: pivot from tar sands case to Persian Gulf destroyer case (Tom kept these as separate teaching units; not merged).

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
