### Pittsburgh cold heading plant (machine-screw insert)
- **Anchor:** `§4.p2`, `§4.p3`

### Blackhawk helicopter tail-rotor washer failure (Arkansas crash)
- **Anchor:** `§8.p4`, `§8.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Black Hawk helicopter tail rotor washer failure` (matches existing canon; closely related to `Blackhawk helicopter stress corrosion cracking failure`, `Aluminum washer stress-corrosion cracking failure`, `Navy aluminum washers shelf storage failure`)
- **Frame in this lecture:** $350 aluminum washer holding the tail rotor. Was supposed to have been stress-relieved and was not — residual stresses → stress corrosion cracking. Failure caused Arkansas night-vision training crash, killing 6 Army soldiers and 2 pilots. First cracks found by the Navy on Seahawks (corrosion leader in the service); first fatality on an Army Blackhawk. Same airframe as presidential helicopters.
- **Materials/systems:** aluminum alloy washer, residual stress, stress corrosion cracking
- **Era:** ~mid-1990s based on Tom's "15 to 20 years ago" framing

## Figures referenced (not cases)

- **Gauge block precision (§2.p1, §2.p2):** 50 millionths of an inch; ±3 µm coarsest, ±1 µm, ±0.8 µm laboratory master; calibrated at 68 °F / 20 °C / 45% RH; thermal expansion ~10⁻⁵ per °C for most gauge materials.
- **Aluminum can production rate (§3.p3):** 200 strokes/min × 14 cups/stroke = ~42 cans/second/line ≈ 1¾ cases/second.
- **Brewery filling rate (§3.p4):** 20 bottles/cans per second; can-line transport limit ~200 yards.
- **Molybdenum forging temperature (§7.p11):** ~1800 °C forging temp vs. 2500 °C melting temp; MoO₃ formation onset ~1000 °C in air.
- **Inhomogeneity factor in strip drawing (§7.p5, §7.p6):** 16% reduction lubricated shallow-angle → IF = 0; same reduction steeper angle → IF ≈ 19%; non-lubricated +10–15%; smaller 0.08 reduction → higher IF due to smaller deformation zone width.
