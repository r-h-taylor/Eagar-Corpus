# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Welding Metallurgy lecture 1 (`DokriGA10Oo`), Summer 2015. Conventions per the catalog established by lecture 11 §1.*

## Sectioning rationale

Lecture 1 is a first-day session that mixes administrative material with substantive teaching. Eleven sections:

- §1–§4: course logistics, Navy program context, presentation assignment, adjusting to MIT.
- §5: the Lester Thurow / soundbite-world digression that links to §6 by way of "communicate in vivid metaphor."
- §6–§7: the substantive teaching arc on toughness, ceramics, the Ashby plot, and concrete.
- §8: the Dan Rather building case (a concrete-rebar tack-weld failure).
- §9: the Sea Wolf submarine weld inspection case.
- §10: the 747-400 titanium catalytic converter digression and aircraft range.
- §11: the closing ductility/toughness demos and the lecture's thesis sentence ("the reason we use metals").

Section breaks fall at clear pivots: completion of an administrative topic, introduction of a new case, return from a digression. §8 is a separate section from §7 because the Dan Rather case is its own developed teaching unit, not merely an illustration of concrete behavior — Tom signals the pivot by saying "I had a case I'll tell."

## Decisions NOT permitted at layer 3 (constitutional constraint)

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
