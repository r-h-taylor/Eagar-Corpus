## Flags for editor review

1. **§5.p3 and §5.p7:** Student attribution inferred from context. The raw transcript does not capture student voices; Tom's "Yeah" and "as you said" make clear someone spoke, but the exact words are reconstructed paraphrases (rendered in brackets) or inferred from Tom's repeat-back (rendered as the student line itself). Editor should confirm or mark as `[Student speaks]` placeholder if even the gist is too speculative.
2. **§6.p6 et seq.:** Tom calls Roger Boisjoly "Bosco Lee" throughout. The canonical cluster name in the aggregate is "Bosco Lee Morton Thiokol O-ring engineering decision." The bracketed correction in L3 reads `[Boisjoly]`. Editor should decide whether to (a) keep the cluster name as Tom's slip and bracket the truth, or (b) rename the cluster to "Boisjoly Morton Thiokol O-ring engineering decision" and bracket Tom's slip as "Boisjoly [as Tom says: Bosco Lee]." The current case_index.md keeps the canonical name.
3. **§6.p5:** "Christa McAuliffe" — Tom likely pronounced her name correctly; the captioner phoneticized as "Krista McAuliffe." The bracket here functions less like a correction of Tom and more like a captioner-correction. Per convention this should probably be a silent fix, not a bracket. Flagged for editor preference.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
