## §3 — Blackhawk washer

### Black Hawk helicopter tail rotor washer failure
- **Anchor:** `§3.p1`–`§3.p9`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Black Hawk helicopter tail rotor washer failure"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Stress-corrosion cracking of 6061-T6 aluminum washer holding the tail rotor of an Army Sikorsky Blackhawk; helicopter lost rotor and crashed in Arkansas. Tom traces the design history: the Coast Guard / Jayhawk team had already switched to T73 and not told the Blackhawk team. Same washers found pre-cracked in Navy supply depots from residual stresses alone (measured 8 ksi vs. 5 ksi SCC threshold).
- **Materials/systems:** 6061-T6 vs. 6061-T73 aluminum; tensile (-51) stress relief practice.
- **Era:** Arkansas crash and forensic; replacement program 15-month lead time.
- **Related:** see "Aluminum washer stress-corrosion cracking failure," "Davenport Iowa plate stress relief by hydraulic jacking" (referenced in §3.p3), "Presidential helicopter welding failure" (referenced in §3.p6 in passing).

### Davenport Iowa plate stress relief by hydraulic jacking
- **Anchor:** `§3.p3`–`§3.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Davenport Iowa plate stress relief by hydraulic jacking"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Referenced as the production-scale analog to the washer's -51 treatment. World's largest rolling mill — for aluminum, not steel — produces aircraft wing plates for Boeing and the former McDonnell Douglas. Hydraulic expanders pull 30-foot, 4-inch plates 1–3% in plastic strain to relieve quench stresses.
- **Materials/systems:** Heat-treatable aluminum plate (2014, 2024); Alcoa Davenport works.

## §4 — Alclad aluminum

### Aluminum aerospace clad-sheet manufacturing (Alclad and continuously cast variants)
- **Anchor:** `§4.p1`–`§4.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** PROPOSED: "Alclad aluminum aerospace sheet — roll-bond and continuous-cast clad processes"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Seventy-year history of roll-bonding 1100 aluminum onto 2014/2024 cores to prevent galvanic micro-cells from copper precipitates. Recent (~mid-2000s) commercial development by a Spokane WA inventor of continuous direct-chill casting that fuses two alloys at a single fusion line, dropping clad-sheet costs and opening kitchen-appliance and brazed-radiator markets.
- **Materials/systems:** 1100 / 2014 / 2024 aluminum; direct chill (DC) casting; brazing alloys for radiators.
- **Note:** May correspond to "Aluminum radiator field brazing development" partly, but the §4 case is properly about clad-sheet manufacturing rather than field brazing.

## §5 — America's Cup hydraulic cylinder

### America's Cup hydraulic cylinder stress corrosion cracking
- **Anchor:** `§5.p1`–`§5.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "America's Cup hydraulic cylinder stress corrosion cracking"
- **Frame in this lecture:** 7075-T6 aluminum hydraulic cylinder at 7,500 psi exploding from SCC initiated outside. Solution: 3 mm → 4 mm wall thickness, sacrificing strength to double SCC threshold and convert failure mode to leak-before-break. Used to introduce the America's Cup design philosophy: if your hardware doesn't fail, you've made it too heavy.
- **Materials/systems:** 7075-T6 aluminum-zinc alloy; Navtec hydraulic equipment.
- **Related canonical clusters referenced in passing:** "Blackhawk helicopter stress corrosion cracking failure" (3,000 psi reference, §5.p3), "V-22 Osprey aircraft" (3,000 psi reference, §5.p3), "Navtech marine hydraulic piston failure (America's Cup yacht)", "America's Cup design philosophy and lightweight failure margins."

## §7 — Beryllium

### MIT Berylliosis cases
- **Anchor:** `§7.p6`–`§7.p8`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "MIT Berylliosis cases" / "Beryllium toxicity discovery at MIT" / "Powdered beryllium discovery in MIT laboratory"
- **Frame in this lecture:** MIT Manhattan Project beryllium machining work (corner of Mass Ave) discovered berylliosis through machinist deaths in mid-to-late 1940s. ## §9 — Magnesium

### Magnesium supply, applications, and Dow Chemical cartel
- **Anchor:** `§9.p1`–`§9.p4`, `§9.p9`, `§9.p13`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Dow Chemical magnesium price cartel (1946–1975)" (for §9.p13); "Magnesium anode nickel contamination failure" / general magnesium electrochemistry (for §9.p4)
- **Frame in this lecture:** Quantity overview (250,000 tons globally vs. 40 million for aluminum, 1 billion for steel). Half goes to Al alloying; rest to steel desulfurization, nodular iron, tantalum/Nb reduction, and sacrificial anodes in hot-water tanks. Tom closes with Joel Clark's first PhD student's thesis tracking magnesium and aluminum prices in lockstep 1946–1975, attributing it to a Dow Chemical illegal cartel.
- **Related canonical clusters:** "Post-WWII aluminum and magnesium industry cartel," "Alcoa monopoly and price fixing with Dow Chemical," "Don Swaay lawsuit against US magnesium producer."

### Navy super-corroding magnesium composites (Port Hueneme)
- **Anchor:** `§9.p7`–`§9.p8`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Naval diver thermal protection using magnesium alloy" / "Navy diver salvage gas generation system (magnesium composite)" / "Fort Fundy magnesium-graphite composite diving application"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Navy Port Hueneme diving school developed magnesium-graphite/SiC composites that corroded faster than they could be metallographically polished (Latanision's work). Navy weaponized the corrosion: (1) divers crack-open packets to generate H₂ for salvage lift balloons; (2) divers pierce packets in cold water for exothermic warmth.
- **Materials/systems:** Magnesium with graphite or silicon carbide reinforcement; "super-corroding alloys" Navy term.

### Magnesium weld stress-corrosion cracking
- **Anchor:** `§9.p11`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Magnesium weld stress-corrosion cracking from humidity"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Fusion-welded magnesium without stress relief will SCC from atmospheric humidity within two days. Reason most magnesium parts are castings (no residual stress) — auto wheels, dashboard structures.

### Magnesium torched-car insurance fraud
- **Anchor:** `§9.p10`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Magnesium car fire from insurance fraud"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Tom's vivid example of magnesium ignition temperature — NYC arson of junker cars with mag wheels. Magnesium burns at ~4000 °F once gasoline-ignited; used as a flare and fireworks material in powder form.

### Blackhawk helicopter high-performance magnesium

## Figures referenced (not cases)

- **Aluminum production scale:** ~40 million tons globally; magnesium ~250,000 tons; steel ~1 billion tons (§9.p9)
- **Aircraft fuselage pressure differential:** 0.8 atm interior vs. 0.4 atm exterior at altitude (§2.p3)
- **6061 stress corrosion cracking threshold:** 5 ksi; measured washer residual stress 8 ksi (§3.p8)
- **T6 → T73 trade:** ~10% strength loss, ~2× toughness, ~2× SCC stress threshold (~10× life in corrosion fatigue) (§3.p5)
- **America's Cup hydraulic pressure:** 7,500 psi vs. Blackhawk/Osprey 3,000 psi vs. typical 1,500–2,000 psi (§5.p3)
- **Critical flaw size at 300 ksi steel:** ~0.1 inch; at K²/σ = 2.5 in 2.5-inch plate, leak-before-break (§6.p2)
- **Beryllium price:** ~$400,000/ton; melting point 1560 °C (§7.p4)
- **Berylliosis genetic predisposition:** ~10% of population (§7.p5)
