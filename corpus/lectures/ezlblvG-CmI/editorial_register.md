# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Solid State Welding, Spring 2013, lecture 4/12. This register records editorial judgments in lifting the cleanup edit to the readable edition.*

## Sectioning rationale

The lecture has nine sections. Tom opens with administrative catch-up plus two follow-up cases from prior sessions (Kielland, Comet) — these form §1. He then pivots to a different teaching unit (Boston gas pipes / §2) followed by another aside on stiffness and surgical tools (§3). The heart of the lecture is friction welding (§4) and a meta-reflection on what a weld is (§5). The closing arc covers three forms of cold welding for industrial use: cold-weld wire drawing (§6), thermocompression wire bonding (§7), with an economics-of-scrap excursion that includes the Pentium redundancy story (§8), and finally explosive bonding (§9). Section breaks fall at clean teaching transitions Tom himself marks ("So catching up on other things"; "I wanted to mention three other types of friction welding"; "The last type of cold welding I want to talk about").

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases that Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
