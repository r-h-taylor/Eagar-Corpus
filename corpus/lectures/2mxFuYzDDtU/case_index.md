### Titanium electroslag weld (Oregon Graduate Center)
- **Anchor:** `§4.p1`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Titanium electroslag weld at Oregon Graduate Center`

- **Materials/systems:** Ti-6Al-4V, electroslag welding

### U.S. Navy heavy-section titanium submarine program (1970s)
- **Anchor:** `§4.p2`, `§5.p2`, `§6.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `U.S. Navy heavy-section titanium submarine program`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Tom's first Navy research contract as a young assistant professor was submerged arc welding of 6Al-4V for heavy-section submarine hull development. Funded by Office of Naval Research. Contact at Navy was a graduate of the department. Program also covered creep-fatigue interaction studies at NRL.
- **Materials/systems:** Ti-6Al-4V, submerged arc welding, heavy section
- **Era:** 1970s–1982

### Soviet Alpha-class submarine
- **Anchor:** `§6.p1`, `§6.p2`, `§6.p3`, `§6.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Soviet Alpha-class submarine`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Centerpiece case of the lecture. Soviets deployed all-titanium attack submarines around 1980 — deeper diving than US depth charges, faster underwater than US destroyers on the surface. Mothballed within two years due to (1) fatigue cracks producing acoustic signature audible to sonar dropped from helicopters, and (2) unsolved creep-fatigue interaction at near-room-temperature seawater. Tom learned of the deployment from an *International Herald Tribune* article on a flight from Europe; learned in 1982 that the Soviets had not solved the creep-fatigue problem — they had built the submarines anyway.
- **Materials/systems:** titanium hull, creep-fatigue interaction at room temperature
- **Era:** ~1980–1982

### U.S. Navy titanium creep-fatigue research (1982 Soviet discovery)
- **Anchor:** `§6.p3`, `§6.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `U.S. Navy titanium creep-fatigue research (1982 Soviet discovery)`
- **Frame in this lecture:** US Naval Research Lab had characterized titanium creep-fatigue at room temperature: under high compressive stress, fatigue cracks initiate readily. In 1982 the US learned the Soviets had not solved the problem either — they had built the Alpha subs without a solution.
- **Materials/systems:** titanium, creep-fatigue
- **Era:** late 1970s–1982
