# Case Index — Lecture 2018-02-20

*Total Quality Improvement, Spring 2018. Citation format: layer 3 paragraph anchor (e.g. `§6.p2`).*

This lecture is primarily a TQM / management-pedagogy session. Most "cases" in the strict materials-failure sense appear briefly as illustrations; the developed teaching units are the three people-problems stories (Honeywell plastic film, Alcoa Tennessee, GM Linden).

## Developed teaching cases

### Honeywell plastic film plant — the mad shitter
- **Anchor:** `§6.p1`–`§6.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `PROPOSED: Honeywell New Jersey facility morale and management crisis`
- **Frame in this lecture:** LFM/LGO student internship in a Honeywell-acquired mom-and-pop plastic film operation. Used to teach the principle that worker dysfunction (extreme: smearing excrement on restroom walls) is a symptom of unheard frustration, addressable through low-cost engagement (donut Fridays, "what can I do to help you?").
- **Era:** Early 1990s.
- **Materials/systems:** Plastic film manufacturing, $3–4M extruder.
- **Note:** Cluster `Honeywell New Jersey facility morale and management crisis` exists in canon and matches.

### Alcoa Tennessee — the toilet paper strike
- **Anchor:** `§7.p1`–`§7.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Alcoa Tennessee 1980s strike and worker conditions`
- **Frame in this lecture:** A management meeting during a late-1980s strike, where a worker explains that the strike's underlying cause was 16-hour shifts with denied bathroom breaks, splintered wooden toilet seats, and no toilet paper. Paired with the Honeywell story as parallel evidence that worker dignity (not wages) drives labor unrest.
- **Era:** Late 1980s strike; 1910 Pinkerton history as backdrop.
- **Companion canonical cluster:** `Alcoa Tennessee labor violence (1910)` — Tom invokes the historical Pinkerton killings (~1910) as the source of the persistent "murdering Alcoans" label.

### GM Linden Chevy Blazer — Bill Fonte
- **Anchor:** `§8.p1`–`§8.p8`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `General Motors Linden New Jersey locating pin failure`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Former LFM student Bill Fonte called Tom in to diagnose welding defects at GM's Linden, NJ plant. On-site walkthrough revealed (a) oil contamination preventing electrical ground, (b) a worn hardened-steel locating pin causing fixture slippage, (c) unnecessary galvanizing on an interior hinge causing frosty welds. Each defect was a "hundred dollar bill on the floor" visible to anyone who would walk the line.
- **Companion canonical clusters referenced in this anchor:**
  - `General Motors Chevy Blazer welding problem (Linden, New Jersey)` — same case, alternate cluster name.
  - `Galvanized steel spot welding in automotive assembly` — the zinc-vaporization frosty-weld mechanism.
- **Era:** Mid-1990s (Bill Fonte's first GM assignment after LFM, ~1996).
- **Materials/systems:** Spot welding of stamped steel hinges; galvanized steel; hardened steel locating pins.
