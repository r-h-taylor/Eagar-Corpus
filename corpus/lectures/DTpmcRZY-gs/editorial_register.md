### Sectioning decision: §5 / §6 interleaving

Tom's actual delivery order in layer 2 is: anneal/normalize (p25–p26) → 4130 intro & bicycle frames (p27–p30) → transformation curve (p31–p33) → bicycle handlebar story (p34–p42) → kit aircraft (p43+).

The transformation-curve discussion (p31–p33) is thematically continuous with anneal/normalize (p25–p26) — both belong to the same teaching unit about how cooling rate transforms steel. Tom introduces 4130 (p27–p30) as a setup for the story that comes later in p34. He then returns to the transformation curve to finish that thread before launching the story.

**Decision:** Section by *teaching unit*, not by delivery order. §5 collects all transformation-curve teaching (p25, p26, p31, p32, p33). §6 collects all 4130 teaching plus the bicycle story (p27, p28, p29, p30, p34–p42).

This is a soft reordering that crosses what might otherwise be considered a section boundary, but it preserves Tom's teaching units intact. Logged here for editor review. If Taylor prefers strict delivery order, §5 and §6 could be merged into one section or split differently.

## Conventions applied (per catalog)

1. Student questions get `**Student:**` attribution where the question survives in the transcript; rendered as `[Question about X.]` where the student voice is inaudible.
2. Physical-object teaching moves get italicized stage directions in brackets (§3.p5).
3. Monetary values: `$10 million`, `$15 billion`, `$5 million`; rhetorical compounds spelled out.
4. Self-corrections smoothed in layer 3, preserved in layer 2.
5. Backward-references to prior lecture sessions ("We talked before about cast iron") preserved at §1.p2 because the reference carries content; cut elsewhere where it's pure orientation.
6. "Okay" preserved where it closes a teaching unit (§7.p6 close); cut as mid-clause filler.
7. "You know" and "I mean" cut as filler throughout.
8. Soft reordering documented above (§5/§6 interleaving).

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could do this because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases that Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
