# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 3 (`42alE7nc_-Y`), all sections. This register records the editorial judgments made in lifting the layer 2 cleanup to the readable edition.*

## Conventions applied

Standard catalog conventions throughout: student turns labeled `**Student:**`; numerals per catalog §3; self-corrections smoothed; "okay" and "you know" handled per §6–7; backward-references to prior sessions cut except where they carry content.

## Sectioning rationale

Eleven sections were chosen. The lecture's structure is unusually rich for 50 minutes — Tom moves through China anecdote, definitions of engineering, canals/aqueducts visual aids, quotes from books, Edison history, Q&A interlude, National Academies, statistics on engineering disciplines, the BU minority-fellowship story, the women-faculty arc, and a closing reflection on respect. Each was a natural teaching unit. The arc from §9 through §11 is one extended autobiographical case about Tom's department headship; the breaks at §10 (becoming department head) and §11 (the listening lesson) follow Tom's own pivots in framing.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could do this because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases that Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
