# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture o2XfliUT_jA (Codes and Standards, Summer 2012, session 3/7). This register records editorial judgments made in lifting the cleanup edit to the readable edition. Conventions follow the corpus catalog established by lecture 11 §1.*

## Sectioning

Twelve sections. The lecture moves through three distinct teaching arcs:

1. **§1–§6** — opening on metrology (gauge blocks), then a digression through Ashby plots and material property limits, illustrated with the National Aerospace Plane and Air Force Mach 5 cases, then a return to observables (energy types, electromagnetic spectrum, Debye frequency, HIP).
2. **§7–§11** — the standards landscape: which agencies write which codes, lightning protection as a worked historical example, enforcement, and pricing.
3. **§12** — the aluminum cement truck tank case as a long unified worked example that ties the whole lecture together.

§12 is intentionally long (~22 minutes, 24 paragraphs). Tom told it as a single sustained case and the editorial rule prohibits splitting cases he handled as one. The internal structure (design failure → first injury → repeated failures → fatal failure → forensic investigation → preemption analysis → inspector ethics → tail) is sequential and reads cleanly as one section.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
