## Cases referenced in this lecture

### Concorde supersonic transport operations and economics
- **Anchor:** `§3.p1`–`§3.p7`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Concorde supersonic transport operations and economics"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Tom's personal flight on the Concorde, used to illustrate (a) the material limits of supersonic commercial aviation, and (b) the maintenance compromises forced by a thin fleet. Sets up the failed National Aerospace Plane.
- **Era:** 1990s flight; Concorde retired 2003.

### National Aerospace Plane
- **Anchor:** `§3.p1`, `§3.p5`–`§3.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "National Aerospace Plane"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Reagan-era hypersonic vehicle proposal — liquid hydrogen tanks in the wings, copper alloy skin, 2,000 Kelvin skin temperature against 20 K hydrogen. Used to argue that materials selection has hard limits not negotiable by procurement ambition.
- **Era:** mid-to-late 1980s.

### Air Force Mach 17 air-breathing aircraft program
- **Anchor:** `§4.p1`–`§4.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Air Force Mach 17 air-breathing aircraft program"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Air Force propulsion-research committee Tom served on. Two grandiose goals — 25,000-mile range and Mach 5 / 17,000 mph — justified by a single anecdote about an Osama bin Laden time-to-target requirement. Tom argues this is a cruise missile masquerading as an aircraft, and the material limits make it infeasible.
- **Era:** mid-2000s (Tom dates it "five or six years ago" from 2012).

### Hot isostatic pressing development at Battelle Memorial Institute
- **Anchor:** `§6.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Hot isostatic pressing development at Battelle Memorial Institute"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Used to demonstrate the practical importance of knowing gas-vs-condensed-phase density ratios. The world's first HIP press at Battelle, the second at MIT (4-131). 2,000°C, 2,000 atmospheres argon, squeezing pores out of powder-metallurgy turbine discs.
- **Era:** post-WWII into present.

### Harley-Davidson aluminum cylinder head casting defects
- **Anchor:** `§6.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Harley-Davidson aluminum cylinder head casting defects"
- **Frame in this lecture:** HIP application example — porosity in cast aluminum cylinder heads is dangerous because the cylinder sits between the rider's legs. Used to underline why criticality determines whether HIP is worth the cost.

### Navy Tributyltin (TBT) paint environmental damage
- **Anchor:** `§7.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Navy Tributyltin (TBT) paint environmental damage"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Used to illustrate the limit of DoD national-security override of civilian regulation. Navy used TBT in hull paint for barnacle prevention; parts per trillion wiped out mollusk populations in every US harbor; Congress overrode the Navy in the 1990s.
- **Era:** mid-1980s through 1990s.

### Initial cement truck tank explosion and worker leg amputation
- **Anchor:** `§12.p10`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Initial cement truck tank explosion and worker leg amputation"
- **Frame in this lecture:** First in-plant failure of the cement truck tank line. Worker lost a leg during pressurization testing with 100 PSI air instead of water. Company paid insurance and continued production without investigation. The setup case for the fatal Pennsylvania failure.

### Pennsylvania cement truck fatality and regulatory failure
- **Anchor:** `§12.p1`–`§12.p22`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Pennsylvania cement truck fatality and regulatory failure"
- **Frame in this lecture:** The lecture's main case. 200-gallon aluminum water tank on cement truck, 55 PSI operating, no doubler plate on nozzles. Welder in a Pennsylvania repair shop pressurizes a previously-repaired tank with 100 PSI shop air instead of regulated 5 PSI, tank explodes, welder killed (found in four pieces 30 yards away). Forensic investigation reveals 100,000 deployed tanks with the same defect. Manufacturer had sought a DOT ruling and stopped; ASME code and Pennsylvania state law required compliance regardless. Code-of-ethics duty to public surfaces when retired ASME committee member Roger reports to the National Board.
- **Materials/systems:** aluminum pressure vessel, fillet-welded nozzle, ASME Boiler and Pressure Vessel Code Section 8, doubler plate reinforcement, stress concentration factor of 3 at a circular hole.
- **Era:** Tom dates the case loosely; the manufacturer started "as a little company in Kansas" before acquisition.

## Figures referenced in §11 (pricing of codes)

These are *figures*, not cases — they're Tom's recurring numeric anchors on the economics of standards bodies.

- MIL-Handbook-5 / MMPDS-05: free or ~$100 (GPO cost).
- NFPA 921: ~$110.
- AWS Structural Welding Code: $400, was $60 fifteen years prior.
- SAE AMS specifications: $59 per 20-page document.
- ASME Boiler and Pressure Vessel Code: $12,000/set, every 3 years.
- AWS total assets: $5M (1990s) → $60M (2012), plus $10–15M in long-term investment.
- The 600-dollar gas-piping standard (half an inch thick) — Tom is alluding to one of the flexible-gas-piping cases in the canon; not developed here.
