# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Codes and Standards, Summer 2012, Lecture 2. This register records the editorial judgments made in lifting the cleanup edit to the readable edition.*

## Sectioning

Eight sections, breaking at the natural teaching pivots:
- §1 — autobiographical opener about how to listen to MIT lectures
- §2 — recap of yesterday's sublime/ridiculous theme
- §3 — practical limits on mass and length
- §4 — Stratford MH-53 bearing case (the main extended teaching unit)
- §5 — time, atomic clocks, and the upshot: comparing precision across manufacturing
- §6 — NIST visit, kilogram redefinition, shipbuilding answer
- §7 — Q&A on design, FBI/QC, simulation history, *2001* trivia
- §8 — materials properties using carbon (diamond vs graphite) as case study

§4 and §7 both treat the Stratford bearing case. Tom returns to it in §7 in response to a student question. Per convention 16 (no merging cases Tom handled separately), the §7 material is kept where Tom delivered it — as a Q&A return to the case after the interlude on time/NIST/shipbuilding — not folded back into §4.

### §5 — time
- §5.p3: smoothed "microwave-assisted stimulated emission of radiation" → "microwave-amplified stimulated emission of radiation." Tom slips on the acronym expansion (MASER = Microwave Amplification by Stimulated Emission of Radiation). **This is a Tom-slip correction**, but it's a definitional acronym, not a name or a date. Corrected silently per convention 10 because the acronym expansion is unambiguous. Flagged here.
- §5.p4: dropped "and I was wrong, they did some of the work up here at, uh, Harvard Smithsonian, but the first maser" — Tom retracts a previous statement we didn't hear him make. Kept the corrected fact (UK measurement lab).
- §5.p7: smoothed "10 to the minus 2 meters, or one centimeter, which is one in 10 to the sixth" — Tom is comparing 10nm to 1cm. Per convention this is a minor numerical clarification: 10⁻⁸ m / 10⁻² m = 10⁻⁶, one part in 10⁶. Layer 3 reads "That's 10 to the minus 8 against 10 to the minus 2, one part in 10 to the sixth" — clarifies which two values are being compared. **Mild factual cleanup** — Tom's stated ratio is correct, the layer 3 just makes the arithmetic transparent.

### §8 — carbon as case study

Tom self-corrects "maker of work" to "maker of war machines." Smoothed per convention 4 to the corrected form. (Note: the actual French etymology — *ingénieur* relates to *engin* = "engine, contrivance" — is more nuanced, but Tom's "maker of war machines" reflects the historical association and is left as Tom said it.)

- §8.p11: cut Tom's "Um, turns out, what's going on here — oh, not that in the way" — orientation toward a slide or board we don't see.

## Decisions that are NOT permitted at layer 3 (constitutional)

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
