- **Anchors:** `§3.p11` (introduces precision-bearing manufacturing context); `§4.p1`–`§4.p26` (full case narrative); `§7.p3`–`§7.p8` (Q&A return to the case)

- **Materials/systems:** Ready Slim 42-inch ball bearing (half-inch cross-section, plus or minus 0.002 inch tolerance); aluminum swash plate housing; MH-53 helicopter.
- **Era:** Tom's involvement appears to be roughly 1980s–1990s based on context (precision-manufacturing era, FBI involvement, multiple fleet incidents including Japanese Navy MH-53 loss).

## Figures referenced (numeric anchors, not cases)

- **Mass measurement floor:** one part in 10⁵ achievable on a marble-isolated balance; limited by fingerprints, wind, vibration, temperature.
- **Length measurement floor:** one part in 10⁵ on a ball-bearing measuring machine in an air-conditioned room; coefficient of thermal expansion of steel ≈ 10⁻⁵ per °C means a 10° room-temperature swing changes a one-inch dimension by 100 micro-inches.
- **Atomic clock precision:** 10⁻¹⁴ averaging over a day (microwave masers); 10⁻¹⁶ (Bose-Einstein condensate optical clocks) = one second in 138 million years.
- **Aircraft carrier precision:** ~300 m hull, plates assembled to ~1 mm → 3 parts in 10⁶, comparable to a semiconductor chip on relative precision.
- **Diamond vs graphite conductivity:** 12–15 orders of magnitude difference.
- **Carbon nanotube predicted strength:** 2 million PSI — same as iron whiskers measured in the 1950s.
