## Conventions applied (from catalog)

1. Student questions get `**Student:**` attribution.
2. Physical-object teaching moves get italicized stage directions in brackets — applied to the projector incident in §4.
3. Monetary values: `$2 billion`, `$1,000`, `$500`.
4. Self-corrections smoothed in layer 3; preserved in layer 2.
5. Backward-references to prior sessions ("Mike had talked about," "Dr. Belmar's talked about") preserved where they carry content; cut where pure orientation.
6. "Okay" preserved where it closes a thought; cut where mid-clause filler.
7. "You know" / "I mean" cut except where invoking shared context.
8. Soft reordering noted; this lecture has minimal soft reordering — most edits are smoothing of false-starts within a sentence rather than reordering across paragraphs.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
