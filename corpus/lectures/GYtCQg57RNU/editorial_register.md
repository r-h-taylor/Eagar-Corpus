### §8 — Fracture mechanics

- **§8.p2-p4.** Tom does an in-his-head fracture mechanics calculation. The algebra in his delivery ("X^2 is equal to square root of pi C, 4 is equal to pi C, 4 over pi is equal to C") is rough — the equation should be K = σ√(πa), so a_critical = (1/π)(K/σ)². With K = 50 ksi√in and σ = 50 ksi, a_critical = 1/π inches ≈ 0.32 in, not "over an inch" as Tom claims. **Preserved as Tom delivered it**, including the "over an inch" result. This is a genuine on-tape error in Tom's arithmetic. Per Convention §16, factual errors are not corrected unless they actively mislead; here, the qualitative conclusion (sub-inch flaws won't propagate in two-year service intervals) is correct, so the slip is preserved.
- **§8.p3.** Student question on "induced stress vs. pressure stress" was awkwardly phrased and Tom's reply slightly confused — the "academic / design" exchange preserved because it shows Tom redirecting to the design-stress concept.
