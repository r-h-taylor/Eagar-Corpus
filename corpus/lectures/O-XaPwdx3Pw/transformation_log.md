# Transformation Log: Layer 1 → Layer 2

*Recitation 2020-02-10 (`O-XaPwdx3Pw`). Mechanical rules applied to the raw transcript to produce the cleaned middle layer. This log records every change so layer 2 is reproducible from layer 1.*

## Global rules applied to all paragraphs

1. **Line-broken delivery joined.** Auto-caption line breaks (typically 3–6 words per line) are merged into continuous sentences. The raw transcript breaks every ~3 seconds of speech onto a new line; layer 2 honors only true sentence boundaries.

2. **Sentence boundaries inserted.** Periods, commas, and question marks added where spoken pauses, falling intonation, and grammatical completion indicate sentence breaks. Capitalization at sentence starts.

3. **Captioner errors corrected silently.** Misrecognitions of common words and proper nouns are corrected in layer 2; logged individually below.

4. **Tom's spoken slips of memory bracketed.** Where Tom misnames a person or thing, the correction appears in brackets on first use per section: `Ford [Forward]`, then `[Forward]` alone thereafter. (One borderline case noted below; Tom uses "Merc Flemings" as a familiar shorthand for Merton Flemings, his actual mentor — this is not a slip but a nickname.)

5. **Paragraph breaks at natural pauses.** Inserted where Tom shifts topic, takes a beat, or arrives at a closing "okay" that ends a teaching unit. Student turns ("**Student:**") are inserted as separate paragraphs at points where the raw transcript shows a question being asked but not transcribed, signaled by Tom's response pattern.

6. **Preserved as-is in layer 2:** filler words ("uh," "um," "I mean," "you know," "okay"), false starts, mid-sentence pivots, self-corrections, repetitions. These are Tom's speech artifacts; removing them is editorial and belongs in layer 3.
