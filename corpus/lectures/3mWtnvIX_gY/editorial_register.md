## Constitutional constraint

Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3:

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot). The same constraint applies to guest lecturers: Alex Slocum is alive (as of corpus assembly), but the corpus discipline is that ratification is not part of the pipeline. If a Slocum line is unrecoverable, it is cut or marked, not reconstructed.
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases the speaker kept separate, or splitting cases they handled as one.
- Removing factual errors the speaker made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; speaker's slips are bracketed; genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
