### Deep-drilling drill bits without elastomer seals
- **Anchor:** `§5.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** PROPOSED: `Deep-water drill bit elastomer-free seal design (Slocum)`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Slocum invented drill bits for hot deep drilling that eliminate rubber seals. His colleagues were hiring elastomer experts to make rubber that lasts at high temperature; Slocum's solution is to remove the rubber entirely. The bits enable horizontal drilling (and therefore fracking) by allowing hydraulic-signal directional control at high temperature.
- **Materials/systems:** Deep-drilling drill bits; horizontal directional drilling; hydraulic flapper-valve directional control.
- **Era:** Modern (developed during Slocum's tenure; ongoing commercial product).
- **Note:** Possibly overlaps with corpus cluster `Deep-water drill bit high-temperature seal failure`, but Slocum is framing this as a *successful innovation*, not a failure. Distinct framing — may warrant its own PROPOSED cluster.

### APHRO — Advanced Pumped Hydro Reverse Osmosis system
- **Anchor:** `§5.p1` through `§5.p14`; resumed at `§6.p1` through `§6.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** PROPOSED: `Slocum APHRO Advanced Pumped Hydro Reverse Osmosis system`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Slocum's signature post-2001 project. The physics coincidence: optimal hydroelectric head height (500 m, 50 atm) equals optimal reverse-osmosis membrane pressure. Therefore coastal-mountain sites can deliver electricity and desalinated fresh water from the same infrastructure. Sites identified worldwide; main work in Mexico, Iran (highest A-index), UAE (Masdar).
- **Materials/systems:** Pumped hydroelectric storage + reverse-osmosis desalination + solar/wind input.
- **Era:** Post-9/11 (Slocum dates his life pivot to 9/11); ongoing.

### Deep-rock nuclear waste disposal proposal
- **Anchor:** `§6.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** PROPOSED: `Deep-rock nuclear waste disposal (1960s proposal, modern revival)`
- **Frame in this lecture:** A 1960s proposal — drill 10,000 ft (2 km) into hard rock at the reactor site itself, store all spent fuel from that plant in a one-meter-diameter borehole at that depth. Slocum argues this was infeasible in the 1960s but is now feasible thanks to oil-industry deep-drilling technology (including his own bits). Pairs with supercritical CO2 reactor cycles that eliminate cooling water.
- **Materials/systems:** Hard-rock geologic strata; oil-industry drilling technology.
- **Era:** 1960s proposal; modern feasibility.
- **Note:** Aggregate has `Nuclear waste repository site selection (1960s proposal)` — likely a match.

### Tapered spiral-welded wind turbine pole
- **Anchor:** `§6.p5` through `§6.p8`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** PROPOSED: `Slocum tapered spiral-weld wind turbine pole (Colorado startup)`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Slocum's Colorado startup. Standard wind turbine poles are constrained to 4.25 m max diameter (bridge clearance for shipping), forcing 3-inch wall thickness, multi-pass welding, premium cost. His insight: bring the *factory* to the site, fabricate tapered spiral-welded poles 7 m at base to 3 m at top, use one-inch steel, one-third the steel mass, lower wind energy cost by ~10% (the projected tipping point).
- **Materials/systems:** Spiral-welded carbon steel pipe; tapered cross-section; on-site fabrication.
- **Era:** ~2014–2015 (active development at time of lecture).
- **Cross-reference:** Aggregate has `Wind turbine pole cost overrun` and `Transmission tower million-ton steel procurement` — possibly related; this is Slocum's specific innovation.

### Lead-screw / belt-driven linear actuator design (St. Venant's principle teaching case)
- **Anchor:** `§2.p4` through `§2.p11`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** PROPOSED: `Slocum lead-screw shaft-failure teaching case (St. Venant)`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Slocum's go-to undergraduate teaching artifact: a motor-pulley-belt-leadscrew assembly where the catalog-engineered version places the belt load at 3–5 characteristic dimensions from the bearings, causing predictable shaft and bearing failure. Reciprocity (flipping the hub) puts the load near the bearings and fixes it.
- **Materials/systems:** Motor, belt, pulley, lead screw, bearings, shaft with keyway/setscrew stress concentration.
- **Era:** N/A — pedagogical artifact, not a dated case.
