# Case Index — Lecture 6B297feoqAw (3.371 Fall 2013, session 6/14)

This lecture is built around a walk-through of an issue of *Advanced Materials and Processes*, with Tom critiquing each article against the material-selection economics he has been developing. Several recurring corpus cases surface here, mostly in their hype-cycle framing.

## Cases referenced

### Sapphire substrate manufacturing for LED market dominance
- **Anchor:** `§1.p1`–`§1.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Sapphire substrate manufacturing for LED market dominance"
- **Frame in this lecture:** New Hampshire company growing million-dollar boules of aluminum oxide for LED substrates, sold to Chinese buyers. Used to set up the externalities-and-technology-transfer argument.
- **Materials/systems:** Single-crystal Al₂O₃ (sapphire/corundum); gallium arsenide LED substrates.

### Aluminum motors vs. copper generators
- **Anchor:** `§4.p3`–`§4.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Aluminum motors vs. copper generators"
- **Frame in this lecture:** The central counterexample. Aluminum has 2× the specific electrical conductivity of copper; used in motors but not in large generators.

### Langley helicopter supersonic blade tip experiment
- **Anchor:** `§9.p2`–`§9.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Langley helicopter supersonic blade tip experiment"

### Military vs. civilian gas turbine engine divergence
- **Anchor:** `§10.p8`–`§10.p10`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Military vs. civilian gas turbine engine divergence"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Until 1980s, military pushed engine technology and civilian followed. With the turbofan, civilian engines diverged — supersonic fighters can't use fans, so the dual-use technology pipeline broke.
