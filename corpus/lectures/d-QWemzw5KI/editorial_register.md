All conventions from the catalog applied: student attribution (§2.p4), stage directions (§2.p1), numerals ($1,500, 20 ksi, 40 ksi, 6061, 2219, etc.), self-corrections smoothed, backward-references cut (§4.p2 "until yesterday"), "okay" preserved where it closes a thought (§2.p7, §2.p8) and cut where mid-clause filler, "you know" cut as filler throughout, pocket-patting cut (§4.p1).

## Flags for editor review

1. **§2.p4 student attribution.** The "NASCAR" answer is inferred from Tom's response pattern in layer 2 `p12`. Confirm against audio.
2. **§2.p1 "15,000-thick" figure.** Tom's spoken phrasing about the beryllium piece pricing is ambiguous; preserved verbatim from layer 2. Likely "fifteen-thousand-dollar piece" or "15,000-dollar block-of-thickness" — editor to confirm against audio.
3. **§2.p1 stage direction span.** The bar-passing happens across the paragraph; rendered as a single stage direction at the moment of "here's a bar of magnesium." If the bars were produced sequentially with separate pauses, the stage direction should be split.

## Decisions NOT permitted at layer 3 (constitutional constraint)

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
