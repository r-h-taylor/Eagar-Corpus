### Egyptian obelisk fracture mechanics (Al Bakaven thesis)
- **Anchor:** `§9.p5`–`§9.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** Egyptian obelisk engineering mystery (Al Bakun / Wendell Wilkenning fracture analysis)
- **Frame in this lecture:** Mid-1970s MIT thesis (Wendell Wilkening under Al Bakaven). Fracture mechanics predicts the obelisk should snap under its own weight during raising; reality is the three-phase granite microstructure forms toughening microcracks at flaw tips, giving 2–3× the toughness predicted from single-phase rocks.
- **Materials/systems:** Granite (silica + feldspar + third phase).
- **Era:** Antiquity (obelisks); 1970s (analysis).

### America's Cup hydraulic hardware failure (Navtec)

- **Materials/systems:** 7075-T6 → 7075-T7 aluminum.

## Background statistics and figures referenced (not cases)

- **Cast iron and steel gas pipe corrosion in the US (USA Today framing):** 80,000 miles of cast iron pipe; pipe history (cast iron 1870s → bare steel 1950s → plastic 1980s); $10B Con Ed replacement estimate; 4.5% gas loss in Pensacola; 10% Boston water loss; $1M/mile urban replacement cost; $10T civil-infrastructure deficit. Anchor: `§4.p2`–`§4.p4`, `§5.p3`–`§5.p4`.
- **Material moduli (psi):** Diamond/ceramics ~60M; steel ~30M; titanium ~20M; aluminum ~10M. Anchor: `§6.p1`.
- **Theoretical bond strength:** ~3M psi from 2–3 eV primary bond. Achieved on iron whiskers (2.8M psi), sapphire fibers (>2M psi), fiberglass fibers (0.5M psi). Anchor: `§6.p3`–`§7.p3`.
- **Fracture toughness ranges:** Steels up to ~100 MPa·m^½; gray cast iron 10–18; ceramics generally lower than cast iron. Anchor: `§9.p2`, `§10.p2`.
- **Turbine-disk economics:** Triple vacuum arc remelted disks cost $4–5K/ton vs $1–1.5K/ton for standard; 16" diameter ingots; $100K finished disk. Anchor: `§3.p4`.
