## Conventions applied (per project catalog)

1. **Student attribution.** §5.p2 labeled `**Student:**`.
2. **Stage directions.** §2.p2 (sample passing) and §4.p3 (plexiglass beam shape sample).
3. **Numerals.** Years as digits (1973, 1975, 1990). Monetary values: "$100,000," "$10 million," "$20 million," "quarter billion dollars" / "quarter-billion-dollar." Percentages as "30%," "75%," "5%" (technical-statistical context throughout). Thicknesses preserved as Tom delivered them: "1,000 microns," "6 to 8 inch," "2 inches," "4,000ths of an inch."
4. **Self-corrections.** Smoothed throughout per convention 4. Specific instances logged above.
5. **Backward-references.** Cut from §1.p2 ("we talked about... camshafts"); preserved in §7.p5 where Tom is genuinely cross-referencing future content.
6. **"Okay" handling.** Many "okays" cut as mid-clause filler; those closing a thought rhetorically preserved (e.g., end of §6.p4).
7. **"You know" / "I mean."** Cut throughout except where they invoke shared context.

## Soft reordering log

No across-section reordering. Two within-section paragraph splits:
- **§3:** L2 `p13` split into §3.p2 (abstract spare-parts argument) and §3.p3 (Wyoming concrete case). Both anchor to `p13`. Justification: aids readability; Tom delivers the abstract logic and then the concrete instance, but they run together in the spoken delivery.

## Decisions NOT permitted at layer 3 (constitutional constraint)

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
