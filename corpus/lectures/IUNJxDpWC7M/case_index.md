# Case Index — Lecture 6 (IUNJxDpWC7M)

*Citation format: layer 3 paragraph anchor (e.g. `§2.p2`). Click-through in the static site reveals the corresponding layer 2 passage with timestamp range.*

This lecture is the corrosion-mechanisms session of the welding metallurgy course. Tom teaches four factors that drive corrosion — oxygen, chlorides, moisture, and (foreshadowed for after the break) acidity — and surfaces several cases as teaching exemplars.

## Cases referenced

### Boston warehouse fire protection system corrosion
- **Anchor:** `§2.p1`, `§2.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Boston warehouse fire protection system corrosion" *(matches canonical: "Warehouse fire protection system piping corrosion" / "Boston warehouse fire protection system corrosion")*
- **Frame in this lecture:** Tom's forensic call-back: he diagnosed the open-system corrosion from a sample alone, by inferring the chronic leak. Used to illustrate the principle "no free oxygen, no corrosion" inverted — a small chronic leak destroys the closed-system condition.
- **Materials/systems:** carbon steel fire protection sprinkler piping, makeup water introducing dissolved oxygen.
- **Era:** consulting case, undated.

### General Electric boiling water reactor stress corrosion cracking (304 stainless safe ends)
- **Anchor:** `§3.p3`, `§3.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "General Electric boiling water reactor stress corrosion cracking" / "General Electric nuclear reactor safe-end cracking"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Late 1970s industry-wide problem. Tom uses it to motivate the chloride+oxygen synergy and the practice of pre-boiler oxygen sparging. Quantifies it as a $2B problem for GE worldwide.
- **Materials/systems:** 304 stainless steel safe-end piping, ~1 ppm oxygen + 0.1 ppm chloride.
- **Era:** late 1970s onward.

### Stainless steel dinnerware bleach pitting (personal anecdote)
- **Anchor:** `§4.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Stainless steel dinnerware bleach pitting (personal anecdote)"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Domestic illustration of the chloride + oxygen pitting mechanism on stainless. Mother-in-law sterilized dinnerware in Clorox overnight.
- **Materials/systems:** stainless steel flatware, sodium hypochlorite solution.
- **Era:** undated personal anecdote (within Tom's 17 years of cohabitation with mother-in-law).

### Arizona warehouse roof corrosion failure
- **Anchor:** `§5.p3`, `§5.p4`, `§5.p5`, `§5.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Arizona warehouse roof corrosion failure"
- **Frame in this lecture:** The worst carbon-steel corrosion rate Tom has ever seen (40–80 mil/year). Diurnal wet-dry cycling between vapor barrier and roof decking created a sauna in the ceiling cavity that destroyed galvanized steel hangers within a year.
- **Materials/systems:** galvanized steel sheet-metal hangers, vapor barrier roof assembly.
- **Era:** consulting case, undated.

### Mothballed B-52s at the Arizona aircraft boneyard
- **Anchor:** `§5.p1`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** PROPOSED: "Arizona aircraft boneyard (Davis-Monthan AFB) mothballing practice"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Brief reference, not a forensic case. Used to make the moisture-pathway point by counterexample (low-humidity desert preserves metals) and to contrast with Navy practice of storing assets in seawater.
- **Era:** SALT-treaty era reference, late Cold War.

### Nuclear submarine / carrier reactor shutdown with hydrazine
- **Anchor:** `§3.p7`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** PROPOSED: "Navy reactor shutdown hydrazine oxygen scavenging"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Pedagogical contrast with commercial pre-boiler sparging. Navy uses hydrazine; commercial plants can't afford it and use steam sparge instead.
- **Era:** ongoing Navy practice.

## Figures referenced (not cases)

- **0.21 atmospheres** — equilibrium oxygen partial pressure in air, anchoring the closed-system magnetite argument (`§1.p2`).
- **18 megohms** — the resistivity of theoretically pure water; commercial reactors run at 10–14 megohms (`§3.p4`).
- **4 mil/year (typical), 10 mil/year (upper), 40–80 mil/year (Arizona warehouse)** — Tom's standing reference figures for carbon steel corrosion rates (`§5.p2`, `§5.p5`).
- **$2 billion** — scale of the GE BWR safe-end stress corrosion cracking problem worldwide (`§3.p6`).
