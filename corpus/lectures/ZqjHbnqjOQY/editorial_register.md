# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture: Corrosion (Summer 2016), 5/5 (`ZqjHbnqjOQY`). This register records editorial judgments made in lifting the cleanup edit to the readable edition.*

## Sectioning rationale

The lecture is structured as a single extended case study (the National Academy / NSF Alvin replacement study) with a thematic pivot at the end to naval shipyard inspection practices. Section breaks fall at:

- §1 → §2: pivot from the Alvin's history to the Navy R&D funding structure (Mansfield Amendment digression sets this up).
- §2 → §3: return from funding-structure digression to the engineering design of the new Alvin (start of "what the scientists wanted").
- §3 → §4: shift from upgrade list to Sea Cliff / Thresher historical material and the pressure-test vessel.
- §4 → §5: pivot from Sea Cliff hull considerations to the cost-per-pound analysis (Jason 2 vs. new Alvin).
- §5 → §6: pivot from cost estimate to what blew up the actual contract (China metals market, lost infrastructure).
- §6 → §7: pivot from Alvin-specific cost to the broader naval-shipyard productivity scandal that the Alvin overrun is compared to. Q&A on radiography at the end of §6 anticipates §7's full development.

## Convention flags

- The **HY-140 [HY-100]** correction (§1.p1) is the only case in this lecture of a Tom spoken slip requiring bracketed correction. Per convention, future occurrences in the same section would be `[HY-100]` alone; only first occurrence carries the slip.
- The **"on the Stellar"** captioner garbling (§6.p3) was unambiguous enough to cut silently rather than preserve with `[?]`. Flagged here because the captioner-derived phrase had no obvious correct reading; the cleanest editorial response was to drop the introductory clause entirely.
- The **Student question reconstruction** at §6.p7 ("Can you do radiography on titanium?") is inferred from Tom's response. Flagged.
- The **inaudible student turn** at §7.p2 is rendered as a bracketed description rather than `[inaudible]`, because Tom's response establishes the topic. Flagged.
- **Layer 2 p14 spans the §4/§5 section boundary.** This is the only intra-paragraph section break in the lecture. Flagged for editor review; alternative sectioning available.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
