# Case Index — Lecture 2018-02-07

*Citation format: layer 3 paragraph anchor (e.g. `§4.p4`). Click-through in the static site reveals the corresponding layer 2 passage with timestamp range.*

This lecture is the opening of the Spring 2018 Total Quality Improvement module. It is heavy on framing and biographical context for TQM as a discipline, and threads several recurring corpus cases through that frame: the World Trade Center collapse paper, the Sony Walkman / neodymium magnets, the Bethlehem Steel Burns Harbor cost figure, POSCO, Baosteel and Chinese steel, the Honda Civic rust history, US Steel under Kennedy, and the USS *Thresher* / SUBSAFE story. The final arc is autobiographical: MIT's manufacturing turf wars in the 1980s.

### IBM customer-driven quality and salaried-workforce reduction
- **Anchor:** `§3.p3`, `§3.p4`, `§3.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "IBM mainframe and salaried workforce restructuring"
- **Frame in this lecture:** The TQM challenge week at the IBM executive training facility on the Hudson, ~1990. IBM's reduction of salaried staff by ~45,000 without touching hourly workers, and the executive's admission that fewer problems rose to the surface afterward. Tom's foundational story for why TQM matters.
- **Era:** circa 1990

### Bethlehem Steel Burns Harbor cost figure
- **Anchor:** `§4.p4`, `§4.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Bethlehem Steel Burns Harbor"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Cost of last integrated steel mill built by a company rather than a country ($5B in late 1960s; Tom's $15B figure multiplied by 3x for 30 years of inflation). Cited in Clayton Christensen's *The Innovator's Dilemma* via Tom's daughter Rebecca.
- **Era:** 1965–1971 construction; cited in 1990s scholarship

### Clayton Christensen and the steel mill cost research
- **Anchor:** `§4.p3`, `§4.p4`, `§5.p5`

### Tom's welder-population estimate and the Welding Handbook

### US-Japan trade and the Star Wars debt
- **Anchor:** `§6.p4`, `§6.p7`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** PROPOSED: Reagan Star Wars and Japanese financing of US debt

### Japan-US Oil and Steel Embargo (pre-WWII)
- **Anchor:** `§6.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Japan-US Oil and Steel Embargo (World War II)"
- **Frame in this lecture:** US 1930s cutoff of scrap steel shipments to Japan as one cause of Pacific War; framed in context of attempted appeasement re: Nanking.
- **Era:** late 1930s

### Honda Civic rust failure (1970s)
- **Anchor:** `§6.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Honda Civic rust failure (1970s)"
- **Frame in this lecture:** 1975 Honda Civic as joke product (rusty within twelve months); Japanese response was to engineer the rust-free Civic of today. Example of being "embarrassed into" quality improvement.
- **Era:** 1970s

### US Steel price rollback under Kennedy (1962)
- **Anchor:** `§6.p9`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "US Steel price control by President Kennedy (1962)"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Kennedy forced US Steel to back down from a 10% price increase to avoid global inflation; US Steel later blamed this decision for its decline. Used to mark the transition from steel to energy as the world's price-setting commodity.
- **Era:** 1962

### 1973 Arab oil embargo / energy as global commodity
- **Anchor:** `§6.p9`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "1973 Arab oil embargo"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Marker for the 1960s–1975 transition during which energy replaced steel as the world economy's denominating commodity.

### USS *Thresher* disaster (1963)
- **Anchor:** `§7.p1`, `§7.p2`, `§7.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "USS Thresher"
- **Frame in this lecture:** April 10, 1963 sinking off Cape Cod during first controlled deep dive. Brazed sea-water valve failure; ballast tank freeze-up on emergency blow; men heard screaming on tender's sonar; collapse at ~6,000 ft. The disaster that prompted SUBSAFE under Rickover with W. Edwards Deming as quality consultant.
- **Materials/systems:** brazed steel sea-water piping, reactor cooling system
- **Era:** 1963

### SUBSAFE program and Navy origins of TQM terminology
- **Anchor:** `§7.p3`, `§7.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** PROPOSED: SUBSAFE program and Navy origins of TQM
- **Frame in this lecture:** Three-year submarine production shutdown post-*Thresher*; SUBSAFE instituted with Deming's involvement; redesigned joints for what would later be called Six Sigma quality. Tom claims the Navy coined "total quality management" terminology in mid-1980s memos.
- **Era:** 1963 origin, 1980s terminology

### *The Machine That Changed the World* and lean manufacturing
- **Anchor:** `§7.p4`, `§8.p1`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** PROPOSED: MIT International Motor Vehicle Program and the origin of "lean"
- **Frame in this lecture:** $5M, five-year MIT study (Womack, Jones, Roos) on the future of the automobile; origin of the term "lean manufacturing." Toyota beating GM/Ford/Chrysler on quality.
- **Era:** late 1980s

### MIT manufacturing turf wars: Materials Processing Center vs. Lab for Manufacturing and Productivity
- **Anchor:** `§8.p1`, `§8.p2`, `§8.p3`, `§8.p4`, `§8.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** PROPOSED: MIT Materials Processing Center vs. LMP turf war (1980s)
- **Frame in this lecture:** Tom's first-person account of routing his Navy welding contract through Flemings's Materials Processing Center; giving Dave Hardt his first contract; the Suh-vs-Flemings feud; Kent Bowen's 1984 offer of the MPC directorship; the 1989 founding of Leaders for Manufacturing with $35M. Tom announces this story will continue next lecture.

### Leaders for Manufacturing / LGO program founding
