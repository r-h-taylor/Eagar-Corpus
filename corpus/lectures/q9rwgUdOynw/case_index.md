## Cases referenced in this lecture

### Alcoa v. aluminum extrusion manufacturer (Boeing wing spar data theft)
- **Anchor:** `§1.p3`, `§1.p5`, `§1.p8`, `§2.p2`, `§2.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Alcoa v. aluminum extrusion manufacturer (Boeing wing spar data theft)"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Active consulting case at time of recording. Alcoa lost 50% of its aircraft wing spar business to a competitor that stole trade-secret tighter compositional specs on 7150 aluminum. Used to teach the difference between published Aluminum Association alloy designations and proprietary internal specs, and the contractual auditing arrangements between alloy supplier (Alcoa) and aircraft OEM (Boeing/Airbus).
- **Materials/systems:** 7000-series aluminum (sold as 7150), 2000-series aluminum
- **Era:** ~2016 (active at time of lecture)

### New England Aquarium sea water piping corrosion
- **Anchor:** `§5.p5`, `§5.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "New England Aquarium sea water piping corrosion"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Stainless steel failed (pitting from dirt), Monel was intermediate, titanium would have been best but cost-prohibitive. Used to illustrate the trade-off ladder for seawater service.
- **Materials/systems:** stainless steel (pitting failure), Monel, titanium
- **Era:** referenced as prior story

### Under-deposit corrosion in cast iron (Hurricane Sandy context)
- **Anchor:** `§3.p2`, `§3.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Under-deposit corrosion in cast iron (Hurricane Sandy context)"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Student-presented case (Brian). Graphitic corrosion of cast iron component exposed to seawater after Hurricane Sandy; the silicon-derived glassy passive film was breached by chlorides under deposits. Tom appends the "cleanliness is next to godliness" corrosion rule.
- **Materials/systems:** cast iron (Fe-4%C-3%Si)
- **Era:** post-2012 (Hurricane Sandy)

### Mormon Temple heat exchanger erosion-corrosion failure
- **Anchor:** `§6.p1`, `§6.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Mormon Temple heat exchanger erosion-corrosion failure"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Belmont, MA. Copper heat exchanger failed by erosion-corrosion at tube-sheet inlet after one year. Tom recommended 70/30 cupro-nickel; only 90/10 was available within the two-week shutdown window; 90/10 has worked for fifteen years. Sub-plot on contractor markup (16k quoted vs 6k from manufacturer).
- **Materials/systems:** copper (failed), 90/10 cupro-nickel (substituted)
- **Era:** ~2001 (15 years before lecture)

### Phoenix warehouse accelerated corrosion during construction
- **Anchor:** `§7.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Phoenix warehouse accelerated corrosion during construction"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Million-square-foot warehouse roof. Insulation got wet during construction rainstorm; deck closure trapped moisture in an 8-inch greenhouse cavity under Arizona sun. Corrosion rate of 20 mils/year (5× typical) perforated 1/16" sheet metal within three years.
- **Materials/systems:** galvanized sheet steel
- **Era:** unspecified

### Miami Art Museum hanging garden failure
- **Anchor:** `§8.p1`–`§8.p8`, `§9.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Miami Art Museum hanging garden failure"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Six-story hanging garden 100 yards from Biscayne Bay. 410 martensitic stainless steel tension bolts failed during construction (low fracture toughness + tack welds). 316 proposed; Tom warned of SCC. Settled on 2205 duplex (Sandvik recommendation), no weld details. Cost increase 7–8M on a 60M building. Tom refused to sign off as engineer of record. Used to teach martensitic vs duplex stainless steels, stress corrosion cracking, and consulting liability.
- **Materials/systems:** 410 martensitic stainless (failed), 316 austenitic (rejected on SCC grounds), 2205 duplex stainless (used)
- **Era:** "a few years ago" — circa early 2010s
