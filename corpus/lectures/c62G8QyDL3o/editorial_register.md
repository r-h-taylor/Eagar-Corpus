# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 31 (Welding Metallurgy, Summer 2014). This register records the editorial judgments made in lifting the cleanup edit to the readable edition.*

## Sectioning

The 37-minute lecture divides cleanly along teaching transitions: weldability tables → Mil Handbook 5 → structural welding codes → allowable stress design and aluminum toughness → Navy ships excursion → filler metal selection → joint design (with the Millstone digression) → Tulsa union digression → joint design designs from the manual → hydrogen porosity. Ten sections; some are short (§5, §8) because they are coherent self-contained digressions Tom inserted between technical units.

The Millstone radar dish story (§7) is grouped with joint design because Tom returned to joint design via the Millstone case (the "reason I got on that story" tag at §7.p10). The Tulsa pipeline story (§8) gets its own section because Tom explicitly pivots ("That's also true in some types of welding of steel for example, but it's more political in that case") and then returns to aluminum design at §9.

### §4 — Allowable stress design

- The lecture ends mid-sentence at §10.p2 ("you have about a fifty..."). Preserved as a truncated final paragraph — the recording cuts off here. Not edited to a complete sentence (constitutional constraint: cannot add Tom's words).

## Soft reorderings
None within sections beyond the smoothing of self-corrections to the corrected form. The §4.p2 yield-strength correction is the most substantive single edit and is recorded above.

## Decisions NOT permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
