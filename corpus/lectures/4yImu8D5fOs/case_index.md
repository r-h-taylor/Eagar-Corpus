### Stainless steel pipes with bleach storage
- **Anchor:** `§6.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Stainless steel pipes with bleach storage`

- **Materials/systems:** austenitic stainless steel, chloride exposure, stress corrosion cracking.

## Figures and framing references

### Schaeffler diagram (1949-era constitution diagram for stainless welds)
- **Anchor:** `§3.p4`–`§3.p6`
- **Type:** named engineering chart, recurring teaching aid.
- **Frame in this lecture:** the central pedagogical tool of the first half of the lecture. Balmforth uses it to locate austenitic, ferritic, martensitic, and duplex alloy regions, and to introduce the iso-ferrite line concept that returns in §7 and §8.

### WRC 1992 diagram (Welding Research Council constitution diagram)
- **Anchor:** `§8.p8`–`§8.p10`
- **Type:** named engineering chart, modern successor to Schaeffler.
- **Frame in this lecture:** Balmforth's preferred chart for predicting solidification mode and avoiding cracking. Introduced as a refinement of the Schaeffler diagram with revised chromium and nickel equivalents and explicit solidification-mode boundaries (A, AF, FA, F).

### Balmforth's PhD work on martensitic-to-ferritic constitution diagrams
- **Anchor:** `§3.p6`
- **Type:** speaker's own research, mentioned in passing.
- **Frame in this lecture:** "That's actually where most of my research was, in this region down here. I developed one of these type of diagrams for this martensitic to ferritic region." Not developed as a case; mentioned only to locate his expertise.

## Notes on the case index methodology for didactic lectures

This lecture is unlike the case-dense Eagar lectures: it is a teaching session built around two engineering charts and one phase diagram, with cases used sparingly as illustrations. The two indexed cases (glove-box welding, bleach-under-the-sink) are both illustrative rather than forensic. Neither has the developed-teaching-unit character of the corpus's named cases (e.g. *USS Sheffield*, *Hyatt Regency walkway collapse*, *Tacoma Narrows Bridge*).
