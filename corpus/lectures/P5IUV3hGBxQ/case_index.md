# Case Index — Lecture 2018-02-28

*Citation format: layer 3 paragraph anchor (e.g. `§6.p4`). Click-through reveals the corresponding layer 2 passage.*

This lecture is a synthesis session on TQM, leadership, and the limits of management buzzwords. Cases are illustrative — most are touched on briefly to make a methodological point rather than developed as full forensic units. The Rolls-Royce / Detroit Diesel Allison fishbone-meeting story (§6) and the Sioux City crash preview (§8) are the most developed.

## Cases referenced

### Ford F-150 plant — theory of constraints failure
- **Anchor:** `§1.p1`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Ford F-150 plant labor constraint"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Tom's go-to counterexample to theory of constraints. The foreman tells him the bottleneck is people, not machines — undermining the textbook ToC framing.
- **Era:** ongoing reference; lecture is 2018.

### Pratt & Whitney New Haven turbine vane plant
- **Anchor:** `§1.p4`–`§1.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Pratt & Whitney turbine blade manufacturing constraint"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Positive example of theory of constraints. The $200M broaching machine is a genuine single bottleneck; cellular manufacturing reorganized around it. 40% of P&W profits from 20% of business (vanes).
- **Materials/systems:** turbine vane castings; Christmas-tree root broaching; mechanical interlock for 20,000 RPM service.

### Ford Taurus bumper paint line
- **Anchor:** `§1.p7`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Ford Taurus bumper paint line constraint"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Second positive ToC example. $200M UV-cure paint line; polypropylene-oxide bumper adhesion problem; throughput controlled by the one paint line regardless of injection-molder capacity.
- **Materials/systems:** polypropylene oxide bumper; UV-cure paint.

### General Motors Chevy Cavalier plant just-in-time excess
- **Anchor:** `§3.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "General Motors Chevy Cavalier just-in-time assembly plant"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Manager-by-rote: enforcing one-hour-stock JIT even for fastener bins where a week's supply fit in a small box. Used to make the "common sense in applying tools" point.
- **Era:** circa 1990s.

### First Gulf War helicopter engine sand ingestion
- **Anchor:** `§4.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "First Gulf War helicopter engine failures from sand ingestion"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Origin story for the Defense Department's rapid prototyping investment that later became "agile manufacturing." Helicopters abraded in three minutes; engine particle separators developed in field.

### MIT additive manufacturing startup ($220M powder metallurgy venture)
- **Anchor:** `§5.p2`–`§5.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "PROPOSED: MIT $220M powder metallurgy 3D printing startup (circa 2017)"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Tom's skepticism case for 3D printing at automotive cost targets. $10,000/lb baseline vs. $2/lb automotive target; twenty print heads as factor-of-ten improvement still leaves factor-of-500 gap.
- **Note:** Likely Desktop Metal (founded 2015 by MIT faculty including Ely Sachs, Chris Schuh, etc.). Tom's wording is deliberately oblique. May correspond to existing cluster "Desktop Metal eyeglass hinges" or "3D metal sintering venture (concept laser acquisition)" — needs reconciliation.

### MIT 3D inkjet printing origin (Sachs and Cima)
- **Anchor:** `§5.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "PROPOSED: MIT 3D inkjet printing origin (Sachs/Cima, early 1990s)"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Historical origin of the term "3D printing" — inkjet head depositing adhesive into ceramic powder beds at MIT.
- **Era:** early 1990s.

### Rolls-Royce / Detroit Diesel Allison turbine disc failures
- **Anchor:** `§6.p4`–`§6.p9`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Rolls-Royce/Detroit Diesel Allison turbine disc failures"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Anchor anecdote for the limits of the Ishikawa diagram as a problem-solving tool. Thirteen unexplained failures over ten years; blue-ribbon panel; Tom shuts down a planned hour-and-a-half walk through the fishbone diagram.
- **Materials/systems:** turbine disc.

### Mad Hatter company (LFM student morale turnaround)
- **Anchor:** `§7.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Mad Hatter company morale and quality crisis"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Counterexample to theory X management. LFM student buys donuts for hourly workers and gets results management couldn't get with formal programs.

### Sioux City DC-10 engine disc failure (United 232)
- **Anchor:** `§8.p1`–`§8.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Sioux City DC-10 rotor failure"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Preview of Brian Hohmann's guest lecture. Titanium nitride inclusion in cast titanium disc; fatigue crack; both tail-mounted engines lost; emergency glide landing.
- **Materials/systems:** titanium turbine disc with hard-alpha (TiN) inclusion.
- **Note:** Tom says McDonnell Douglas DC-10 (correct) and contrasts with Sully / Hudson River landing (US Airways Flight 1549).

### US Airways Flight 1549 (Sully / Hudson River)
- **Anchor:** `§8.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "US Airways Flight 1549 (Sully)"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Mentioned only as the more famous later analog to the Sioux City glide landing.

### MIT student alcohol death

### Nashua Corporation CEO succession case
- **Anchor:** `§9.p7`–`§9.p8`

### MIT custodial Christmas breakfast (department-head leadership example)
