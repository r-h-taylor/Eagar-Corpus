# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 9jCXHL9awLk (3.371 Codes and Standards, Summer 2012, session 6/7). Guest lecture by a former Eagar postdoc. This register records the editorial decisions made in producing the readable edition.*

## Speaker note

This lecture is delivered by a guest — a structural/civil-engineering consultant who was a former Eagar postdoc. The speaker is not Tom. All standing conventions for "Tom's voice" apply by analogy to the guest speaker; in particular, no new sentences are added on his behalf.

## Conventions applied (per the standing catalog)

- Student turns labeled `**Student:**` where the layer 2 transcript records a question. Several student turns in layer 2 (p22, p33, p91, p93, p95, p97) record only the lecturer's repetition of the question; layer 3 reconstructs the student's question in brackets where layer 2 already does so. Where the student's actual phrasing is uncertain, the layer 3 student turn paraphrases the substance the lecturer responded to, with no quotation marks.
- Stage directions in italicized brackets for physical-board moves: `*[Draws.]*`, `*[The lecturer searches for a working marker.]*`.
- Filler ("um," "uh," "I mean," discourse-marker "you know") cut throughout.
- "Okay" cut as mid-clause filler; preserved where it closes a thought.
- Self-corrections smoothed to the corrected form.
- Numerals: technical/datum values kept numeric ("12 KSI", "30,000 cycles", "1.5", "10 to the minus six"); years as digits ("2007"); rhetorical/round counts spelled out ("fifty percent", "twenty percent", "a hundred-year period"); percentages in prose spelled out per convention.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3 (constitutional constraint)

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
