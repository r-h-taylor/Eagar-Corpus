*Lecture 9jCXHL9awLk (Codes and Standards, Summer 2012, lecture 6/7). Guest lecture given by a former Eagar postdoc (not Tom himself; Tom is referenced in third person as "Professor Eagar"). Mechanical rules applied to the raw transcript to produce the cleaned-up middle layer.*

## Global rules applied

1. **Line-broken delivery joined.** Auto-caption line breaks (typically 3–6 words per line) merged into continuous sentences.

2. **Sentence boundaries inserted.** Periods, commas, question marks, capitalization added at audible pauses and grammatical completions.

3. **Captioner errors corrected silently** where unambiguous. Logged individually below.

4. **Speaker's misstatements / slips** bracketed where they would mislead. None requiring `[brackets]` in this lecture — the speaker self-corrects audibly several times and those self-corrections are preserved as-is.

5. **Student turns labeled** `**Student:**`. Most student questions in this lecture are inaudible/off-mic and the speaker repeats them back; in those cases the student turn is rendered as a bracketed paraphrase `[Question about ...]`.

6. **Paragraph breaks at natural pauses** — 98 paragraphs across the ~87-minute lecture.

7. **Preserved as-is:** filler ("um," "uh," "you know," "I mean," "okay"), false starts, self-corrections, marker-search business, repetitions.
