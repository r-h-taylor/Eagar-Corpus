### Faberware Millennium cookware hydrogen cracking
- **Anchor:** `§1.p1`–`§1.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Faberware Millennium cookware hydrogen cracking` (provisional match; see register flag — may overlap with `Millennium cookware hydrogen cracking and Teflon coating` and/or `High-strength steel cookware hydrogen cracking`)
- **Frame in this section:** Top-of-the-line cookware shipped from Brooklyn to Japan develops cracks in transit. Tom diagnoses delayed hydrogen cracking. The three ingredients line up: martensitic structure (from the steel choice), residual stress (from forming), and a hydrogen source — which turns out to be the 95 argon / 5 hydrogen plasma-spray gas used to deposit a porous stainless steel surface onto the cookware interior so the non-stick polymer can impregnate it. Fix: take the hydrogen out of the plasma-spray gas.
- **Materials/systems:** martensitic stainless steel cookware body; plasma-sprayed stainless steel powder porous surface; non-stick polymer impregnation; 95Ar–5H₂ spray gas.
- **Era:** not stated in fragment; cluster context suggests late 1990s.
