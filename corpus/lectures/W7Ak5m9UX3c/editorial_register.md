## Sectioning rationale

The lecture as recorded is a single 9-minute teaching unit on preheat (Tom closes with "that's enough for today"). It is short, but it has a clean five-part structure that maps directly to the teaching content:

- **§1** introduces the three levels of preheat as a taxonomy (the "what we're going to cover" frame), including the Seawolf submarine illustration of why preheat matters operationally.
- **§2** treats level one (≥50°F minimum) — the moisture argument.
- **§3** treats level two (100–300°F) — slowing the cooling rate to allow hydrogen diffusion.
- **§4** treats level three (>300°F) — tempering of martensite.
- **§5** synthesizes via the Venn diagram and closes.

Section breaks fall at natural teaching transitions: each new temperature regime is a new section, and the Venn diagram synthesis is a distinct unit. Sections are short (1–2 minutes each), but the lecture itself is short.
