## Convention catalog (carried from §1 worked example)

1. Student questions get `**Student:**` attribution. (No clearly labeled student turns in this segment; flagged §2.)
2. Physical-object teaching moves get italicized stage directions. (None in this segment.)
3. Monetary values: `$50,000`, `$30,000`; rhetorical numbers spelled out.
4. Self-corrections smoothed to the corrected form in layer 3; preserved in layer 2.
5. Backward-references to prior lecture sessions cut from layer 3, *except* where they carry teaching content (here: §5.p1 "we were starting to talk about earing" is preserved because the section actually continues that thread).
6. Tom's "okay" preserved where it closes a thought; cut where it's mid-clause filler.
7. "You know" and "I mean" cut except where they invoke shared context.
8. Soft reordering permitted within a section, never across. Logged here every time.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could do this because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases that Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
