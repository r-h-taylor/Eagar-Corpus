## Sectioning decisions

The lecture divides naturally into twelve sections, organized around two arcs: the composites framing (§§1–6) and the X-33 case study (§§7–12). Section breaks fall at clear teaching transitions: each new conceptual unit (what is a composite, Boeing trajectory, questions to ask, composite forms), the Q&A interruption on the nacelle (§6), and the staged unfolding of the X-33 failure narrative (§§7–12). The X-33 narrative could be condensed into fewer sections but Tom builds it in distinct teaching units — the program description, the reliability digression with Feynman, the EAPS digression, the rapid-prototyping contract framing, the autoclave/safety-factor problem, and the hydrogen permeability finale — each of which is its own teaching beat.
