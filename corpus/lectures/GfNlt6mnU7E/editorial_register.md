# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 22 (Welding Metallurgy, Spring 2014). This register records editorial judgments lifting the cleanup edit to the readable edition.*

## Sectioning rationale

The lecture divides into nine sections at natural teaching transitions:
- **§1** (electrodes / welding symbols) — opening prop demonstrations.
- **§2** (structure of welding codes) — Tom pivots from passing materials around to explaining the D-series codes.
- **§3** (economics of codes) — Q&A about update frequency opens an extended digression on the code-publishing business, NIST, journals.
- **§4** (strength / specific strength) — slide change; Tom pivots to the aluminum lecture proper.
- **§5** (alloy designations) — first systematic treatment of the 1000–8000 series.
- **§6** (temper designations) — H, F, T system.
- **§7** (weldability) — alloy-by-alloy weldability grading.
- **§8** (phase diagrams / freezing range) — physical-metallurgical explanation.
- **§9** (heat-treated strength / precipitation) — strength data and aging cycle. Recording cuts off mid-paragraph.

- **§1.p3.** "Okay" closing the thought ("not a very significant code, okay") cut as filler in this position; the sentence resolves cleanly without it. "So far as that goes" trailing the paragraph cut as filler.

## Flags for editor review

- **§2.p3** "unless complicated" → "unless it got simpler." This is the largest interpretive call in the lecture. Tom's spoken phrasing is genuinely ambiguous ("unless complicated" could mean "unless they made it more complicated to read, which they did via referencing another document" — which is in fact what he goes on to say). Editor may wish to revisit.
- **§3.p5** — "Howitt Campbell was a Harvard" silently corrected to "was at Harvard." If Tom in fact said "was a Harvard man" or similar, the cut "man" should be restored. Recommend listening to the audio at ~09:30.
- **§4.p3** — "South Carol—" cut as a verbal slip (Tom corrects to "Southern California"). Confirmed by context (California wildfires, drought).

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3 (constitutional constraint)

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
