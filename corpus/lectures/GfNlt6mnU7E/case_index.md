# Case Index — Lecture 22 (Welding Metallurgy, Spring 2014)

*Citation format: layer 3 paragraph anchor (e.g. `§4.p3`). Click-through in the static site reveals the corresponding layer 2 passage with timestamp range.*

This lecture is primarily a technical exposition of welding codes and aluminum metallurgy. Cases appear mostly as brief illustrative references rather than developed teaching units.

## Cases referenced

### 7,500-ton forging press
- **Anchor:** `§2.p1`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "7500-ton forging press failure"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Cited as the kind of heavy machinery that falls under AWS D14 (welded joints in machinery equipment) rather than D1 (structural) or the ASME boiler and pressure vessel code.

### California transmission line wildfires
- **Anchor:** `§4.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "California transmission line wildfires"
- **Frame in this lecture:** 1060 aluminum catenary lines (on steel core) sag in summer heat, contact trees, arc, ignite drought-dry vegetation. Utilities settle for hundreds of millions, recover via the rate base. Used to motivate the introduction of 1060 alloy and to illustrate why aluminum's low-temperature strength loss matters.
- **Materials/systems:** 1060 aluminum on steel core, 300,000-volt transmission lines.

### Space Shuttle main tank (2219 aluminum)
- **Anchor:** `§7.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Space Shuttle External Tank (2219 aluminum)"
- **Frame in this lecture:** 2219 chosen as the rare aluminum-copper alloy with good weldability. Tank had to be welded; leaks were unacceptable.
- **Materials/systems:** 2219 aluminum-copper alloy.

### Space Shuttle Challenger O-ring
- **Anchor:** `§7.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Space Shuttle Challenger"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Referenced as the cautionary example for what happens when joints leak — motivating why the external tank used welded 2219 aluminum.

### Wright Brothers aircraft engine
- **Anchor:** `§5.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Wright Brothers aircraft engine"
- **Frame in this lecture:** First aerospace use of aluminum-copper alloy (engine housing, 1903). Used to anchor the historical depth of the 2000-series aerospace alloys.
- **Era:** 1903.

### Coca-Cola / beer cans (3004 aluminum)
- **Anchor:** `§5.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Coca-Cola can manufacturing"
- **Frame in this lecture:** 3004 is "thirty or forty percent of all the aluminum made" — one of the largest-tonnage alloys, work-hardened, lightly alloyed with manganese and magnesium.

### Werner Ladder Company extrusion
- **Anchor:** `§6.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "PROPOSED: Werner Ladder Company extrusion temper case"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Used to illustrate T1 temper — extrusion heat itself serves as the solutionizing treatment; the ladder rail ages naturally as it cools in the plant. A teaching scenario rather than a documented failure case.
- **Materials/systems:** Aluminum extrusion, T1 temper.
