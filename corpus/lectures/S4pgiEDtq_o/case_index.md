*Citation format: layer 3 paragraph anchor (e.g. `§3.p4`).*

### GTD-111 superalloy patent dispute
- **Anchor:** `§9.p1`–`§9.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `GTD-111 superalloy patent dispute`
- **Frame in this lecture:** The central developed case of the lecture. - **Materials/systems:** directionally solidified nickel superalloy, hot isostatic pressing rejuvenation
- **Era:** 1980s patent filing through late 1990s adoption to early-2000s issuance

## Figures referenced (not cases)

- **Steel productivity:** one person-year per ton → 20 minutes per ton (~three tons per person-hour) — §3.p3. Same figure used in the lecture-11 worked example as "1 person-hour/ton (1990) → 0.3 person-hours/ton (today)." Tom is drawing on the same productivity arc from both ends.
- **Value-per-pound by industry:** automotive $2/lb, commercial aircraft $200/lb, aerospace $20,000/lb saved — §3.p4.
- **Materials energy content:** aluminum 6× steel; plastic 2× steel per pound — §4.p3, §4.p6.
- **Turbine firing temperature:** every 50 °C is $2 billion/year in airline fuel savings — §9.p2.
- **Westbrook log-log usage/cost plot:** 1962. Cost reduction by 2× → volume gain by 4× (usage slope steeper than iso-market slope) — §5.p3, §5.p7.
- **NdFeB volume advantage over Alnico:** ~50× smaller for the same 1000 G at 5 mm — §8.p8.
- **U.S. environmental damage / GDP:** GED/GDP fell from 6.4% to 3.2% (1999-2008); ~0.02% net GDP gain attributed to environmental improvement — §2.p2-p4.
