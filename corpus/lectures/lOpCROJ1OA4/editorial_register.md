## Items flagged for editor review
1. **§1.p2 — Charles Martin Hall name slip.** Tom says "Paul Charles Martin Hall." Preserved as spoken since the surrounding identification is unambiguous, but worth a footnote if this lecture is published.
2. **§2.p4 trailing fragment** ("the Navy is the crew leader services, my sophomore") — unrecoverable; cut at section boundary. If a higher-quality transcript source becomes available, recheck.
3. **§3.p3 temperature inconsistency** — Tom moves between 230°F, 329°F, and 320°F as aging temperatures. Layer 3 reads 320°F. If this lecture is being prepared for student reference, an editor should confirm against Tom's slide.
4. **§3.p5 "4504" alloy** — not a standard designation; possibly Tom intended 5054 or similar. Preserved as spoken.
5. **§4.p5 unit conversion** — Tom's 0.0308 inches → "three millimeters" is wrong (should be ~0.78 mm). He immediately restates as "one millimeter" which is consistent with the math. Readable form uses "three-quarters of a millimeter" and "one millimeter" — Tom's spoken slip not bracketed because it doesn't actively mislead.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
