# Case Index — Lecture 9 (3.371 Codes and Standards, Fall 2012, session 9/14)

*Note: this lecture is delivered primarily by Dr. Delmar (guest lecturer), with Tom Eagar interjecting at §6 and §13. The case load is therefore unusual for the Eagar corpus: most cases here are Dr. Delmar's, not Tom's. Tom's contributions surface one canonical Eagar case (the Florida tree stand) and one reference to a generic-but-recurring framing (structural welding code allowables).*

## Cases referenced in this lecture

### Car elevator loading failure
- **Anchor:** `§1.p2`–`§1.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Car elevator loading failure"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Dr. Delmar uses this case to illustrate how off-center loading by a forklift (small wheel span, fully loaded, all weight on one side) defeats a code-compliant design that assumed centered loading.
- **Era:** unspecified; Dr. Delmar's consulting work
- **Speaker:** Dr. Delmar

### Custom-built trailer design and field testing
- **Anchor:** `§2.p1`, §3–§5, §7
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Custom-built trailer design and field testing"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Dr. Delmar's own custom trailer — designed and built by him, in service ~8 years at time of recording. Central teaching example for the lecture: how to design without a code, how to use welds in compression rather than tension, and how field service history substitutes for prescriptive design.
- **Materials/systems:** structural steel beams (3-inch cross-section), single torsional-suspension axle, 1,000 lb/wheel tire rating, composite U-shape upper structure
- **Era:** built ~2004, in service through 2012
- **Speaker:** Dr. Delmar
- **Note:** also indexed as "Trailer overload with firewood (3000 lb vs. 2000 lb design)" in the canonical list — §3.p7 explicitly describes the firewood overload event. Two canonical clusters may reference the same case; reconciliation needed at aggregate level.

### Ladder with unauthorized welds
- **Anchor:** `§7.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Ladder with unauthorized welds"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Cautionary tale about specification gaps — a 24-foot ladder spec didn't prohibit welding, supplier built it from two 16-foot members welded together, "came out to be a very big problem."
- **Speaker:** Dr. Delmar
- **Note:** likely the same case as "Ladder standards specification outdated by regulation" in the canonical list, but Dr. Delmar's framing is about supplier behavior under an underspecified standard, not about regulatory obsolescence. Flagged for editorial review.

### ATV exhaust and water ingress assembly failures (or related ATV design cluster)
- **Anchor:** §8–§10
- **canonical_cluster_id:** PROPOSED: "ATV front suspension and control arm design"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Dr. Delmar's second extended case — design of ATV/quad front suspension A-arms, ball joints, knuckle, field testing with force transducers, fatigue treatment, manufacturing tolerances via segmented welds. Used to teach how field history substitutes for analytical worst-case design in mass-produced consumer vehicles.
- **Materials/systems:** welded hollow tube A-frames, ball-joint forgings, rubber wheels
- **Speaker:** Dr. Delmar
- **Note:** the canonical cluster "ATV exhaust and water ingress assembly failures" appears in the aggregate but does not match Dr. Delmar's framing here. Proposing a new cluster specific to ATV suspension design. May converge with "Honda ATV design optimization history" — flagged for editor review.

### Nuclear recovery system decking — drilled holes in seismic frame
- **Anchor:** §11
- **canonical_cluster_id:** PROPOSED: "Nuclear recovery decking frame seismic plasticity modification"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Dr. Delmar's consulting case. Contractor drilled holes in main structural frame for electrical conduit; frame was designed to deform in plasticity for seismic loading. Plug-welding the holes would have added residual stress and increased crack risk. Resolved with a welded stiffener placed away from the strengthened zone.
- **Materials/systems:** structural steel tubing, seismic-grade plasticity design
- **Speaker:** Dr. Delmar
- **Note:** not in canonical cluster list; proposed as new cluster.

### Florida tree stand welding failure
- **Anchor:** `§13.p1`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Florida tree stand welding failure"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Tom's marquee interjection. Same teaching point as Dr. Delmar's trailer case (welds in compression vs. tension), but with a fatal outcome — Tom's case down in Florida where a tree stand failed because the weld was placed in tension. The convergence of teaching point between Dr. Delmar (designed for it; survived) and Tom (didn't; killed someone) is the lecture's strongest pedagogical moment.
- **Speaker:** Tom Eagar
- **Era:** unspecified; Tom's consulting work

## Cases referenced only as framing or by name

### Structural welding code (AWS D1.1 implied)
- **Anchor:** `§6.p1`, `§13.p2`
- **Frame:** Tom cites the structural welding code's design allowable stresses (50% of yield for members, 30% of yield for welds) and the 1.67 / 2.0 fatigue safety factors for buildings and bridges respectively.
- **Note:** this is a recurring framing reference Tom uses across many lectures, not a case. Flagged as a *figure* (per the figures-vs-cases distinction proposed in the case index methodology).

### ASME pressure vessel code
- **Anchor:** `§1.p5`, `§6.p3`
- **Frame:** referenced by Dr. Delmar as an example of a code where working stress is built to handle pressure variation without explicit safety-factor adjustment by the designer.
- **Note:** framing reference, not a case.

### Tractor-trailer frame welding prohibition
- **Anchor:** `§13.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "High-strength truck frame welding violations" (probable match)
- **Frame in this lecture:** Tom's trailing final point, cut off when the recording ends — that code prohibits welding on tractor-trailer steel frames, that too many trailers have had to be welded, and that bolts are heavier but the field had to switch to them.
- **Note:** Tom never completes the case; recording cuts. Indexed for cross-reference to other lectures where Tom develops this point.

## Figures referenced (not cases)

- **25 psf wind pressure at 65 mph** (`§5.p2`–`§5.p3`) — Dr. Delmar's rule of thumb for trailer aerodynamic loading.
- **50% of yield = infinite fatigue life** (`§13.p2`) — Tom's standard reference to the S-N curve for steel.
- **1.67 / 2.0 structural welding code safety factors** (`§13.p2`) — Tom's standard reference to AWS D1.1 buildings vs. bridges.
- **90/10 weight rule for trailer loading** (`§3.p4`) — Dr. Delmar references the conventional rule that ninety percent of trailer weight should be on the wheels, ten percent on the tongue.
