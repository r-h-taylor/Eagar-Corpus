# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 9 (Codes and Standards for Safety, Fall 2012, session 9/14). This lecture is unusual in the corpus: it is a guest lecture delivered by Dr. Delmar, with Tom Eagar interjecting twice — once mid-lecture (§6) and once at the end (§13). The editorial register notes where Dr. Delmar's spoken French-influenced English required smoothing that goes beyond the conventions established for Tom's voice.*

## Conventions applied

Standard conventions from the catalog: student attribution, stage directions, numerals, self-corrections smoothed, filler removed, "okay" handled per §6/§7 of the catalog, "you know" / "I mean" cut.

## Special handling: Dr. Delmar's voice

Dr. Delmar speaks fluent but non-native English with characteristic syntactic patterns ("you would want to have it more resistant to buckling, the way I drew it"). Layer 3 smooths these into idiomatic English where the smoothing is unambiguous, and preserves them where the construction is distinctive but readable. This is more aggressive than the smoothing applied to Tom's voice — flagged here because the convention catalog was developed against Tom's speech patterns. The principle "do not add new sentences" is held strictly; only word-level and clause-level smoothing is applied.

Captioner errors specific to Dr. Delmar (e.g., "rack [wrack]," "Steve [SN]," "curb [curve]," "trust [trust]" for "truss," "defenders" for "fenders," "fine element" for "finite element," "disciples" for "cycles") corrected in layer 2 with bracketed alternatives where ambiguous; in layer 3, the corrected reading is used silently when unambiguous. Logged here:

- "rack" / "wrack" — used in compound "to rack"; modern usage allows both; layer 3 reads "rack."
- "trust" → "truss" — clear from context.
- "fine element" → "finite element" — clear from context.
- "Steve" → "S-N" — clear from context (Dr. Delmar is discussing fatigue curves; "Steve" is a phonetic captioner error for "S-N").
- "disciples" → "cycles" — clear from context; Tom said "cycles."
- "curb" → "curve" — clear from context (Tom's interjection at §6.p2 is about driving around a curve).
- "defenders" → "fenders" — clear from context.
- "decking" / "digging" — Dr. Delmar said something between these; "decking" makes structural sense in the context of nuclear recovery deck framing. Layer 3 reads "decking."
- "seal" / "weld" in Tom's §13.p1 — Tom said "weld"; "seal" was a captioner mishearing.
- "two million disciples" → "two million cycles" in §13.p2.

## Sectioning decisions

Thirteen sections is at the upper end of the recommended range for a 54-minute lecture. The high section count reflects the dual-speaker structure (Dr. Delmar's two case studies — trailer and ATV — each break naturally into multiple teaching units, and Tom's two interjections each merit their own section because they shift speaker and tone). The trailer case alone occupies §3–§5; the ATV case occupies §8–§10. Section breaks fall at natural teaching transitions within each case.

## Per-section editorial decisions

### §1 — Continuing the safety factor
- Backward-reference to "first lecture" preserved at p1 because it carries actual content (Dr. Delmar is picking up where he left off, and the reader benefits from knowing this is continuation, not introduction).
- p5: "ASME last week" — backward-reference left in because the ASME framing is the substantive point.

### §2 — Two examples without codes
- Single-paragraph section. Sets up the rest of the lecture. Tempting to fold into §3, but Dr. Delmar's framing here ("today we don't really have a code or standard") is a discrete teaching move that introduces the no-code methodology for the next ~40 minutes of lecture.

### §3 — The trailer
- §3.p1: Added stage direction `*[Dr. Delmar draws the trailer frame on the board.]*` to mark the physical board-drawing moment. Dr. Delmar's "I'm going to draw it" in layer 2 functions as the move; layer 3 records the move as stage direction.
- §3.p4: "uh straightening these two in the middle, um because of possible added force floating this way" — "straightening" is preserved as Dr. Delmar's word, though "strengthening" is what he meant. Reading "strengthening" makes the sentence work; using a soft correction here. Logged.
- §3.p6: Student question marked inaudible; Dr. Delmar's response stands alone, with the question's content inferable from the response.

### §4 — Welds in compression
- §4.p1: Added stage direction `*[Dr. Delmar sketches the joint.]*` for the moment Dr. Delmar draws the connection geometry on the board.
- §4.p4: "ductile weaving [way]" → "ductile way" — clear captioner error; corrected silently in layer 3.

### §5 — Trailer's upper structure
- §5.p1: "cover trailers" → "covered trailers" — minor smoothing.
- §5.p3: "in the middle [mega]" — captioner garbling; layer 3 reads "in the middle."
- §5.p4: Tom Eagar's voice does not yet appear; Dr. Delmar handles the fatigue question himself. The student question is marked inaudible.

### §6 — Tom's first interjection
- Tom's voice enters at p22. The section title flags this as a speaker shift.
- §6.p1: "30 [percent]" — Tom said "thirty" and the captioner rendered as "30"; layer 3 follows the convention of spelling out percent in prose ("thirty percent").
- §6.p2: Tom's "Crash. Okay, no, seriously" preserved — this is Tom's characteristic teaching humor (the trailer/airplane comparison), and cutting it would erase a tonal feature of his voice that the corpus is trying to preserve.
- §6.p3: Dr. Delmar resumes after Tom. The transition is preserved.

### §7 — Practical fabrication
- §7.p1: "In retrospect — in in retrospect" smoothed to single "In retrospect."
- §7.p4: The ladder cautionary tale is the editorially most charged moment in this section. Dr. Delmar's "and it came out to be a very big problem" is preserved as he said it; this is a real case in the corpus (probably the "Ladder with unauthorized welds" canonical cluster).

### §8–§10 — ATV
- §8.p1: Added stage direction `*[Dr. Delmar refers to a diagram.]*` to mark the visual aid moment.
- §10.p1: "around a round number" — Dr. Delmar likely said "around a round member" (talking about welds around a circular tubular member). Layer 3 reads "round member." Soft correction logged.

### §11 — Nuclear-recovery deck holes
- This is Dr. Delmar's own consulting case. The "decking [digging?]" captioner ambiguity is resolved as "decking" per the rationale above.
- §11.p3: "Professor Eagar talked about factor of three here" — backward-reference preserved because it carries content (Dr. Delmar is citing a specific prior point Tom made about stress concentration factors).

### §12 — Closing summary
- Dr. Delmar's three closing paragraphs. The "we don't design things much stronger than you need to be at the very end" sentence smoothed to "than they need to be."

### §13 — Tom's closing
- Tom's voice resumes at p56. The Florida tree-stand case is the marquee teaching moment of this section and is in the canonical case list as "Florida tree stand welding failure."
- §13.p2: Several captioner garbles — "cycle speeds" → "cycles," "50[%] of yield" → "fifty percent of yield," "disciples" → "cycles." All silent corrections in layer 3.
- §13.p3: Tom trails off mid-sentence: "Bolts are heavier, they're not as strong as welds. It takes longer to do — it has to do —" Layer 3 preserves the trailing fragment because the lecture ends here (recording cuts). This is the lecture's actual ending; the convention is to preserve, not smooth, an incomplete final sentence.

## Soft reorderings logged
None. The lecture was already linearly organized by Dr. Delmar around two cases (trailer, then ATV), with Tom's interjections falling at natural breaks. No reordering was needed within any section to make the teaching read clearly on the page.

## Flagged for editor review
- The "Florida tree stand" case in §13.p1 is significant — Tom uses it to make the same teaching point Dr. Delmar made about welds-in-compression. This is one of the few moments where a guest lecturer and Tom land on the same case independently, and the case index entry should note this convergence.
- Dr. Delmar's nuclear-recovery deck case (§11) is not in the canonical cluster list. Proposed as a new cluster.
- The student questions at §3.p6 and §5.p4 are inaudible; attribution is unambiguous (questions came from the class), but the content of each question has been inferred from Dr. Delmar's response and should be reviewed against the original audio if a richer rendering is wanted.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
