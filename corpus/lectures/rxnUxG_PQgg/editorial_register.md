## Co-lecturer turn labeling

This lecture has three speakers. Convention applied:
- Tom's speech is unlabeled (default voice).
- When Belmar or Lyons speak in extended turns, paragraphs are prefixed `**Simone Belmar:**` or `**Steve Lyons:**`.
- When Tom resumes after a co-lecturer turn, his first paragraph is prefixed `**Tom:**` for clarity.
- Student turns labeled `**Student:**` per convention.

## Convention catalog applied

All conventions from the §1 worked example applied. Notable applications in this lecture:

- **Stage directions:** none in this lecture (administrative session, no physical-object teaching moves).
- **Backward-references:** Tom's "we talked about that" and "as I said" instances are rare in a first-day lecture; where present, cut.
- **Numerals:** $4,000, $100,000, $200,000, $500,000, $1 and $2 million, six-hundred-billion-dollar (compound adjective), 10% (technical figure preserved as digit-percent in the SBIR context).
- **Bracketed spoken slips:** "Hesus [Jesús] del Alamo" — Tom mispronounces the name and the bracket records the correction.
- **Captioner errors silently fixed:** "eager-tot.mit.edu" → "eagar-tqi.mit.edu" (Tom's website name; captioner garbled both halves).

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
