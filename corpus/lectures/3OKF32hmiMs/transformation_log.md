# Transformation Log: Layer 1 → Layer 2

*Lecture 2019-09-26 (Additive Manufacturing, guest lecture by Alex Stephens of Digital Alloys, with Tom Eagar moderating). Mechanical rules applied to the raw transcript to produce the cleaned-up middle layer. This log records every change so layer 2 is reproducible from layer 1.*

## Global rules applied

1. **Line-broken delivery joined.** Auto-caption line breaks merged into continuous sentences. Sentence boundaries inserted from audible pauses and grammatical completion.

2. **Speaker attribution.** This lecture has three voice categories: Tom (introducing, moderating, interjecting), Alex (the primary presenter), and unattributed students (asking questions). Speaker turns labeled `**Tom:**`, `**Alex:**`, `**Student:**` at paragraph start. Where student question audio is not captured by the captioner (frequent — the camera mic favors the presenter), the question is reconstructed in brackets from Alex's response, flagged as `[Question about X — inaudible on captions.]`.

3. **Captioner errors corrected silently** for proper nouns and common-word misrecognitions where unambiguous. Listed below per paragraph.

4. **Tom's spoken slips bracketed** on first occurrence per section. None occur in this lecture (Tom's interjections are short and the slips Tom is known for — Forward/Ford — do not surface here).

5. **Paragraph breaks at natural pauses.** Sixty paragraphs. Heavier paragraph density than a typical Tom solo lecture because of the Q&A interleaving — each speaker turn boundary typically starts a new paragraph.

6. **Preserved as-is:** filler words, false starts, self-corrections, repetitions, pivot mid-sentence. Standard Layer 2 convention.
