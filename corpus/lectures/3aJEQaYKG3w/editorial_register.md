# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 2018-03-06, guest lecture by Brian Holman on critical airplane engine components and the United Airlines Flight 232 (Sioux City) case study.*

## Special note: this lecture is not in Tom Eagar's voice

This is a guest lecture by Brian Holman. The standard Eagar editorial register conventions apply (filler removal, self-correction smoothing, student attribution, stage directions), but the voice on the page is Holman's, not Tom's. Two specific points:

1. **No bracketed name-slip corrections.** The convention for `Ford [Forward]` etc. is calibrated to Tom's known recurring slips. We do not have a calibrated catalog of Holman's slips, so we do not introduce any.
2. **Backward-references to Tom's previous lectures preserved.** Where Holman says "Professor Eagar, I think in one of the previous lectures, showed one where you can open it up," that reference is preserved — it's content (an attribution and an actual prior teaching reference), not filler.

## Conventions applied

- **Student attribution.** Three student questions appear (§8.p4, §10.p8, §10.p10, §11.p3, §11.p8). All are inaudible on the recording; layer 2 marks them `*[question, inaudible]*`. Layer 3 preserves them as `**Student:** *[question, inaudible]*` so the reader knows a question was asked and Holman is responding.
- **Stage directions.** §11.p5: layer 2 has "A couple of these that I'll pass out" mid-paragraph. Layer 3 renders this as an italicized stage direction `*[Brian passes samples around the class.]*`, consistent with the convention established for Tom's physical-object teaching moves.
- **Numerals.** Monetary values rendered as words where rhetorical ("five thousand dollars", "fifteen million dollars", "fifty-five million dollar loss"); years as digits ("1989", "1999", "2009"); rpm and engineering units kept as digits ("20,000 rpm", "40 atmospheres", "2500 Fahrenheit"); percent values kept as digits with percent sign in technical contexts ("40%", "60%", "7%") and spelled out where rhetorical ("over eighty percent", "twenty to twenty-five percent", "ten to twenty percent"). This is consistent with convention §3.
- **Filler removed.** "You know", "I mean", "I guess", "basically", "right?" cut throughout where they carry no semantic load. Preserved where they invoke shared context or close a thought.
- **Self-corrections smoothed.** §11.p1 "off various fatigue cycles, they will grow and then meet up" — Holman's mid-clause grammatical stumble; layer 3 reads as one clean clause.

## Sectioning

13 sections for a ~53-minute lecture. The structure is unusually well-marked by Holman's own slide transitions, so section breaks fall at slide-change points: introduction → engine architecture → critical parts/LLPs → microstructure/manufacturing → failure modes/discs → titanium alloys → nickel-base segregation and inclusion types → in-service damage → NDE methods → UA 232 accident and disc recovery → fracture analysis → NTSB findings → cost-benefit. Sections are 2–6 minutes each, on the short end of the target range, reflecting the slide-deck pacing.

## Decisions NOT permitted at layer 3 (constitutional constraint)

- Adding new sentences in the speaker's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases the speaker kept separate, or splitting cases they handled as one.
- Removing factual errors the speaker made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; speaker slips are bracketed; genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted only when they would actively mislead a reader.)

This constraint is the project's safeguard against drift into reconstruction.
