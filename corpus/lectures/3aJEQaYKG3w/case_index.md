# Case Index — 2018-03-06 (Brian Holman guest lecture)

*Citation format: layer 3 paragraph anchor (e.g. `§11.p2`). This lecture is built around a single major case study (United Airlines Flight 232) with several supporting cases referenced in passing.*

## Primary case study

### United Airlines Flight 232
- **Anchors:** §10.p1–§10.p11, §11.p1–§11.p9, §12.p1–§12.p3, §13.p4
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "United Airlines Flight 232"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Centerpiece case study for the lecture. CF6-6 engine stage-one fan rotor failure caused by hard alpha inclusion in the titanium disc, originating in the original ingot. Crack initiated on the virgin flight, grew over many cycles, missed by both ultrasonic and macro-etch inspections at GE manufacturing, and again by fluorescent penetrant inspection 760 cycles before failure (where discoloration around the inclusion was noted but the part was returned to service). Catastrophic disc separation severed all three hydraulic systems; pilot flew the plane manually to Sioux City. 111 fatalities, 47 serious injuries. Disc recovered three months later by a farmer. Case drove FAA process changes in quality assurance and NDE protocols.
- **Materials/systems:** Ti-6Al-4V titanium fan disc, hard alpha inclusion (Type 1 high-interstitial defect)
- **Era:** 1989

### Hard alpha inclusion in forged disc — non-destructive testing case study
- **Anchors:** §7.p3, §11.p2, §12.p1–§12.p3
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Hard alpha inclusion in forged disc — non-destructive testing case study"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Treated as the metallurgical/manufacturing root cause of UA 232. Holman uses it to introduce the Type 1 titanium defect taxonomy in §7.p3, then returns to it in §11–§12 as the specific finding of the NTSB report. The macro-etch timing error (performed upstream of the critical machining operations rather than after) is identified as the procedural failure that allowed the inclusion to escape inspection.
- **Materials/systems:** Ti-6Al-4V, alpha-phase nitrogen/oxygen/carbon concentration
- **Era:** 1989

- **Anchor:** §13.p2, §13.p4
