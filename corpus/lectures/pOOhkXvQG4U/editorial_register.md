## Conventions applied

1. Student turns labeled `**Student:**`. Where the captioner has not transcribed the student's words (§3.p3), the turn is marked `[unintelligible]` and flagged here.
2. No stage directions needed in this section — Tom's physical objects are slides (the filler-metal chart, the Alcoa data sheet), referenced verbally rather than displayed to the camera.
3. Numerals: temperatures and ksi values kept as digits; rhetorical fractions ("80, 90 percent") preserved as Tom said them.
4. Self-corrections smoothed to the corrected form; preserved verbatim in layer 2.
5. Backward-references to prior session cut (§4.p1: "I don't remember if I talked to you about this").
6. Forward-reference to Friday's lecture (§3.p1, §3.p5) preserved — this is substantive teaching content (Tom is setting up the next session), not a class-management aside.
7. Tom's "okay" preserved where it closes a rhetorical thought; cut where mid-clause filler.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
