# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 6 (Fall 2014). This register records every editorial judgment made in lifting the cleanup edit to the readable edition.*

## Sectioning

Eleven sections. The lecture is structurally a Q&A opener (~12 minutes on materials economics and university productivity), a coal aside, then four interlocking case-blocks on magnesium and corrosion (magnesium anodes → magnesium Achilles heels with the Alcoa-Dow cartel → aluminum/chloride → Aloha Airlines fatigue), a transition to voltage and bond energy via fuel cells, and a final arc on conveying water that goes lead → terne → galvanized → copper → PEX, with the Eagle Scout galvanized-handrail case treated as a freestanding section because it's an extended forensic-and-personal narrative interrupting the materials sequence.

Section breaks fall at natural transitions: a new student question (s1→s3, s5→s7), a self-announced aside ("This was not what I intended to lecture on today" — but the magnesium-cartel block stays in s5 because Tom kept teaching the magnesium thread), a new physical demonstration (s4 with the magnesium bar, s11 with the PEX), and Tom's own marker "There are limits to the properties of materials, and it has to do with the strength of the bonds. In the case of a fuel cell or a battery..." which closes s8.

## Per-section decisions

### §1 — Energy content and the productivity revolution
- **§1.p1 / §1.p2 reorder.** Layer 2 opens with Tom's answer "Today, it's ninety-five percent energy" *before* the student's question is captioned. The captioner placed the student's question in the middle of p2. Layer 3 surfaces the student's question first (§1.p1) and Tom's quick answer second (§1.p2), which is the natural reading order. The student-question text is taken verbatim from layer 2 p2. This is a soft reordering within section.
- **Cut throughout:** "okay" as mid-clause filler; "you know" as discourse filler; backward-references to "I mentioned that" and "we talked about that" (§1.p4, §1.p8, §1.p11) removed where they don't carry content.
- **Numerals:** percentages spelled out ("ninety-five percent", "fifty percent", "five percent"); decades and years as digits.

## Conventions applied (per the catalog)

1. Student attribution: `**Student:**` label.
2. Stage directions: italicized brackets for `*[Tom holds up the magnesium bar.]*`, `*[Tom produces a book.]*`, `*[Tom holds up a sample of PEX.]*`, `*[Tom checks the sample.]*`.
3. Numerals: monetary values as `$5,000`/`$9,000`; rhetorical/percentage numbers spelled out where Tom is in rhetorical mode.
4. Self-corrections smoothed to the corrected form.
5. Backward-references to prior sessions cut from layer 3 except where they carry content.
6. "Okay" preserved as thought-closer, cut as mid-clause filler.
7. "You know" / "I mean" cut except where they invoke shared classroom context.
8. Bracketed corrections per convention 9: `Hawaiian Airlines [Aloha Airlines]` and `Johnson [Ethicon, J&J]`.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
