# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 7 (Solid State Welding, Spring 2013). This register records editorial judgments made in lifting layer 2 to the readable edition.*

## Sectioning

Nine sections were identified from teaching transitions:

- §1 — Tom's wrap-up of last session's surface-energy chart, leading to the Ford PEO bumper case as illustration of low-surface-energy bonding.
- §2 — Production of the Farberware Millennium pot, prop-driven teaching of cold bonding and the Brooklyn-to-Japan hydrogen cracking case.
- §3 — Pivot to the kinetics taxonomy (solvent removal, polymerization, vaporization, solidification). Tom explicitly says "let's finish up."
- §4 — Pivot to automotive case as application of adhesive kinetics. Tom moves from water-based solvents into the historical narrative (1973 oil embargo onward).
- §5 — Continuation through the price spectrum of adhesives (fish gurry to aerospace), and the Lord Corporation digression.
- §6 — Pivot to joint design: surface roughness, area increase, scarf/step/strap joints. Tom moves from materials to geometry.
- §7 — Tom's signposted return: "Now let me tell you the trick of how to bond to Teflon."
- §8 — Pivot to phosphated steel and cold heading. Distinct prop (the zinc-plated fastener).
- §9 — Pivot to diffusion bonding, signaled by "Let's go on to diffusion bonding." Closes the lecture.

## Convention applications

- Stage directions used at §2.p1 (Millennium cookware) and §8.p1 (fastener).
- Bracketed slips: §2.p4 (helium→hydrogen, visible self-correction), §9.p3 (F-20→F-21).
- Numerals: monetary values as `$100`, `$200`, `$400`, `$15`; years as digits (`1973`, `1985`, `1990s`); rhetorical numbers spelled out (`fifteen or twenty years ago`, `thirty years ago`, `three-eighths of an inch`).
- Self-corrections smoothed throughout per convention; layer 2 preserves originals.
- "Okay" preserved where it closes a thought; cut as mid-clause filler.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could do this because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases that Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
