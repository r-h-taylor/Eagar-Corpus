# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 1 (Spring 2012, Deformation Processing). This register records editorial judgments made in lifting the cleanup edit to the readable edition.*

## Sectioning

The lecture is divided into 9 sections. Section breaks fall at natural teaching transitions:

- §1 → §2 at the pivot from "how I teach" (problem sets, hiring) to broader critique of MIT culture (math obsession, self-esteem, leadership vs. management).
- §2 → §3 at the pivot back to course mechanics ("What is this course about?").
- §3 → §4 at the introduction of the NRC Defense Materials report handout.
- §4 → §5 at the pivot to deformation processing and Backofen's book.
- §5 → §6 at the return to schedule and module logistics.
- §6 → §7 at the introduction of the part-selection assignment ("you must tell us whether you're going to emphasize…").
- §7 → §8 at the pivot to life assessment via the hammer (a new physical-object demonstration).
- §8 → §9 at the closing pivot to fracture mechanics and imperfection-controlled strength.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3 (constitutional constraint)

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)

This constraint is the project's safeguard against drift into reconstruction.
