## Conventions applied

- Student turns: there are no clear Student turns in this lecture (the questions Tom poses to the class are mostly rhetorical and he answers them himself). No `**Student:**` labels needed.
- Stage directions: two physical-object teaching moves preserved as italicized brackets — Tom locating the superplasticity sample part (§3.p5) and Tom showing the FCC/BCC crystal models (§9.p5 and §9.p6).
- Numerals: years as digits; dollar amounts as `$6 million`, `$30 million`, `$300 a kilogram`; small counts and rhetorical numbers spelled out ("ninety-eight percent" → kept as "98%" since it's a technical/datum context, per convention §3); decade references like "the early '60s" with apostrophe; weights kept as `28 kg`, `154 kg`, `126 kg`, `45 kg`, `50 kg`.
- Self-corrections: smoothed throughout. Examples in per-paragraph notes below.
- Filler ("I mean", "you know", "uh") cut except where invoking shared context.
- "Okay" handled per convention §6.
