# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 8 (3.371 Fall 2016). This register records the editorial judgments made in lifting the cleanup edit to the readable edition.*

## Sectioning rationale

Ten sections, each marking a natural teaching pivot:

- **s1** (00:00) — opens with course logistics and quickly reviews the property-limits framing from the previous session. Ends as Tom moves from melting points to modulus.
- **s2** (02:33) — stiffness via geometry (submarine propeller shaft), introduction of Charpy testing as the alternative to force-of-fracture measurement. Ends with Tom holding up his Charpy bar halves.
- **s3** (09:40) — the historical pivot: Liberty ships and the brittle-fracture problem that motivated fracture mechanics.
- **s4** (13:59) — Griffith, Irwin, Wells, and the formal apparatus. Ends with the Nova Scotia slug-welded yacht trailer anecdote, which closes the Liberty-ships-as-slugged-welds idea.
- **s5** (19:22) — modes of fracture and the Egyptian obelisk specimen (Wendell Wilkening's thesis).
- **s6** (22:38) — Charpy energy specifications, safety-factor inflation, Quincy LNG tanker case.
- **s7** (27:42) — ratio analysis diagrams; North Andover pressure vessel as worked example of critical flaw size.
- **s8** (32:03) — student question pivots to rail steel; Northridge earthquake; the lag between knowledge and code.
- **s9** (34:38) — aluminum ratio diagram, Brazilian helicopter rotor case, titanium for submarines, silicon boule demonstration.
- **s10** (40:46) — class summary across material families (metals/ceramics/polymers); closing logistics.

## Decisions NOT permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
