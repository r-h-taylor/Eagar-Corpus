# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 1s_KEnbkpmQ (3.371 Welding Metallurgy, Summer 2014, session 34/35). This register records editorial judgments made in lifting the cleanup edit to the readable edition.*

## Sectioning

The lecture divides naturally into eight sections at clear teaching transitions:

- **§1** (00:01): Student question opens the session on friction stir welding history.
- **§2** (03:56): Pivot to titanium microstructure ("So far as titanium…").
- **§3** (06:50): Pivot to shielding box requirements and acceptable colors.
- **§4** (11:23): Pivot to semiconductor stainless steel piping and the Alpha submarine shipyard story.
- **§5** (15:07): Pivot to first major consulting case — Engelhard / Boeing 747-400.
- **§6** (22:28): Pivot to second consulting case set — GE Lynn spot welding.
- **§7** (28:12): Pivot to GE Lynn electron beam titanium welding (continuation of GE Lynn but a different welding process and a different visit).
- **§8** (33:41): Final case — Alcoa Davenport plate thickness, framed by Tom as "one more story that goes along with that."

The §6/§7 split was a judgment call: both occur at GE Lynn during overlapping consulting engagements, and Tom narrates them as a continuous arc. They are kept separate because they treat different welding processes (resistance spot welding vs. electron beam) and were physically different visits to the facility; Tom himself marks the transition at p30 with "So this is a similar type of problem."

## Convention catalog application

- **Numerals:** "ten thousandths of an inch" preserved spelled out (rhetorical); "$15,000," "$25,000," "$70,000," "$150,000" rendered with digits and dollar signs. "Boeing 747-400" rendered with digits. "10,000 amps," "3,000 spot welds," "2,000 good ones" rendered with digits.
- **Self-corrections:** smoothed throughout per Convention 4.
- **Backward-references:** "this is, um, deep TIG I talked about" preserved (carries content — points to a specific prior teaching unit).
- **"Okay" handling:** preserved where it closes a thought rhetorically (§5.p7, §6.p5, §7.p6); cut where it's mid-clause filler.
- **"You know" / "I mean":** cut except where invoking shared context.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
