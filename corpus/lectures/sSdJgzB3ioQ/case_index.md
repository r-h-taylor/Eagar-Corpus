# Case Index — Lecture 18, Welding Metallurgy Spring 2014

*Citation format: layer 3 paragraph anchor (e.g. `§3.p1`). Click-through reveals corresponding layer 2 passage with timestamp range.*

This lecture is dominated by two extended forensic cases — the Wyman Gordon forging press I-beam crack (§3, §4) and the North Andover hot isostatic press explosion (§6) — bracketed by stress-relief teaching content. Several smaller cases surface as asides.

## Major cases

### USS Battleship Iowa turret repair (Puerto Rico)
- **Anchor:** `§2.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "USS Battleship turret hydrogen cracking repair (Puerto Rico, ~1980)"
- **Frame in this section:** Used to illustrate that the U.S. has lost the technology to weld 14-inch armor plate. Tom uses the case as the closing of the "no one remembers how to do it" argument.
- **Materials/systems:** 14-inch armor steel, 16-inch shells, large-section weld repair.
- **Era:** Late 1970s–1980s.

### Wyman Gordon 50,000-ton forging press I-beam fatigue crack
- **Anchors:** `§3.p1` through `§3.p9`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "50,000-ton Wyman-Gordon forging press structural crack"
- **Frame in this section:** Extended teaching case on weld residual stress, fatigue crack initiation at the toe of a fillet weld, and the economics of repair vs. replacement. Tom personally consulted on the repair.
- **Materials/systems:** Welded I-beam (10 feet × 10 inches, ~50-ton beam), two-inch fillet welds, 1950 fabrication, mid-1980s fatigue failure.
- **Era:** Press built 1950s; failure mid-1985; Tom's involvement mid-1980s.
- **Related figures:** $2B replacement cost estimate; tens of millions for repair; 30-year service life before crack.

### Wyman Gordon / Krupp crankshaft exit and GARA legislation
- **Anchors:** `§4.p1`, `§4.p2`, `§4.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Wyman Gordon crankshaft product liability risk"
- **Frame in this section:** Tom uses Krupp's decision to exit the small-aircraft crankshaft business to teach about product liability in low-volume safety-critical manufacturing. The Kentucky replacement supplier's recall is the punchline.
- **Materials/systems:** Steel crankshafts, aluminum aircraft engine cases, Teledyne Continental and Lycoming engines.
- **Era:** Early 1990s. GARA passed 1994.

### North Andover hot isostatic press explosion
- **Anchors:** `§6.p1` through `§6.p10`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Hot isostatic pressing vessel explosion" (also overlaps with "North Andover hot isostatic press explosion" and "IMT hot isostatic press vessel failure")
- **Frame in this section:** Extended teaching case combining stress corrosion cracking diagnosis, fracture mechanics (K1c, critical flaw size, leak-before-break design philosophy), Japan Steel Works forging, heat treatment QC failure, and Harley-Davidson cylinder HIP processing context.
- **Materials/systems:** 300-ton forged steel pressure vessel (60-inch ID, ~20 ft tall), 20,000 psi argon working pressure, Harley-Davidson aluminum cylinder HIP load, Pratt & Whitney/GE turbine disc HIP load.
- **Era:** Vessel designed mid-1980s on Cray supercomputer; explosion ~1990s (Tom: "20 years ago now" as of 2014).
- **Note:** Three aggregate-v2 cluster names appear to refer to this same case or closely related events: "Hot isostatic pressing vessel explosion," "North Andover hot isostatic press explosion," and "IMT hot isostatic press vessel failure." Recommend reconciliation at canon level.

## Figures referenced
- **30-year fatigue life** on the forging-press I-beam (§3.p4): the time-to-crack-initiation that Tom uses to anchor the "you can't bolt a small detail onto a big weldment and walk away" lesson.

- **20,000 psi argon working pressure** in HIP vessel (§6.p9): the stored energy that made the explosion catastrophic.
