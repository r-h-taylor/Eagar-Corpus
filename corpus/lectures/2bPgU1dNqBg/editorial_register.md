# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 2bPgU1dNqBg (3.371 Spring 2016, session 2/12). This register records editorial judgments made in lifting the cleanup edit to the readable edition.*

## Conventions applied

All conventions from the §1 worked example carry forward:
- Student turns labeled `**Student:**`.
- Physical-object teaching moves rendered as italicized stage directions.
- Monetary values as numerals (`$300`, `$500`); rhetorical numbers spelled out.
- Self-corrections smoothed to corrected form.
- Backward-references to prior sessions cut where they carry no content.
- "Okay," "you know," "I mean" cut as filler; preserved where they invoke shared context or close a thought.
- Captioner garbling silently corrected where unambiguous (Tom's spoken slips bracketed on first occurrence).

## Sectioning rationale

Ten sections for a ~54-minute lecture. The lecture has a natural two-part structure: opening logistics (s1), then the *Technology Review* question framing the rest (s2). The body proceeds in clear teaching transitions:
- **s3** introduces the origin story (stereolithography, Cima-Sachs).
- **s4** narrows to metal attempts (PCBs, Tom's Navy work).
- **s5** pivots into the five-whys analytical framework (and detours through Liberty ships).
- **s6, s7, s8** are the three physics-based reasons metals resist 3D printing — mechanical, surface, heat transfer — each its own section because Tom develops each at length.
- **s9, s10** close with the Navy particle-beam pivot and the 500-lb/hr proposal, separated because the pivot setup (peace breaks out, spares problem) and the technical proposal (electron beam, Grüneisen) are distinct teaching moves.
