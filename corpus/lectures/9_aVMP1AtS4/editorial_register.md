# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 13 (final session, 3.371 Fall 2014). This register records editorial judgments made in lifting the cleanup edit to the readable edition. The lecture is structured as a survey march across the periodic table; sectioning follows that march.*

## Sectioning rationale

The lecture has a strong natural structure: a brief opener on Alcoa (continuation from previous session), course schedule, then a periodic-table walk-through. Twelve sections were chosen to follow Tom's element-by-element pacing. Each section corresponds to either (a) a specific element or group Tom treats as a teaching unit, or (b) a thematic interlude (MMPDS handbook, wildfires/rate base). Sections range from ~1.5 minutes (§7 stent) to ~5 minutes (§4 MMPDS, §9 zinc/beryllium).

## Per-section decisions

- Filler cut: "you know" (multiple), "I mean," "uh."

- §2.p1 is the housekeeping interlude. Preserved largely intact because it sets the temporal frame (Tom rearranging the schedule because of his Houston trip — a poignant detail given context).

- "lithiated glass and substitute it with potassium" — Tom's spoken sequence reads ambiguously (substitute *what* with *what*?). Layer 3 clarifies: "substitute lithium with potassium." Editorial inference from context (he's listing the substitution chain Na→K→Li→K).

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
