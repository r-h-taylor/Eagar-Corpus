# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 10, Solid State Welding (Spring 2013). This register records editorial judgments made in lifting the cleanup edit to the readable edition.*

## Sectioning

The lecture divides into nine sections at natural teaching transitions:

- §1 (00:06) opens with thermal expansion review — Tom signals "last time" and walks through the table.
- §2 (04:03) pivots to power electronics packaging with the first stage-direction sequence (Tom produces transistors and a diode).
- §3 (09:21) is Tom's transition out of show-and-tell into the wetting/surface-energy theory. Opens with "any questions?" closing the prior unit.
- §4 (13:28) opens "I'm going to talk a little bit about the requirements of a flux" — explicit new teaching unit, with three failure-mode examples (oxalic acid, glucose, abietic acid).
- §5 (20:23) opens "If you add just the least little bit of hydrochloric acid" — pivot to rosin fluxes, includes the long rosin-baked-potato aside.
- §6 (24:28) opens with the handout reference and the solderability table — distinct unit, ends with student Q on bad-soldering bonds.
- §7 (28:12) opens "Some things are not solderable" — pivot to reaction fluxes, leads into chloride corrosion mechanism.
- §8 (37:28) opens "Let me show you another kind of thing" — pivot to lead-free solders.
- §9 (45:01) opens "Some of this is just catching up" — repair-cost economics, leading to Motorola pager and Ford Mustang stories. Closes the lecture.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3 (constitutional constraint)

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
