# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture OszzaQpMV50 (Welding Metallurgy, Summer 2015, 10/18). This register records the editorial judgments made in lifting the cleanup edit to the readable edition.*

## Sectioning

The lecture covers five distinct teaching units in the first ~21 minutes of audio captured by this layer 2 segment:

- **§1** (00:03–06:25): Tom's catalog-of-standards aside. Codes/standards as an industry.
- **§2** (06:25–09:23): Resumption of stainless steel content from prior lecture. Schaeffler diagram, AL-6.
- **§3** (09:23–12:17): The Egyptian Abrams tank dissimilar-metal welding case.
- **§4** (12:17–18:27): The Farberware pot strain-induced martensite case.
- **§5** (18:27–end): Travel narrative — the trip to Brooklyn, and a second New York structural-beam case Tom begins but does not finish in this segment.

Section breaks fall at natural pivots: §1→§2 is Tom's explicit "I'm just going to catch up on a few things"; §2→§3 is the move from diagram to specific case ("My example of that"); §3→§4 is the explicit "Another story on stainless steel"; §4→§5 is the travel anecdote opening "So you show up in Brooklyn."

## Per-section decisions

### §1 — Codes and standards

- **§1.p1:** Captioner noise at the head ("It will use technology and they be quick, running a lot of their own standards") cleaned to "Commercial standards bodies write their own technology and run their own standards." This is the unambiguous reading from context.
- **§1.p1:** Stage direction added — Tom is clearly holding up the ASTM volume he describes ("This is the book on ships and marine technology"). The physical-object move is preserved.
- **§1.p3:** Stage direction added for the second standards volume Tom shows.
- **§1.p4:** Tom says "all sixty standards referenced by AWS in the structural welding code." Captioner had "AWS [ASTM]" garbling; corrected silently to AWS (the AWS reference is correct in context — these are AWS-compiled standards).
- **§1.p5:** Captioner garbling around AWS finances cleaned: "back then were about two or three million dollars" preserved; the "this side of the year certifying welders" garbling reduced to "They're making money certifying welders" — unambiguous from context.
- **§1.p6:** "okay" preserved at end of paragraph as the closing-thought discourse marker per convention.

### §2 — Stainless steel and Schaeffler diagram

- **§2.p1:** Captioner garbling "the L versions and Elbert ought help" cleaned to "the L versions help" — Tom is referencing low-carbon L grades; the "Elbert" appears to be captioner misrecognition with no semantic content.
- **§2.p1:** "All stainless steel submarine" preserved as Tom's framing; this matches the canonical cluster *US Navy all-stainless steel submarine program (AL-6X alloy)*.
- **§2.p2:** Year reference "back in nineteen seventy-eight, seventy-nine, whatever it was" smoothed to "back in 1978 or 1979."

### §3 — Egyptian Abrams tanks

- **§3.p1:** Tom's spoken slip "[HY-80?]" was already bracketed in layer 2. In layer 3 it reads "HY-80" without brackets — this is the unambiguous reading and the case is canonical.
- **§3.p1:** Captioner garbling "the Boston extends to welcome" cleaned to "with austenitic stainless steel" — this is unambiguous from immediate context (Tom has just said they used austenitic filler).
- **§3.p1:** Aside "Chrysler, the neighbor of stamps, and I love" — appears to be captioner garbling around Tom's parenthetical about Chrysler. Cut, as the corrected reading is not unambiguous; preserved in layer 2.
- **§3.p3:** Tom's punchline collapsed: layer 2 has a fragmented Q&A-with-self ("Tom, defective ones, right?... yeah, paint over little, polly fill, yeah, get rid of them"). Layer 3 trims to the essential summary: "The Egyptians were unhappy, the State Department was embarrassed." **Soft reordering decision** — Tom's self-quoted dialogue is too garbled in the captioner output to reconstruct faithfully. Cutting it forfeits some of his style but preserves accuracy. Flagged for editor review.

### §4 — Farberware pot

- **§4.p1:** Stage direction added for the pot. Tom clearly produces the physical pot ("they brought this before and I kind of show you").
- **§4.p2:** "garden-variety" preserved (Tom's recurring phrase for 304 stainless).
- **§4.p3:** Captioner garbling "ferrover, ferritic stainless steel" cleaned — Tom is talking about 304 austenitic stainless and strain-induced martensite, not ferritic. The "ferritic" appears to be a captioner misrecognition or Tom's own slip; the technical content that follows is unambiguously about austenite→martensite transformation in 304. Cleaned silently.
- **§4.p4:** Self-correction "Are you well, the only thing causes really crappy in steels" smoothed to "The only thing that causes really crappy delayed cracking in steels."
- **§4.p5:** Captioner garbling "I can run my feeble [finger?] through a candle" — the bracketed correction from layer 2 is resolved to "finger" in layer 3 (unambiguous in context). "I've done it through a welding arc" preserved as Tom's recurring teaching anecdote.
- **§4.p6:** "My rate is probably 150" → "$150." Numeral convention.

### §5 — Brooklyn trip and George Washington Bridge beam

- **§5.p1:** Cabdriver dialogue cleaned: "go away, okay, let's go around" → "I'm going, okay, let's go." The garbled version in layer 2 is preserved; the layer 3 reading is the unambiguous gist.
- **§5.p2:** This section is incomplete in the source — Tom breaks off mid-sentence at "I go and I knock —". Layer 3 preserves the em-dash break to indicate the segment ends mid-narrative. The case will presumably resume in a later layer 2 segment of this same lecture.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3 (constitutional constraint)

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
