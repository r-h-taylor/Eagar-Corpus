### High-temperature superconductors hype and failure to solve power transmission
- **Anchor:** `§3.p1`–`§3.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** High-temperature superconductors hype and failure to solve power transmission
- **Frame in this lecture:** Extended treatment. The MIT materials department's four tenured superconductivity professors refused to join the bandwagon; only the untenured one did. ### High-temperature superconductor critical current measurement error
- **Anchor:** `§3.p4`–`§3.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** High-temperature superconductor critical current measurement error
- **Frame in this lecture:** *Science* magazine papers reporting critical currents two orders of magnitude above Tom's thesis values — measured with conductor parallel to the magnetic field, which is physically meaningless (J×B = 0). The peer reviewers didn't catch it. "One of the problems when there's lots of money, you bring out all the people who don't know what they're doing."

### Aluminum silicon carbide brake calipers
- **Anchor:** `§4.p4`–`§4.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** Aluminum silicon carbide brake calipers
- **Frame in this lecture:** Mid-80s automotive industry attempt to replace cast iron brake calipers with Al-SiC metal matrix composite. Star Wars era. Failed initially because aluminum creeps and the calipers sprung open after two or three hard brake applications — brake fade. Tom has heard the problem has since been solved.
- **Materials/systems:** Cast iron baseline; aluminum / silicon carbide MMC; NVH (noise, vibration, harshness).
- **Era:** Mid-1980s.

### NASA X-33 liquid hydrogen tank failure
- **Anchor:** `§5.p1`–`§5.p8`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** NASA X-33 liquid hydrogen tank failure
- **Frame in this lecture:** Extended teaching unit with physical artifact. $1.3 billion program, cancelled. Rapid prototyping forced substitution of 3M adhesive + Nomex honeycomb + graphite tape for the originally specified 3D-woven composites. Adhesive shelf life trap: data sheet said 10 days out of refrigeration; actual strength after 10 days was 10% of new. Delaminations dropped safety factor from 2 to 1.05. Liquid hydrogen got into the pores and exploded during the Huntsville test.
- **Materials/systems:** Nomex honeycomb, graphite fiber laminate, 3M adhesive, Inconel struts, aluminum oxygen tank.
- **Era:** Early-to-mid 1990s; cancelled before launch.

### Space Shuttle cost overrun
- **Anchor:** `§5.p1`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** Space Shuttle cost overrun
- **Frame in this lecture:** Background to the X-33 project. The shuttle was sold to the public on a $20,000/lb → $1,000/lb cost reduction promise, which it never met, and was secretly justified to Congress by the Defense Department's space-laser-weapon cargo-bay size requirement.

### Air Force Mach 17 air-breathing aircraft program
- **Anchor:** `§7.p1`, `§7.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** Air Force Mach 17 air-breathing aircraft program
- **Frame in this lecture:** Air Force propulsion committee, ~2003–2005. Wanted Mach 17 in air to deliver ordnance on Osama bin Laden within 15 minutes from a Gulf aircraft carrier. Skin frictional heating at 6–7,000 Kelvin leaves only three or four candidate materials (carbon, hafnium carbide).

### National Aerospace Plane
- **Anchor:** `§7.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** National Aerospace Plane
- **Frame in this lecture:** Mid-80s proposal: copper skin 1–2 mm thick, liquid hydrogen on one side at 20 K, plasma on the other at 4–5,000 K. "As long as you didn't have a leak, everything was fine. If you ever had a leak, you have a big bomb."

### Military ultra-long-range aircraft requirement
- **Anchor:** `§7.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** Military ultra-long-range aircraft requirement
- **Frame in this lecture:** Air Force wanted a 25,000-mile-unrefueled aircraft because they anticipated losing all overseas air bases (Philippines, Okinawa, Diego Garcia). Tom's response: the earth's circumference is 25,000 miles, so 12,000 miles is the maximum useful range; he had just flown Dallas-Sydney on an A380 (17 hours, ~12,000 miles) to make the point.

### V-22 Osprey Ship Four crash and rigged court of inquiry
- **Anchor:** `§6.p1`, `§6.p3`–`§6.p4`

- **Frame in this lecture:** Boeing Vertol Philadelphia. Ship 2 was hovering 10–12 feet up; oscillated and flipped on its back because two control wires had been miswired (right and left reversed). Test pilot walked away. "Sort of an embarrassment."

### Langley helicopter supersonic blade tip experiment
- **Anchor:** `§6.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** Langley helicopter supersonic blade tip experiment
- **Frame in this lecture:** NASA Langley pushed a test helicopter past sonic tip speed for a few seconds. The general lesson: helicopters are noise-and-vibration-limited at ~200 mph because the advancing rotor tip approaches Mach 1.

### BMW i3 carbon fiber automobile design
