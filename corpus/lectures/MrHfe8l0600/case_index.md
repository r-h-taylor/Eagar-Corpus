### Neodymium-iron-boron magnet thermal failure in electric vehicles
- **Anchor:** `§5.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** *Neodymium-iron-boron magnet thermal failure in electric vehicles* (matched from canon)
- **Frame in this section:** Used to motivate dysprosium as a topic. Tom owns two all-electric vehicles (purchased June 2017). NdFeB magnets fail near 140°F Curie temperature; dysprosium-substituted alloys push that to ~180-190°F, which matters when driving hard in mountains.
- **Materials/systems:** NdFeB, Dy-substituted magnets, EV traction motors.

### Electrically conductive polymers (polyacetylene) misconception
- **Anchor:** `§8.p1`, `§8.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** *Electrically conductive polymers (polyacetylene) misconception* (matched from canon); also relates to *Polyacetylene conductive polymers* and *Conductive polymers hype cycle*
- **Frame in this section:** Tom's shaving-mirror epiphany. ~30 years before 2017 (i.e., ~late 1980s), a junior polymer-science faculty member claimed in a Chipman Room talk that polyacetylene's higher *specific* conductivity meant rotating machinery would abandon copper. The next morning Tom realized aluminum already has higher specific conductivity than copper but is still not used for generator windings, because absolute conductivity is what matters.
- **Materials/systems:** polyacetylene, copper, aluminum windings.
- **Teaching frame:** the "half-truth through specific properties" template Tom uses to flag overstated materials claims.

### 3D metal sintering venture (concept laser acquisition)
- **Anchor:** `§8.p3`, `§8.p4`, `§8.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** *3D metal sintering venture (concept laser acquisition)* (matched from canon); also relates to *Desktop Metal eyeglass hinges*
- **Frame in this section:** Tom names two reference points: a department spin-off that has raised $215M for metal sintering (Desktop Metal), and GE's $400M acquisition of Concept Laser. He argues current 3D-printed metal part cost (~$10,000/lb fabricated) is incompatible with automotive value-per-pound-saved (~$2/lb). Cites a Lawrence Livermore student source. Acknowledges $37,000 race-car titanium parts are economically viable; commodity automotive parts are not.
- **Materials/systems:** titanium 3D-printed parts, metal sintering.
- **Era:** 2017 (current at lecture date).

### World Trade Center collapse
- **Anchor:** `§10.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** *World Trade Center collapse* (matched from canon)
- **Frame in this section:** Used as a writing-for-your-audience example. Tom's article, written for a high-school-science-student audience in three hours at Joel Clark's invitation, was the most-read article on WTC for nine years. Touches on the conspiracy-theorist response: websites accusing Tom of being a government shill, letters to MIT's president demanding his tenure.
