## Cases developed in this lecture

### Cardiac guide wire (stainless steel weld failure)
- **Anchor:** `§1.p1`–`§1.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** Cardiac guide wire lawsuit
- **Frame in this lecture:** Physical demonstration. Tom passes around a 25-year-old guide wire whose weld tip failed at less than 2.5 lb. 304 stainless, very fine coiled wire. Anchor for lawsuit anecdote (surgeon left 30 inches inside patient).
- **Materials/systems:** 304 austenitic stainless steel, teflon coating, fine-wire coil
- **Era:** circa 1990s (instrument is "twenty-five years old" in 2015)

### Cow magnet (stainless-steel magnet demonstration)
- **Anchor:** `§1.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** Cow magnet ingestion in livestock
- **Frame in this lecture:** Physical demonstration. Stainless-steel magnet swallowed by dairy cattle to capture stray ferrous wire and protect the udders. Used as an aside off the guide-wire topic.
- **Materials/systems:** stainless steel + carbon steel; stomach-acid environment

### John Galati torpedo tube welding (Electric Boat)
- **Anchor:** `§2.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** Torpedo tube welding distortion control
- **Frame in this lecture:** Student-of-Tom anecdote. Balanced-weld procedure (alternating clock positions around the circumference) developed for ~40-foot-long torpedo tubes to control distortion.
- **Materials/systems:** thick-section steel
- **Era:** 1980s

### Davenport Iowa Alcoa plate stress relief
- **Anchor:** `§3.p8`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** Davenport Iowa plate stress relief by hydraulic jacking
- **Frame in this lecture:** Mechanical stress relief example. Six-inch plate, ten feet wide, pulled 3% in hydraulic jacks the size of half a football field. Used as the canonical case of mechanical (not thermal) stress relief in heavy aluminum plate.
- **Materials/systems:** thick aluminum plate, 70 ksi yield class
- **Era:** ongoing operation

### Naval Air Rework Facility CF-30 weld repair (Vietnam War)
- **Anchor:** `§4.p1`–`§4.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** PROPOSED: Naval Air Rework Facility CF-30 titanium vane peening (Vietnam War)
- **Frame in this lecture:** Tom's first-summer-as-intern story. Final-inspection crack in titanium compressor vane, repair by welding and shot peening using an Almen gage, Tom asked to sign off as engineer of record. Anchors the entire §4 narrative on shot peening and the §5 transition to liability.
- **Materials/systems:** titanium compressor vane, shot peening, Almen gage
- **Era:** mid-1960s

### Loose-bolt $700,000 farm equipment engine fire
- **Anchor:** `§5.p2`–`§5.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** Farm equipment engine fire (loose bolt)
- **Frame in this lecture:** Current consulting case (referenced as "last week"). Bolt-came-loose engine fire on $700k farm equipment, 100 hours old. Defense attorney's theory converts an assembly defect into a design defect via the flatbed-transport argument.
- **Materials/systems:** diesel engine, threaded fastener
- **Era:** 2015 active case

### Johnson & Johnson laser-surgery instrument coating
- **Anchor:** `§5.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** Johnson & Johnson laser surgery instrument coating
- **Frame in this lecture:** Patent and held-harmless arrangement. Tom developed an anti-reflective surface coating for J&J laser surgical instruments to prevent burn injury from reflected laser energy. Took six months of J&J legal department to issue a hold-harmless letter.
- **Materials/systems:** metal surgical instrument with anti-reflective coating

### 1985 Ford Aerostar rear axle fatigue
- **Anchor:** `§6.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** 1985 Ford Aerostar rear axle fatigue failures
- **Frame in this lecture:** Welded rear axle of HSLA steel, designed assuming fatigue strength proportional to tensile strength. Brackets welded onto axle; field failures in first six months. Big product recall. Triggered industry-wide work on fatigue of welded HSLA sheet.
- **Materials/systems:** HSLA sheet steel, fillet-welded brackets
- **Era:** 1985

### Pennsylvania cement-truck water tank explosion
- **Anchor:** `§7.p5`–`§7.p14`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** Cement truck pressure vessel explosion (Pennsylvania)
- **Frame in this lecture:** Extended teaching case. Aluminum water tank, no doubler plates, regulatory gap (DOT defers to ASME, ASME excludes water tanks, Pennsylvania attorney reads regs and concludes no code applies). Iraq-veteran welder killed during 100 psi over-pressure test repair. Earlier near-miss (leg amputation) had been attributed to a fluke; company responded by building a test cage. Tom got involved through Roger, ex-ASME committee. Hundred thousand similar vehicles on the road; National Board of Pressure Vessel Inspectors says they can't be inspected at that scale.
- **Materials/systems:** aluminum pressure vessel, fillet-welded nozzles, no doubler plates
- **Era:** post-Iraq War (post-2008)

### King Street Bridge (Melbourne)
- **Anchor:** `§8.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** King Street Bridge failure
- **Frame in this lecture:** Brittle fracture of welded bridge in Melbourne, late 1970s. Doubler-plate detail with weld going across the loaded direction; hydrogen crack from welding grew by fatigue to critical size. Major thoroughfare lost for ~1 year. Used to justify the welding-detail prohibition (don't weld around the corners).
- **Materials/systems:** welded steel bridge plate, doubler plate
- **Era:** late 1970s

### USS Thresher
- **Anchor:** `§9.p1`–`§9.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** USS Thresher
- **Frame in this lecture:** Closing case. Built at Portsmouth Naval Shipyard; lost on first controlled deep dive in the mid-1960s. Tom names the failed-braze-joint theory on seawater piping penetration. Detailed in-class explanation from a (Navy-trained) student covers ~30% inspection failure rate on seawater joints, reactor scram, ice in emergency MBT blow air system, failure to surface, breakup at test depth. SUBSAFE program and modern quality-management practices came out of this disaster. Tom alludes to a recording of the sub captain narrating hull collapse.
- **Materials/systems:** brazed seawater piping, pressure hull steel, emergency air systems
- **Era:** mid-1960s

### USS Sea Wolf (welding lessons)
- **Anchor:** `§9.p1`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** SSN-21 Sea Wolf hull cracking problem
- **Frame in this lecture:** Brief reference. Source of lessons on welding high-strength steels. Used as a parallel to the Thresher in Petroski's framing of "we learn from failure."
- **Era:** 1990s
