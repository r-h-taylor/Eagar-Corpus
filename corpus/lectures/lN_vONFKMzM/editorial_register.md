## Sectioning rationale

The lecture divides naturally into fourteen sections. §1 is a brief residual from the previous lecture's fracture mechanics topic (the Griffith energy balance), followed by §2 logistics. §3 opens the main topic (steel) with a framing argument about the "silicon age" misconception. §§4–14 trace the productivity history: Weald → Saugus → Bessemer → Carnegie → open hearth → BOF → AOD → continuous casting → 1980s shakeout → Simmons/Allegheny Ludlum → Chaparral/Forward → Mittal closing. Each break falls at a clear teaching transition (new case, new era, named figure introduction).

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could do this because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases that Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
