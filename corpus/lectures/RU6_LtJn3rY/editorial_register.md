# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 9, all sections. This register records every editorial judgment made in lifting the cleanup edit to the readable edition.*

## Sectioning rationale

The lecture divides naturally into six sections:

- **§1 (00:05–09:07)** — wrap-up of the composites material from prior lectures, structured around the "marriage of advantages and disadvantages" framing and the aluminum-silicon carbide case. Closes with a Q&A on concrete in tension.
- **§2 (09:07–15:04)** — pivot into fiberglass and resin-mold composites as Tom moves from theory to widely-used examples; closes with the cost-per-pound chart.
- **§3 (15:04–22:22)** — explicit topic pivot ("I'm going to talk now... the first material is steel"). Industry scale, productivity, and the "most technologically advanced material" argument.
- **§4 (22:22–29:48)** — pivot to corrosion as steel's Achilles heel, the eight forms of corrosion, and the black iron pipe demonstration.
- **§5 (29:48–40:25)** — narrows from corrosion in general to hydrogen embrittlement specifically. Heat-treating shop case and Sea Wolf case anchor the section.
- **§6 (40:25–end)** — pivot to phase diagram and steel metallurgy proper: hardness, hardenability, hardening ability, alloying elements.

## Conventions applied (per catalog)

Standard catalog rules applied throughout: filler removed, self-corrections smoothed, numerals normalized, "okay" preserved where it closes a thought and cut where mid-clause filler, backward-references to prior sessions cut.
