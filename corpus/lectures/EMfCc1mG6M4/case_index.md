## Cases referenced

### Black Hawk helicopter tail rotor washer failure (Arkansas)
- **Anchor:** `§1.p1`–`§2.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Black Hawk helicopter tail rotor washer failure"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Stress corrosion cracking case study. Arkansas night-vision-goggle training crash; 7075-T6 washer should have been T7351 (compression-stress-relieved as T7352). Government contractor defense; Sikorsky settled for $10M two weeks after the chief of rotors testified.
- **Materials/systems:** 7075 aluminum, T6 vs T7351 vs T7352 tempers, residual stresses, stress corrosion cracking
- **Era:** Lawsuit ~early 2010s; case material from early Black Hawk program

### CH-53/MH-53 helicopter washer corrosion problem
- **Anchor:** `§1.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "CH-53/MH-53 helicopter washer corrosion problem"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Referenced as the parallel Sikorsky engineering group that had already discovered the T6 stress corrosion problem and ordered fleet-wide washer replacement before the Black Hawk accident.
- **Materials/systems:** 7075-T6 aluminum washers
- **Era:** Pre-accident discovery, ~9–15 months before the Arkansas crash

### Aircraft landing gear and turbine engine shaft vacuum arc melting
- **Anchor:** `§3.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Aircraft landing gear and turbine engine shaft vacuum arc melting"
- **Frame in this lecture:** How 250 ksi yield landing gear is made without hydrogen cracking — triple vacuum melting gets hydrogen below half a part per million.
- **Materials/systems:** high-strength steel, vacuum arc remelting, VIM
- **Era:** Standard practice reference

### Air Force Buy-to-Fly Ratio in Aircraft Manufacturing
- **Anchor:** `§3.p4`–`§3.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Air Force Buy-to-Fly Ratio in Aircraft Manufacturing"
- **Frame in this lecture:** 32:1 historical buy-to-fly ratios down to <10:1 driven by 1980s near-net-shape manufacturing program. Cost multipliers on aluminum plate ($10/lb → $100/lb fly weight) and nickel superalloys ($100/lb → $1000/lb fly weight).
- **Era:** 1980s–present

### Electric Boat welding humidity specification violation
- **Anchor:** `§4.p1`–`§4.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Electric Boat welding humidity specification violation"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Newport News bay-doors-closed humidity practice. Navy spec prohibits welding above 80% humidity; shipyards keep doors closed even in temperate weather to control atmospheric moisture exposure.
- **Materials/systems:** shipyard practice, hydrogen control
- **Era:** Ongoing

### Magnesium anode nickel contamination failure
- **Anchor:** `§6.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Magnesium anode nickel contamination failure"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Hot-water-tank sacrificial anode. Nickel solubility in magnesium is ~7 ppm; excess nickel forms precipitates that cause local galvanic self-consumption — anode disappears in hours without protecting the steel vessel.
- **Materials/systems:** Mg anode, Ni contamination, galvanic corrosion
- **Era:** ongoing reference

### Arizona pipeline stacker bracket failure
- **Anchor:** `§9.p1`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Arizona pipeline stacker bracket failure"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Tom's insurance-adjuster consulting case. - **Materials/systems:** mild steel fillet welds, carbon steel
- **Era:** Tom's consulting career; specific date unspecified

### Massachusetts Supreme Court ICC bumper jack failure case
- **Anchor:** `§9.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Massachusetts Supreme Court ICC bumper jack failure case"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Case law on expert testimony scope. Man jacking truck by ICC bumper, bumper welds broke without frame deformation.
