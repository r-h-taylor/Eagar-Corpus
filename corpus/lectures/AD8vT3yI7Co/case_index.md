# Case Index — Lecture 5/12, Solid State Welding (Spring 2013)

*Citation format: layer 3 paragraph anchor (e.g. `§4.p2`). Click-through in the static site reveals the corresponding layer 2 passage with timestamp range.*

This lecture is methodological — its spine is the pressure-bonding spectrum, the Langmuir contamination time, and the surface energy explanation for why metals bond differently. Cases are introduced as illustrations rather than as the teaching units themselves.

## Cases referenced

### NASA-funded cold welding research (post-Mercury)
- **Anchor:** `§7.p4`, `§7.p5`, `§7.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `NASA cold welding research initiative`
- **Frame in this lecture:** The Mercury graphite-lubricant failure (knobs sticking in vacuum) motivated NASA to fund cold welding research, including the 1967 ASM conference and the *Adhesion or Cold Welding of Materials in Space Environments* volume Tom holds up.
- **Materials/systems:** Graphite (sp² basal-plane lubricant), space-vacuum conditions ~10⁻¹⁰ atm.
- **Era:** Early 1960s failures; 1967 research conference.

### Mercury / Apollo program graphite lubricant failure
- **Anchor:** `§7.p3`, `§7.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Mercury/Apollo program graphite lubricant failure`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Used to teach why graphite is a lubricant at atmosphere (CO₂/O₂ absorbed on basal planes) but an abrasive in vacuum. Astronauts' knobs sticking.
- **Materials/systems:** Graphite, sp² vs sp³ carbon bonding.
- **Era:** Early 1960s (Mercury), spilling into Apollo.

### Incandescent light bulb development (Edison and Langmuir)
- **Anchor:** `§6.p2`, `§6.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Incandescent light bulb development (Edison/Langmuir)`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Used to introduce the Langmuir as a unit of contamination time. Edison's carbonized horsehair filaments → tungsten in argon → factor-of-100 longer life from suppressing evaporation.
- **Materials/systems:** Tungsten filament, half-atmosphere argon backfill.
- **Era:** Late 19th–early 20th century.

### HMS Sheffield (Exocet missile, Falklands War)
- **Anchor:** `§4.p2`, `§4.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `HMS Sheffield`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Galvanic-corrosion theory of why the aluminum superstructure of the Sheffield burned after the Exocet hit. Connects to the steel-aluminum transition joint case (§4.p1). Tom flags this as a disputed theory.
- **Materials/systems:** 5000-series aluminum superstructure, steel hull, seawater galvanic couple, explosive-bonded transition plug.
- **Era:** 1982 Falklands War.

### MBTA thermite rail welding at Rosendale
- **Anchor:** `§4.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `MBTA Thermite Rail Welding at Rosendale`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Tom's 30-years-ago site visit. Used to illustrate thermite welding as an exchange reaction: aluminum + iron oxide → iron + aluminum oxide + heat.
- **Materials/systems:** Aluminum powder, iron oxide, firebrick-lined steel bucket, ceramic rail molds.
- **Era:** ~1980s.

### World Trade Center collapse (and the 9/11 conspiracy thermite theory)
- **Anchor:** `§4.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `World Trade Center collapse`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Tom's role as published commentator and ongoing target of conspiracy-theorist correspondence. Used pedagogically to show how observed phenomena (sparks during the collapse) admit multiple explanations — thermite reaction vs. electrical arcing of high-power conductors.
- **Materials/systems:** Structural steel; thermite reaction (aluminum + iron oxide); high-current electrical conductors.
- **Era:** 2001 event; ongoing (2013) correspondence.

### Boeing friction-stir welding machine investment
- **Anchor:** `§2.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Boeing Friction-Stir Welding Machine Investment`
- **Frame in this lecture:** $10 million capital investment for friction stir welding of aluminum wing component attachments. Used to illustrate that aluminum's 6% solidification shrinkage drove industry toward solid-state joining.
- **Materials/systems:** Aluminum aircraft structures, friction stir welding tooling.
- **Era:** ~1990s–2000s push.

### Navy ship aluminum-steel transition joints
- **Anchor:** `§4.p1`, `§4.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Navy Ship Aluminum-Steel Transition Joints - Galvanic Corrosion`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Three-ply explosive-bonded transition joint allowing fusion welding of an aluminum superstructure to a steel hull. Galvanic corrosion in seawater is the persistent problem; the Sheffield case (§4.p2) is the proposed consequence.
- **Materials/systems:** Carbon steel, 5xxx aluminum, three-ply transition joint.
- **Era:** Mid-20th century onward.

### Sulfuric acid containment in chemical plants
- **Anchor:** `§3.p2`, `§3.p3`, `§3.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** PROPOSED: Sulfuric acid containment - graphite vs titanium clad vessel
- **Frame in this lecture:** Used to motivate explosive-bonded titanium-clad steel pressure vessels. Only graphite and titanium resist concentrated sulfuric acid; graphite is brittle, titanium is silver-priced. Solution: titanium thin layer explosively bonded to a thick steel backing.
- **Materials/systems:** Concentrated sulfuric acid, graphite pressure vessels, titanium, explosive-bonded titanium-on-steel clad plate.
- **Era:** Standard mid-to-late 20th century chemical engineering practice.

### Tube-sheet heat exchanger explosive welding
- **Anchor:** `§5.p1`, `§5.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Power Plant Heat Exchanger Tube Sheet Joining`
- **Frame in this lecture:** $50M utility heat exchanger; tube-to-tube-sheet joint made by explosive welding rather than arc welding because of the 10,000-joint count. Tapered hole + internal charge + 10–15° angle = explosion-welded seal.
- **Materials/systems:** Thick steel tube sheet, heat exchanger tubing, internal explosive charge.
- **Era:** Mid-20th century onward.

### Soviet vs. American risk culture in early human spaceflight
- **Anchor:** `§7.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** PROPOSED: Early Soviet vs. American human spaceflight risk philosophy
- **Frame in this lecture:** Brief aside — Yuri Gagarin's three orbits vs. Shepard's sub-orbital arc. Used to color the NASA-cold-welding-funding story as embedded in a specific safety culture.
- **Era:** 1959–1961.

## Figures referenced

- **The Langmuir = ~10⁻⁸ atmosphere-seconds.** §6.p4. Time to form a monolayer of surface contamination at 1 atm. Recurring figure.
- **Six orders of magnitude of pressure variation in structural welding.** §5.p3. Framing statistic.
- **6% volume change on solidification for aluminum vs ~2.5% for steel.** §2.p3. Recurring figure in welding distortion teaching.
- **1,000 ergs/cm² = 1 J/m² (surface energy unit conversion).** §8.p4. Pedagogical conversion.
- **Metal surface energies ~1 J/m² vs hydrocarbons ~20 dynes/cm.** §8.p2, §8.p3. The factor underlying soldering and brazing flux chemistry.
