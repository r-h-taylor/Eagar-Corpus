## Sectioning

The 45-minute lecture was sectioned into 9 units at natural teaching transitions:
- §1 (00:02): opening framing of the pressure-bonding spectrum
- §2 (02:54): pivot to friction welding stage demos
- §3 (05:47): pivot to explosive bonding, with the sulfuric acid containment case
- §4 (11:15): transition joints opens onto the Sheffield case and thermite digression

## Convention catalog applied

Standard conventions from the §1 worked example carried through. New conventions established:
- Bracketed student-turn placeholder `[question about X]` used where Tom's response makes the question recoverable but the audio of the question itself is not in the transcript. Flagged as attribution-constructed.
- Tom's geographic / political slips treated case by case: simplified silently when rhetorical (Taiwan-Korea → China-Japan), bracketed when teaching content (none in this lecture).

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
