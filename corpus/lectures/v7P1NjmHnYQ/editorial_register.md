# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Fusion Welding, Summer 2013, Lecture 1/6. This register records editorial decisions in producing the readable edition.*

## Conventions applied (from corpus catalog)

- Student turns labeled `**Student:**`. The transcript has no speaker tags; in §1.p5 a student question is inferred from Tom's response ("does anyone know on a log scale..."), but the actual student utterance is inaudible. Flagged below.
- Stage directions for physical-object teaching moves rendered as italicized brackets (`*[Tom lights a flame]*`, `*[Tom sets up two torches and a steel welding wire]*`, etc.).
- Tom's recurring spoken slip "Forte's law" for "Fourier's law" bracketed as `Forte's [Fourier's]` on first occurrence per section, then `[Fourier's]` thereafter (§2.p1, §2.p2, §4.p1). Convention §9 of catalog.
- Numerals: monetary values as `$`-prefixed digits where Tom is being numerically specific (the slip "five hundred dollars worth of medical malpractice insurance" example doesn't apply here); ratios and orders of magnitude kept as `10³`, `10⁴`, etc. for readability of a technical lecture. Where Tom said "ten to the third" rhetorically, it's rendered as `10³`. Years as digits.
- Self-corrections smoothed; false starts cut.
- "Okay" handling per catalog §6: cut as mid-clause filler, preserved where it closes a thought.
