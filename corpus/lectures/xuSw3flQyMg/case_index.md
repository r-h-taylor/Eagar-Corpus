# Case Index — Lecture 8 (Codes and Standards, Fall 2012)

*Citation format: layer 3 paragraph anchor (e.g. `§3.p8`). This lecture is a framework / methodology lecture rather than a case-heavy lecture; cases appear largely as illustrative anecdotes for the standards-and-inspection arc.*

## Cases referenced

### Watertown Arsenal Charpy bar standards
- **Anchor:** `§2.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Watertown Arsenal Charpy bar standards"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Origin story of Charpy bar calibration — Army arsenal at Watertown (now Watertown Mall) calibrated impact bars from ~1900 because Army was interested in steel impact properties. NIST took over after Mansfield Amendment (~1972).
- **Era:** 1900–1972

### Bethlehem Steel high-toughness Charpy testing
- **Anchor:** `§2.p8`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** PROPOSED: "Tom Eagar Bethlehem Steel research engineer Charpy machine stopping"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Tom's personal anecdote — as research engineer at Bethlehem working on high-toughness steels, he routinely stopped the Charpy machine (steel too tough to break), triggering calibration requirement. Test lab ignored the requirement.
- **Era:** Tom's Bethlehem years (early career, pre-MIT)

### Alaskan Pipeline Welding Union Control
- **Anchor:** `§3.p6`, `§3.p7`, `§3.p8`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Alaska Pipeline Welding Union Control"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Tulsa Oklahoma welders' union controls field welding contracts on pipelines worldwide. Tom's 1970s Alaskan pipeline anecdote: the union told the oil company CEO they would sabotage automatic welding if introduced. Also covers the American-food union contract requirement and the worker-pays-for-rework custom.
- **Materials/systems:** API 5L line pipe, circumferential field welds
- **Era:** mid-1970s (Alaskan pipeline construction)

### Raytheon Aegis missile part aluminum bending incident
- **Anchor:** `§4.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Raytheon Aegis missile part aluminum bending incident"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Illustration of ISO 9000's "everything must have a written procedure" requirement. Navy inspector found a worker putting a torch to heat-treated aluminum with no documented procedure; plant shut down for three days.
- **Materials/systems:** heat-treated aluminum, torch operation, Aegis missile component
- **Era:** post-1990 (ISO 9000 era)

### Sweden cadmium electrical switch fire hazard
- **Anchor:** `§5.p3`, `§5.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Sweden cadmium electrical switch fire hazard"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Sweden outlawed cadmium ~30 years ago (i.e., ~1982 from a 2012 lecture). Light switches without cadmium oxide arc-suppression burned down houses; standard relaxed for electrical switches.
- **Materials/systems:** silver cadmium electrical contacts, 15-amp residential switches
- **Era:** ~1980s

### Norwegian chlorine elimination proposal
- **Anchor:** `§5.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Norwegian chlorine elimination proposal"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Bob Rose's periodic-table-with-chlorine-X'd-out gift to Tom — Norwegians proposed banning an element. Used as setup for the Congress-DNA-oxygen punchline.
- **Era:** Tom's graduate student years (Bob Rose was Tom's thesis advisor)

### Congressional DNA-destruction material ban proposal
- **Anchor:** `§5.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Congressional DNA-destruction material ban proposal"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Congress nearly passed a law banning anything that destroys DNA; would have outlawed oxygen. Used as the punchline for "politicians taking over science."

### Salem Harbor steam generator explosion
- **Anchor:** `§8.p1`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Salem Harbor steam generator explosion"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Utility steam generator (built 1960s, ~10 stories tall), inspector failed to perform required biennial inspections for ~15 years; corrosion-thinned vessel developed steam leak that killed four workers ~7-8 years before the lecture (so ~2004-2005). Illustrates inspection-protocol failure even when code is in place.
- **Materials/systems:** steam generator pressure vessel, corrosion-thinned wall
- **Era:** vessel built 1960s; failure ~2004-2005; closure announcement 2012
