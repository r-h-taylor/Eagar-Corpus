# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture: `nLWgIwqPozE`, Codes and Standards Summer 2012 [7/7]. This register records every editorial judgment in lifting the cleanup edit to the readable edition.*

## Sectioning rationale

Nine sections. The lecture pivots cleanly: it opens with a brief framing about the explosion of codes (§1), spends about seven minutes on aluminum in Navy ship design (§2), develops the aluminum design / fatigue / welding code material (§3), then makes the major topical pivot at 18:36 to CSST, which it explores in five substantial sections (§4 introduction and the speaker's home gas leak; §5 ANSI LC1 and market entry; §6 lightning; §7 grounding and impedance; §8 Arkansas class action and the slow regulatory correction), closing with the lessons section (§9). Section breaks were placed at Tom's natural teaching transitions — most clearly at 18:36 ("What I wanted to talk about is corrugated stainless steel tubing"), and within the CSST arc at the entry/lightning/impedance/litigation pivots.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
