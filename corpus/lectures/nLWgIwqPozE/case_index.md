### Corrugated stainless steel tubing (CSST) — central case
- **Anchor:** `§4.p1`, `§5.p1`–`§9.p7` (developed across six sections)
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Corrugated Stainless Steel Tubing` (also `Corrugated stainless steel gas pipe lightning strike fires`)
- **Frame in this lecture:** The lecture's summative case. Japanese earthquake-driven product, brought to U.S. market via ANSI LC1 (written by Foster Wheeler consultant, approved in Canada first to bypass U.S. bureaucracy), introduced 1997. By 1998, NFPA data shows spike in lightning-induced gas fires. Industry knew by 2001, blamed plumbers' grounding through 2007, eventually developed conductive-jacket version (Gastite) and an inferior six-coulomb competitor. The 2003 Arkansas class action settled; one Texas county banned the product. Tom advised the Massachusetts State Plumbing Board in 2003, leading to the most restrictive state installation requirements in the country.
- **Materials/systems:** thin-wall corrugated 304/316 stainless steel, polymer jacket, brass and steel fittings
- **Era:** Japanese development pre-1987; U.S. ANSI LC1 1997; problem emergence 1998–2001; class action 2003; better product 2003+; ongoing legal dispute through lecture date (2012)
