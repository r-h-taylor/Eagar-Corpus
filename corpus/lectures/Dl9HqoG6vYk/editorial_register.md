# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 9 (Dl9HqoG6vYk), 3.371 What is Engineering, Fall 2015. This register records the editorial judgments made in lifting the layer 2 cleanup to the readable edition.*

## Sectioning

Eight sections, divided at natural teaching transitions:
- §1 — administrative opener (logistics, presentation schedule).
- §2 — the central frame of the lecture: scientist-vs-engineer, capped with Reif's MIT *Spectrum* quote.
- §3 — Kahneman, System 1 / System 2 introduction.
- §4 — Johns Hopkins pad eye case (extended worked example of S1 heuristic + S2 analysis).
- §5 — "Surviving at MIT" and career anchors handouts; second half of the student-advice arc.
- §6 — logical fallacies (Engel) and the deposition trick.
- §7 — scientific method applied to the church oven fire.
- §8 — scientific method applied at MIT (Nancy Hopkins / Birgeneau / Brown).

The pad eye case (§4) and the church oven case (§7) are both substantial enough to be their own sections rather than be folded into §3 (Kahneman) or §6 (fallacies); Tom uses them as developed teaching units, not asides.

### §4 — Pad eye case
- **§4.p2:** Captioner garbling: "there is a short Marine [?] with a gun behind every tree" — the "[?]" in layer 2 was flagged as uncertain. Resolved to "a Marine with a gun" in layer 3; "short" was likely captioner misrecognition of an indefinite article or filler. Logged here for review.
- **§4.p3:** Tom is sketching at the board throughout this case. Inserted stage direction `*[Tom sketches the towed object and pad eye on the board.]*` at §4.p3.
- **§4.p4 — "hole filled" → "hole failed":** Captioner clearly garbled "failed" as "filled" (the corrected reading is unambiguous from context — Tom is comparing which failed first). Fixed silently.
- **§4.p4:** Smoothed Tom's mid-paragraph rhetorical pause "*[pause]* Yeah, Isaac Newton said something" — the pause is removed and the Newton sentence flows directly. This is permitted because Tom himself answers the rhetorical question; no student turn occurs.
- **§4.p6 — strain calculation:** Tom says "point 0 5" but the arithmetic (10/2000 = 0.005) requires the strain to be 0.005, not 0.05. Tom's spoken "0.05" multiplied by 30 million gives 1.5 million psi, but he lands on 150,000 psi, which matches 0.005 × 30M. The captioner transcribed what Tom said; Tom's spoken slip ("point 0 5" instead of "point 0 0 5") is a real misstatement, but it's recoverable from the surrounding arithmetic. Per Constitutional constraint, Tom's factual slips are preserved unless they would actively mislead. Decision: smoothed to "0.005 strain" because Tom's own next number (150,000 psi) confirms he meant 0.005, and a reader given "0.05" would be misled into thinking the arithmetic is wrong. Flagged for review.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3 (constitutional constraint)

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could do this because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases that Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
