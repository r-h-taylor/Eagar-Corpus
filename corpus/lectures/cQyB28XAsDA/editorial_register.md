# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 3, Solid State Welding (Spring 2013). This register records editorial judgments made in producing the readable edition.*

## Conventions applied (from catalog)

- Student turns labeled `**Student:**`; inaudible questions preserved as italicized stage notes.
- Stage directions in italicized brackets for physical-object teaching moves (telephone book, packing foam, board drawings, samples).
- Numerals: monetary values as `$25,000`, `$200`; years as digits; rhetorical numbers spelled out (`one hundred thousand dollar car` written as "hundred-thousand-dollar car").
- Filler ("uh," "um," mid-clause "okay," "you know") cut; "okay" preserved where it closes a thought.
- Self-corrections smoothed (e.g., "1990 steel was one hour per ton, uh one person hour per ton" → "one person-hour per ton" — does not occur here but is the model).
- Backward-references to prior sessions cut where they carry no content; preserved when they orient a teaching unit ("we talked about dislocations" at §1.p1 retained as it sets the lecture's launching point).

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3 (constitutional constraint)

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
