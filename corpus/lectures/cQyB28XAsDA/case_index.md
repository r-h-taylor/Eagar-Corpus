# Case Index — Lecture 3, Solid State Welding (Spring 2013)

*Citation format: layer 3 paragraph anchor (e.g. `§5.p5`). This lecture is a thematic survey of cold welding, friction welding, and ultrasonic welding — each illustrated with cases drawn from Ford, Sandia, GE Lynn, and the historical record.*

## Cases referenced

### Gold cohesion experiment (ultra-high vacuum, 1950s)
- **Anchor:** `§1.p5`, `§3.p1`, `§3.p2`, `§3.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Gold cohesion experiment (ultra-high vacuum, 1950s)"
- **Frame in this lecture:** The foundational experimental proof that primary chemical bonds have million-psi strength when surface contamination and roughness are eliminated. Used to set up the two-axis framework (contamination, roughness) for the rest of the module.
- **Materials/systems:** Gold, ultra-high vacuum.
- **Era:** 1950s.

### Ford aluminum ultrasonic welding research
- **Anchor:** `§5.p2`, `§5.p4`, `§5.p5`, `§6.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Ford aluminum ultrasonic welding research"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Ultrasonic welding of aluminum sheet as a near-miss process — it doesn't eliminate contamination, only redistributes it. Sample passed around class. Contrasted with Sandia's full-area-contact approach.
- **Materials/systems:** Aluminum sheet, ultrasonic sonotrode.

### Alcoa-Audi all-aluminum automobile development
- **Anchor:** `§5.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Alcoa-Audi aluminum vehicle development partnership"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Ten-year development cycle for all-aluminum auto body. Used to make the point that aluminum vehicles are technically feasible but economically marginal at consumer price points.

### Andrew Mellon all-aluminum Pierce Arrow automobile
- **Anchor:** `§5.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Andrew Mellon all-aluminum Pierce Arrow automobile"
- **Frame in this lecture:** 1930s precedent — Mellon, on the Alcoa board, commissioned an all-aluminum car. Used to make the point that cost-no-object aluminum vehicles long predate modern attempts.
- **Era:** 1930s.

### Boeing aircraft door manufacturing and cost analysis
- **Anchor:** `§5.p7`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Boeing aircraft door manufacturing and cost analysis"
- **Frame in this lecture:** 1989 student internship at Boeing manufacturing program produced the $188-per-pound weight value, close to the $200/lb figure long held secret in the industry.
- **Era:** 1989.

### Space Shuttle cost overrun
- **Anchor:** `§5.p8`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Space Shuttle cost overrun"
- **Frame in this lecture:** The shuttle missed its $10,000/lb target; was supposed to fly 100 missions a year. "An absolute disaster from a financial point of view."

### Sandia ultrasonic aluminum welding research
- **Anchor:** `§6.p5`, `§6.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Sandia ultrasonic aluminum welding research"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Sandia's variable-pressure radiused-sonotrode technique that sweeps aluminum oxide outward in a circular wave to achieve 100% area of contact. Probably developed for a nuclear warhead application; contrasted with Ford's partial-contact approach.
- **Materials/systems:** Aluminum sheet, copper sonotrode with four-foot radius.

### Manhattan Project infrastructure
- **Anchor:** `§6.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Manhattan Project infrastructure"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Post-WWII establishment of duplicate national labs (Livermore/Los Alamos, Bettis/Knolls) for redundancy and competition in nuclear weapons work.

### General Electric Lynn jet engine turbine disk friction welding
- **Anchor:** `§8.p1`, `§8.p2`, `§8.p4`, `§8.p5`, `§8.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "GE Lynn jet engine turbine disk friction welding"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Friction welding of two $25,000 titanium turbine disks to make a $70,000 part — geometry too tight for electron beam access. Used as the high-stakes example of friction welding's irreversibility and the cost of getting it wrong.
- **Materials/systems:** Titanium turbine disks.

### Automobile drive shaft universal joint friction welding
- **Anchor:** `§7.p7`, `§7.p8`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Automobile drive shaft universal joint friction welding"
- **Frame in this lecture:** The largest-volume friction welding application — universal joints on car drive shafts, welded in 1.5 seconds per part.

### Mercury/Apollo program graphite lubricant failure
- **Anchor:** `§10.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Mercury/Apollo program graphite lubricant failure"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Forward reference to Friday's lecture. Graphite is a lubricant on Earth (water vapor intercalates between planes) but an abrasive in vacuum; early Mercury shots seized when this was not understood.
- **Era:** Early 1960s.
