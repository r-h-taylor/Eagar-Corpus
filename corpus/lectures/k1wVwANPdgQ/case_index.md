## Cases developed in this lecture

### Miami Art Museum hanging garden failure
- **Anchors:** `§1.p1` through `§3.p5` (entire opening narrative arc).
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Miami Art Museum hanging garden failure"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Forensic consulting case (Tom retained by general contractor). 20-ton reinforced concrete cantilevered beams in a Miami building, hung by two three-quarter-inch threaded rods in tension, originally specified in 410 martensitic stainless. Tack welds caused delayed hydrogen cracking; beams fell in August and November 2012. Material substitution to 316 austenitic stainless raised chloride stress corrosion cracking concerns at the marine site; Tom refused to sign off as PE, sent a written notice to general contractor, who forwarded it to the owner. Final resolution: duplex stainless steel. Litigation pending as of recording (Summer 2014).
- **Materials/systems:** 410 martensitic stainless steel (hydrogen cracking); 316 austenitic stainless steel (chloride SCC risk in marine environment); duplex stainless steel (resolution).
- **Era:** 2012–2014.
- **Note:** Tom does not name the museum on tape; cluster name from aggregate.

### Hyatt Regency walkway collapse
- **Anchor:** `§2.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Hyatt Regency walkway collapse"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Structural analog. Kansas City, circa 1980. Tension threaded rods (not stainless) in shear connection failed in indoor atrium during a party; several hundred casualties. Tom invokes it as ethical-stakes anchor for his Miami decision: tension connections in occupied space.
- **Materials/systems:** carbon steel threaded rods, shear connection.
- **Era:** 1981.

### Tom Eagar's residential high-efficiency boiler chimney
- **Anchor:** `§7.p6`–`§7.p7`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Tom Eagar's home high-efficiency boiler chimney liner specification"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Personal anecdote, late 1970s. Tom declined to pay $2,000–$3,000 to line his chimney stack with superferritic stainless steel to route boiler exhaust up the stack rather than out the side wall; still vents through the side decades later. Used to illustrate the superferritic / chloride-cracking-resistance application and the role of mortgage-scale economics in materials selection at the consumer level.
- **Materials/systems:** superferritic stainless steel chimney liner; austenitic stainless (would have failed in moist-chloride condensate).
- **Era:** 1970s, ongoing.

### General Electric boiling water reactor stress corrosion cracking
- **Anchors:** `§6.p1`, `§6.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "General Electric boiling water reactor stress corrosion cracking"
- **Frame in this lecture:** GE's response to in-reactor sensitization-driven SCC, via the 304→304L→ultra-low-carbon→LN-grade (low-carbon, nitrogen-strengthened) sequence. Tom uses it to motivate the AOD-driven cost collapse for low-carbon stainless production.
- **Materials/systems:** 304, 304L, GE LN steel.
