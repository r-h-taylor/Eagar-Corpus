## Cases developed in this lecture

### Thixotropic / semi-solid casting patent suppression at MIT
- **Anchor:** `§2.p5`–`§2.p9`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Thixotropic/semi-solid casting patent suppression at MIT"
- **Frame in this lecture:** The rheocast / thixocast / thixomolding family of processes, developed by Flemings' student in 1970, licensed by MIT to a company that sat on it 10–15 years to suppress competition. Used to teach (a) the physical principle (extracting half the heat of fusion before injection) and (b) the institutional consequence — MIT's march-in-rights patent reform.
- **Materials/systems:** Lead-tin (research alloys); magnesium (Canon SLR bodies); aluminum, zinc, brass (production).
- **Era:** Research 1970; commercialization withheld until mid-1980s.

### Single-crystal turbine blades (GE/Pratt & Whitney)
- **Anchor:** `§4.p6`–`§4.p9`, with technological-history context in `§5.p2`–`§5.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Single-crystal turbine blades (GE/Pratt & Whitney)"
- **Frame in this lecture:** The $6,000 single-crystal blade as the showcase application of investment casting. Tom walks the full chain: ceramic-shell mold → polycrystalline cast → temperature-gradient furnace → pigtail seed-crystal selection → CAT-scan wall-thickness inspection. Used to teach how casting can produce the most thermodynamically demanding part in modern engineering.
- **Materials/systems:** Nickel-base superalloys with 5–6% rhenium.
- **Era:** 1950s equiaxed → 1960s directional → late-1960s single-crystal → 1980 (the specimen Tom is holding).

### Modern single-crystal turbine blade casting (core shift defect)
- **Anchor:** `§5.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Modern single-crystal turbine blade casting (core shift defect)"
- **Frame in this lecture:** 15-thousandths-inch walls with ceramic-core shift up to 5–10 thou leaving zero wall remaining; 100% CAT-scan inspection at a half-million-dollar machine; <30% scrap rate the production target.

### NASA deep-space mission plutonium-fueled thermoelectric generators with iridium containment
- **Anchor:** `§5.p6`, `§5.p8`, `§5.p10`, `§5.p12`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "NASA deep-space mission plutonium-fueled thermoelectric generators with iridium containment"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Tom's worked example of why iridium would be the perfect jet-engine material (3000°C melting point) but is impossible at scale (100 oz/year world production). Pivots into the actual deployed application: iridium spheres containing plutonium-238 for RTGs on deep-space probes, with the safety concern that a launch failure scatters nuclear-warhead material into the Atlantic.
- **Materials/systems:** Iridium (containment); plutonium-238 (heat source); semiconductor thermoelectrics.

### US Army M16 firing pin semi-solid steel injection molding program
- **Anchor:** `§6.p1`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "US Army M16 firing pin semi-solid steel injection molding program"
- **Frame in this lecture:** 1970s Army program that gave Flemings' group millions to injection-mold semisolid steel firing pins. Failed because no mold material could withstand the temperatures; 100 parts per $100,000 mold. Used to mark the limit of the semisolid process for steel.

### Attleboro gold alloy and casting operations

- **Frame in this lecture:** The two continuous casters at the Attleboro firm that uses more gold than any company in the country. Drives home why continuous casting pays even for short runs when the metal is precious. Connects to the US Mint Sacagawea-dollar contract.

### Continuous casting of steel strip (50-year unsolved challenge)
- **Anchor:** `§7.p17`–`§7.p18`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Continuous casting of steel strip (50-year unsolved challenge)"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Hazelett's attempt to extend their copper-on-steel-band process to steel strip. Failure mode: steel-on-steel has no thermal conductivity advantage; bands wear out. The "billion-dollar-if-you-solve-it" framing.

### Amtrak New Haven to Boston trolley wire / Amtrak catenary wire procurement fraud case
- **Anchor:** `§9.p1`, `§9.p6`–`§9.p12`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Amtrak New Haven to Boston trolley wire" (with associated cluster "Amtrak catenary wire procurement fraud case")
- **Frame in this lecture:** Tom's forensic case. British supplier's caster couldn't deliver enough cross-sectional-area reduction; large grains → rough surface → arcing at 100 mph. Tom rejects $6M of wire on two days' notice; British firm sues; counter-investigation by US federal authorities reveals sole-source procurement fraud (Phelps Dodge had been kept out of the bid). Wire is replaced with Phelps Dodge material via the Conform redundant-deformation process. The Providence test-track 6-mile arcing demonstration is the dispositive forensic evidence.
- **Materials/systems:** Silver-bearing copper; Conform process (British origin).
- **Era:** Job in progress at the time of Tom's involvement; case settled via criminal-fraud track.
