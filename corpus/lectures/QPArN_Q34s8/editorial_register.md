# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture: `QPArN_Q34s8` (Casting, Summer 2011, 5/7). This register records the editorial judgments made in lifting layer 2 to layer 3.*

## Sectioning rationale

Nine sections, following Tom's natural teaching transitions:

- **§1** (00:05): opener, framing continuous casting as the central productivity problem.
- **§2** (03:25): pivot to undercooling theory, then the semisolid / rheocast story and the MIT patent history.
- **§3** (12:57): pivot to shrinkage and the riser/chill-block apparatus, including the Stefan problem aside.
- **§4** (17:58): pivot to investment casting, leading into the single-crystal turbine blade as the showcase application.
- **§5** (27:32): pivot from turbine alloys to the broader Pt-group story and the NASA deep-space iridium/plutonium engine.
- **§6** (36:39): pivot back to sand casting and the range of cast-part sizes (firing pin → generator housing → zinc ingot).
- **§7** (44:31): break, then return; sustained treatment of continuous casting in copper (Attleboro / Southwire / Hazelett).
- **§8** (64:42): pivot to microstructure — segregation, dendrites, macro vs. micro segregation, homogenization by forging.
- **§9** (80:14): the trolley wire forensic case, which had been seeded at §7.p4 but is properly developed here from the metallurgy of the §8 grain-size argument.
