## Cases developed in this lecture

### Tree stand aluminum turnbuckle (White Plains, NY)
- **Anchor:** `§2.p1`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Tree stand aluminum turnbuckle lawsuit`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Forensic case used to illustrate that small amounts of porosity (four or five pores under half a millimeter in a cast aluminum turnbuckle) are not the cause of failure. Tom on the witness stand uses a five-ping-pong-balls-on-a-table analogy to convey the porosity fraction. The actual failure cause was overloading from improper climbing.
- **Materials/systems:** cast aluminum turnbuckle, homemade tree stand hardware
- **Era:** unspecified (Tom's earlier consulting work, likely 1990s–2000s)

### Quincy Shipyard rinse-tank pH failure
- **Anchor:** `§4.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Quincy Shipyard barge cracking failure`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Aluminum-cleaning rinse tank maintenance failure. Rinse water pH had drifted to 2.3 (Coca-Cola acidity). Used as cautionary example that chemical-bath cleaning requires maintenance discipline. Barges were destined to carry number six bunker oil.
- **Materials/systems:** aluminum barge structures, sodium hydroxide / acid cleaning baths
- **Era:** late 1970s / early 1980s

### Oklahoma aviation engine crankcase weld repair
- **Anchor:** `§5.p1`–`§5.p10`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Aviation aluminum crankcase weld repair dispute`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Recent (January 2014) consulting email from a family-owned engine-repair business in Oklahoma, one of the few FAA-approved shops for weld repair of Lycoming and TCM aircraft engine crankcases. The dispute: Canadian failure investigators criticized their use of 4043 filler (undermatching) on Aluminum Association 355 castings. Tom walks through the matching/undermatching/overmatching trade-off and the four classes of filler (4145 overmatching, 4009 matching, 4043 undermatching). Frames the case as a demonstration of how to give a client options without making the design decision yourself ("if an aircraft goes down...I don't want them coming back to me").
- **Materials/systems:** Aluminum Association 355 casting alloy, AMS 4280, 4xxx series aluminum filler (4043, 4047, 4145, 4009/4010/4011); Helicoil thread inserts
- **Era:** January 2014 (the consulting email); the business has been doing this work for 30 years

## Cases referenced briefly

### Erin Brockovich / hexavalent chromium
- **Anchor:** `§4.p3`, `§4.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `Erin Brockovich hexavalent chromium case`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Public-awareness anchor for hexavalent chromium toxicity. Tom uses it to explain why chromic-acid-based aluminum cleaning baths fell out of favor, and pairs it with the parallel collapse of automotive chrome plating between 1982 and the mid-1990s. Tom adds a personal recollection of disposing of chromic acid down the laboratory drain at MIT in the early 1970s.

### Ford Explorer chromic acid plating shop
- **Anchor:** `§4.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** PROPOSED: Ford Explorer chromic acid plating facility (1990s environmental retrofit)
- **Frame in this lecture:** Mid-1990s, Ford built an environmentally controlled chromic acid plating shop for Ford Explorers at a couple hundred million dollars. Used to mark the end of the chrome-plating gap that began in 1982.
- **Era:** mid-1990s

### MIT undergraduate lab chromic acid disposal
- **Anchor:** `§4.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** `MIT undergraduate lab chromic acid disposal practice`
- **Frame in this lecture:** Tom's own student-era recollection (early 1970s) of routine disposal of chromic acid down the laboratory drain after etching glassware. Used to mark how dramatically environmental standards have shifted in his career.

## Figures referenced

- **Hydrogen solubility drop in aluminum on solidification:** factor of ~100 (from ~50 down to <0.05 cc H2 / 100 g Al), §1.p1. Framing figure for the porosity problem.
- **Aluminum solidification shrinkage:** 6%, §3.p1. Framing figure for crater cracking.
- **Aluminum oxide vs. aluminum melting points:** 2000 °C vs. 660 °C, §4.p1. Framing figure for the cleaning problem.
- **ASTM A143 reference radiograph standard cost:** ~$300 (Tom's 1990s purchase) vs. ~$1300 (current), §1.p2. Aside about technical-society pricing.
- **Strength loss in 7039 aluminum at 40% porosity:** ~40% reduction in tensile strength, with elongation falling from 15 to 5 (a 70% reduction), §1.p4 and §2.p2.
