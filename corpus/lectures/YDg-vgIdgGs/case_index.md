## Cases developed in this lecture

### Corrugated stainless steel gas pipe lightning strike fires
- **Anchors:** `§1.p1`, `§1.p2`, `§1.p3`, `§1.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** *Corrugated stainless steel gas pipe lightning strike fires* (matches canonical cluster *Corrugated Stainless Steel Tubing* and *Flexible gas piping lightning strike fires*)
- **Frame in this lecture:** The opening case of the course. Product brought to market in 1998 by an industry that spent six or seven years checking codes and standards but never considered lightning. By 1999 home fires; by 2001 identified as lightning perforation. Used to set up the entire course's central theme: codes and standards as a historical repository of things that went bad, and the gap between the codes you check and the codes you didn't think to check.
- **Materials/systems:** Thin-wall corrugated stainless steel tubing for residential fuel gas distribution; contrasted with black iron pipe.
- **Era:** 1998 introduction; 1999–2001 failure recognition; ongoing.
- **Related canonical clusters referenced:** *Black iron pipe corrosion rate field observation* (mentioned as the prior-standard product); ANSI LC1 (1992) noted as the only product-specific code.
