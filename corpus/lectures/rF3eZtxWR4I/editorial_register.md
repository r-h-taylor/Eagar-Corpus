## Sectioning

Nine sections. The lecture has a clear two-act structure: an extended "why welding matters" overview through ~35 minutes (§§1–6), then the substantive opening of cold welding theory (§§7–9). Section breaks fall at natural teaching pivots:

- **§1 → §2** at the *Alexander Kielland* introduction.
- **§2 → §3** at the return to general framing ("So far as that goes, we talked about iron and steel...").
- **§3 → §4** at the particle-beam digression.
- **§4 → §5** at the AWS / "limits of joining" pivot ("So welding is poorly trained for...").
- **§5 → §6** at the show-and-tell hand-around ("If we talk about different products, here's a metal wood").
- **§6 → §7** at "Any questions before I start the first subject?" — the lecture's clearest hinge.
- **§7 → §8** at "But in fact, most materials don't have anywhere near that strength" — the pivot from theoretical bond strength to real-world weakening.
- **§8 → §9** at the modern-day pivot to bucky balls and nanotubes.
