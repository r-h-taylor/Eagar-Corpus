# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 5, 3.371 What is Engineering, Fall 2015. This register records editorial judgments in lifting the cleanup edit to the readable edition.*

## Sectioning rationale

Ten sections were used for this ~51-minute lecture. The breaks fall at natural teaching transitions:

- §1 → §2: pivot from class logistics (presentations) to topic of plagiarism/attribution.
- §2 → §3: shift to MIT's institutional position in engineering.
- §3 → §4: anecdotal pivot — Tom notices Goldman Sachs left the room messy.
- §4 → §5: return to course substance with "let's get to it."
- §5 → §6: pivot to the LIGO case as exemplar of best engineering at MIT.
- §6 → §7: from LIGO to the historical narrative of physics-vs-engineering at MIT.
- §7 → §8: from MIT institutional history to NAE founding narrative.
- §8 → §9: from NAE founding to the Twentieth Century Great Achievements list.
- §9 → §10: from twentieth-century achievements to twenty-first-century challenges.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3 (constitutional constraint)

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
