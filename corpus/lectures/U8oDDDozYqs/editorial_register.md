# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 2 (Codes and Standards, Fall 2012, U8oDDDozYqs). This register records every editorial judgment made in lifting the cleanup edit to the readable edition.*

## Sectioning

Six sections, breaking at natural teaching transitions:
- **§1 LIGO** (00:01–10:01): opens with handout admin and pivots into the sublime measurement case study.
- **§2 Spark testing** (10:01–13:59): explicit pivot — "Now let's talk about the ridiculous."
- **§3 Ball exercise** (13:59–31:54): in-class lab handout and discussion. Long section but a unified teaching unit.
- **§4 Length measurement** (31:54–38:30): pivot to "the practical… let's start with length."
- **§5 Chips to ships** (38:30–44:31): pivot from instruments to the question "what are the most precise things we manufacture?"
- **§6 Weight, time, limits** (44:31–end): pivot to weight and time after closing length.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could do this because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases that Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
