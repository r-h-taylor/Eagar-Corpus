## Provenance note

This file is a narrated technical instructional film shown by Prof. Eagar in his Fusion Welding course (Summer 2013), not a lecture in Tom's own voice. It contains no named industrial-historical cases, no consulting anecdotes, no forensic investigations — the genre of "case" that populates the corpus index elsewhere.

What it does contain is a sequence of **experimental demonstrations** — x-ray cinematography of EB welding in aluminum, high-speed optical observation of laser welding in soda-lime glass, streak-photograph reconstruction of front-wall motion, tandem-beam suppression trials. These are reproducible laboratory observations, not historical cases.

Accordingly, no case clusters from the canonical aggregate are matched, and no `PROPOSED:` cases are introduced. The film should be cross-referenced as a *teaching aid* in lectures where Tom discusses electron beam welding, laser welding, spiking defects, beam-hole physics, or tandem-beam suppression in his own voice.

## Techniques / phenomena documented (for cross-reference)

These are framing labels, not cases. Listed so the corpus search index can locate the film when Tom references these topics elsewhere.

### Electron beam welding — beam hole dynamics
- **Anchors:** `§3.p1`–`§3.p7`, `§5.p4`–`§5.p10`
- **Materials:** aluminium alloy (for x-ray transparency); copper foil and brass foil inserts as contrast / vapor-pressure probes
- **Observation methods:** transmission x-ray cinematography (200 fps), beam-hole x-ray pinhole imaging, streak photography

### Laser beam welding — beam hole dynamics
- **Anchors:** `§4.p1`–`§4.p4`, `§5.p11`
- **Materials:** soda-lime glass (for optical transparency)
- **Observation methods:** high-speed optical (8,000 fps)

### Spiking defect mechanism
- **Anchors:** `§5.p1`–`§5.p11`
- **Phenomenon:** cyclic local breakdown of the front wall at critical scrape-off rates, producing periodic deep tongues in the weld root

### Porosity formation in deep penetration welding
- **Anchors:** `§3.p5`–`§3.p6`, `§4.p3`–`§4.p4`
- **Phenomenon:** intense local evaporation and fast melt flow at the front wall produce a cave at the rear wall that collapses into porosity

### Tandem electron beam welding (defect suppression)
- **Anchors:** `§6.p4`–`§6.p7`
- **Phenomenon:** a low-power second beam (one-twelfth the power of the first) impinged into the existing beam hole stabilizes the cavity and suppresses spiking, porosity, and humping

### Laser gas cutting
- **Anchors:** `§7.p1`–`§7.p9`
- **Phenomenon:** transition from periodic ignition-and-quench cutting (regular striations) to self-sustained continuous burning (smooth surface) as travel speed crosses ~2 m/min; front-wall temperature reaches 2,150 °C at 10 m/min

### Beam-power outlook (early–mid 1980s)
- **Anchors:** `§8.p3`
- **Figures:** 100 kW EB welder routinely welding >30 cm thick plate in one pass; 300 kW EB welder under construction; 10–100 kW laser machines anticipated. These figures date the film and provide a reference point for Tom's later commentary on EB and laser power scaling.
