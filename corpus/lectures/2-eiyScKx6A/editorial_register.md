# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 5 (Deformation Processing, Spring 2012). This register records editorial judgments made in lifting the cleanup edit to the readable edition.*

## Sectioning rationale

The lecture divides into eleven sections. Tom opens with a physical-object aside (the mushroomed hammer), pivots into a question-and-answer review of inhomogeneous-flow consequences (§2), shows the etched forgings to illustrate beneficial grain flow (§3), takes a rate-effects Q&A (§4), then breaks into a substantial residual-stress detour (§5) ending in the submarine first-deep-dive anecdote. After a brief scheduling interlude (§6), he resumes inhomogeneous deformation (§7) culminating in the Conform/trolley-wire case, moves to the ring test for friction (§8), the big-press case (§9), forgeability questions (§10), and a closing classification slide (§11). The §5/§6 break and §9/§10 break are clear teaching transitions; the §3/§4 break occurs where a student question pivots the topic to rate effects.

- "Kingdom to Brunell is not a kingdom, it's a person" — Tom's slip "Kingdom to Brunell" preserved as he said it; the correct name "Isard Kingdom Brunell" bracketed [Isambard Kingdom Brunel] on first occurrence.
