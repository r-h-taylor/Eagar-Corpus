# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 3 (Summer 2014). This register records editorial judgments made in lifting the cleanup edit to the readable edition.*

## Sectioning

The lecture is the final ~11 minutes of a session and divides naturally into five teaching units:

- **§1 (00:02)** — Hot corrosion thermodynamics and kinetics on NiCrAlY coatings. Tom is working through diagrams.
- **§2 (02:29)** — Pivots to the specific case (Mexican airline turbine blade sulfidation) that motivated the diagrams.
- **§3 (04:01)** — Spawned by Tom's rhetorical question "How often does an engine fail?" — a digression on engine reliability statistics.
- **§4 (06:12)** — Resumes the teaching frame: introduces Tom Eagar's nine principles and the Venn diagram of corrosion.
- **§5 (08:23)** — The Fenway Franks hot dog cooker case, which exemplifies the "wrong material" leg of the Venn diagram.

## Flagged for editor review

1. **§3.p1** — Tom said "probability per hour of failure" but meant "probability per hour of *not* failing." Layer 3 corrects to "not failing." If the editor prefers to preserve Tom's slip with a bracket, change to "probability per hour of failure [not failing]."
2. **§5.p3** — "About two percent molybdenum" — the word "About" is editorial. Alternative: bare "Two percent molybdenum."
3. **§5.p3** — "You could have made this thing out of gold" — pronoun "You" is editorial. Tom's spoken form was elliptical ("Could have made…").

## Convention catalog (applied)

Conventions from the §1 worked example applied throughout:
- Numerals: monetary values as `$N`; rhetorical numbers spelled out; temperatures and engineering quantities as digits with units.
- Filler ("um," "uh," mid-clause "okay," "I mean," "you know") cut except where they carry shared classroom context.
- Self-corrections smoothed to corrected form.
- Captioner errors corrected silently where unambiguous from context (this lecture has many — "sulfidated" → "sold for the sofa" is the most dramatic).
- Backward-references to prior sessions thinned but preserved where they carry content (e.g., Gulf War destroyer in §5.p4).
- No stage directions in this lecture — Tom references samples he passed around in earlier sessions and one he plans to pass around tomorrow, but produces no physical object during this segment.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
