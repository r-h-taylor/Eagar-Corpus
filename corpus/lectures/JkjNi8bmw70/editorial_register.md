# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 4 (JkjNi8bmw70), 3.371 What is Engineering, Fall 2015. This register records the editorial judgments made in lifting the cleanup edit to the readable edition.*

## Sectioning

The lecture divides naturally into ten teaching units:

- §1 (00:03): A short opening tying back to the previous session's department-head problem-solving frame.
- §2 (01:34): Boeing's durable qualities — Tom moves to a new slide.
- §3 (05:42): The Big E engineering report and the integrative/deductive survey.
- §4 (08:20): "So now I want to talk a little bit about the history of engineering" — explicit pivot.
- §5 (14:23): The disciplines arrive at MIT — mechanical, metallurgy, electrical, chemical, naval, aeronautical, nuclear. Tom moves chronologically through the courses.
- §6 (18:31): "Let's go more specifically to Harvard–MIT" — explicit pivot.

- §10 (38:39): Sputnik, the Walker quote, the Walker–Noyes split, Caltech — the closing historical arc.

### §2

Flagged here for editor review — this may warrant a footnote rather than an inline bracket if the editorial dial is to preserve Tom's spoken word more faithfully.
- **§6.p5.** "Well, they did in the beginning, but nonetheless they started their own school of engineering" — smoothed for clarity; Tom's antecedent ("they had been going down that road") restored from earlier sentence.

## Soft reordering log

No content was moved across paragraph boundaries within sections in this lecture. The Tom-self-corrects-and-restarts pattern is handled by reading the corrected form in place (per convention 4), not by reordering.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
