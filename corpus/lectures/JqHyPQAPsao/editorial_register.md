## Flagged for editor review

1. **§3.p7 — "Professor Sata[wa]y [Saint-Way?]":** Identity of the MIT colleague Tom is addressing this remark to. Captioner rendered uncertainly; need a colleague-name lookup against Tom's MIT metallurgy department contemporaries.
2. **§6.p3 — "not with steady state":** Whether the smoothed reading (Tom meant "they assumed steady state and missed the transient") is the right correction. Tom's spoken word was "not with," which is technically incorrect if read literally; the transient-thermal-stress discovery is the punchline of the case, so context strongly supports the smoothed reading.
3. **§4 sectioning:** This section is brief and answers student questions during a break, on a different case from §3 and §5. Could be folded into §5 if the editor prefers fewer sections. Kept separate because the etched-identifier hydrogen-crack story is a distinct teaching case from the Wyman Gordon thread.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
