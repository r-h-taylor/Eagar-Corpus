### U.S. Army Underground Storage Tank Corrosion Study
- **Anchor:** `§1.p1`, `§1.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "U.S. Army Underground Storage Tank Corrosion Study"

### Florida high-rise copper-plated stainless steel water piping failure
- **Anchor:** `§2.p2`, `§2.p3`, `§2.p4`

### New England Aquarium exterior panel corrosion
- **Anchor:** `§4.p6`–`§4.p10`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "New England Aquarium exterior panel corrosion"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Fish-scale facade specified as passivated stainless but ground for texture, leaving active surface; rust in grinding marks within a year. Tested citric/phosphoric acid/Coke/naval jelly for in-place re-passivation.
- **Materials/systems:** passivated stainless sheet, chloride atmosphere
- **Era:** consulting circa 1980s–1990s

### Pacemaker battery case corrosion
- **Anchor:** `§5.p1`, `§5.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Pacemaker battery case corrosion"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Stainless pacemaker battery cases (for three major pacemaker companies) showing intergranular pitting; root cause traced to deionized water with pH 3.
- **Materials/systems:** stainless battery cases, deionized rinse water
- **Era:** referenced 35–40 years ago (~1970s–1980s)

### Quincy Shipyard barge cracking failure
- **Anchor:** `§5.p3`, `§5.p4`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Quincy Shipyard barge cracking failure"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Brass heating pipes (3" dia, Dowtherm-heated) inside heavy-crude barges; stress corrosion cracking traced to rinse-water pH of 2.3. Recommendation: change rinse water weekly or daily, with pH monitoring.
- **Materials/systems:** brass tubing, Dowtherm heat transfer fluid, #6 fuel oil
- **Era:** Tom's early consulting career (late 1970s, when Quincy Shipyard still operated)

### Manwich hot water tank stress corrosion cracking
- **Anchor:** `§6.p2`–`§6.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Manwich hot water tank stress corrosion cracking"
- **Frame in this lecture:** 99-gallon hot water tank (designed under ASME-exempt threshold) holding Manwich sloppy joe product at 210°F; nightly chloride disinfectant migrated under insulation; 304 stainless stress corrosion cracking; tank exploded scalding a worker with 200°F steam.
- **Materials/systems:** 304 stainless, chloride disinfectant, insulation
- **Era:** Knoxville, TN; consulting case

### Admiralty brass heat exchanger plugging failure

- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Admiralty brass heat exchanger plugging failure"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Utility heat exchanger lacked filters; solids deposit caused pit-through of admiralty brass tubes within one year. Argument for filters / maintenance.

- **Era:** consulting case (undated)

## Figures referenced

- **Soil corrosion rate:** 20 thousandths/year derived from 6-year leak through 1/8" wall (§1.p2).
- **MIC bacteria radiation tolerance:** 40,000 rad (vs. 3-minute lethal dose for humans) (§1.p5).
- **MIC literature emergence:** ~1979 first nuclear reactor reports (§1.p5).
- **Pourbaix immunity zone:** high-pH steel immunity (§1.p4).
- **Stainless chromium surface concentration:** ~100% Cr at top of >10–12% Cr alloys; oxide ~50 nm thick (§4.p1).
- **Massachusetts lead solder ban:** 1978 (§1.p6).
- **Florida condo perforation rate:** 20 thousandths stainless pierced in 24 hours, 100 ppm chloride, warm water (§2.p4).
- **ASME code exemption threshold:** 100 gallons for pressure vessels; resulting market peak at 99 gallons (§6.p3).
- **FDA hot-hold standard:** 190°F for prepared foods (§6.p2).
