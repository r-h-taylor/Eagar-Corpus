# Case Index — Lecture 8, Deformation Processing (Spring 2012)

*Citation format: layer 3 paragraph anchor. Cases are named teaching units Tom develops with forensic or industrial-historical specifics; figures are recurring numeric anchors that are not cases.*

## Cases referenced in this lecture

### Varian crossfield amplifier brazing (gold-nickel → copper-gold-nickel TLP bond)
- **Anchor:** `§2.p2`–`§2.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Varian cross-field amplifier brazing failure"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Tom's mid-1980s consulting case at Varian (North Shore, MA). Gold-nickel braze on copper-to-molybdenum joints in radar crossfield amplifiers failed 5T bend tests because Mo–Ni intermetallics are brittle. Varian had stumbled empirically onto a transient liquid phase diffusion bond using 65Cu-35Au-3Ni; Tom diagnosed it after metallography. He had to write a letter to convince the Navy the cheaper alloy was actually better.
- **Materials/systems:** Gold-nickel braze (82% Au, 18% Ni); Cu–Mo joint; Cu-Au-Ni TLP alloy.
- **Era:** 1985–1986.

### Mississippi pressure vessel explosion
- **Anchor:** `§6.p1`, `§6.p3`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Mississippi pressure vessel explosion"
- **Frame in this lecture:** 4-ft diameter, 2-inch-thick steel pressure vessel hydrotested with nitrogen at 2200 psi (1.5× the 1500 psi operating pressure). Door blew off, weighed 4000 lb, found half a mile away. One worker killed, one sandblasted and going blind. Used here as the setup for Charpy testing and cup-cone fracture samples.
- **Materials/systems:** Carbon steel pressure vessel, nitrogen hydrotest.
- **Era:** ~2011 (Tom references "last fall" testing).

### Edison New Jersey gas pipeline explosion
- **Anchor:** `§6.p2`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Edison, New Jersey gas pipeline explosion"
- **Frame in this lecture:** 42-inch diameter pipeline, 1000 psi, laid in 1960s through then-farmland. Let go in the 1990s after suburban buildup. Flames 600 ft in the air; radiant heat melted neighboring roofs.
- **Materials/systems:** Carbon steel transmission pipeline.
- **Era:** failure ~1994; construction 1960s.

### Texas gas compressor station failure (check valve / turbine reversal)
- **Anchor:** `§6.p4`, `§6.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Gas compressor station failure in Texas"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Distinct from the Mississippi case — gas distribution line where a failed check valve caused the pressurizing turbine to run in reverse. Cup-cone tensile fractures and Charpy bars from this case are passed around the class. Some steels tested were so brittle they snapped without deformation; others so tough they exceeded the 300 ft-lb capacity of the test machine.
- **Materials/systems:** Various pipeline steels.
- **Era:** ~2011.

### New Jersey prison elevator lawsuit
- **Anchor:** `§7.p5`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "New Jersey prison elevator bearing failure"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Elevator pulley bearing failed; safety brakes engaged at 1.6 G. Guard transporting three prisoners (one a former NY Giants lineman) was on the floor with a slipped disc when the surveillance camera came back on after 10–15 seconds of obstruction. Court awarded $1.6M because jury could not be told what likely happened during the blackout.
- **Materials/systems:** Elevator sheave bearing.
- **Era:** failure date not given; lawsuit context.

### Elevator cable wire hydrogen embrittlement (consulting case)
- **Anchor:** `§7.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Elevator cable brittle fracture investigation"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Recent consulting query — investigator saw brittle wire failures in an elevator cable but couldn't find intergranular fracture, so was reluctant to call it hydrogen embrittlement. Tom's point: piano-wire-class wire has been deformed so much the grains are wavy, so intergranular fracture is not the diagnostic indicator it normally is.
- **Materials/systems:** High-strength steel cable wire.
- **Era:** ~2012 (recent consultation).

### Aircraft-application high-strength steel hydrogen embrittlement
- **Anchor:** `§7.p6`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "Classified aircraft hydrogen embrittlement case"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Companion case to the elevator wire — Tom can't name it but notes it involved a very high strength steel similar to aircraft landing gear material. Same diagnostic confusion (no intergranular fracture despite hydrogen embrittlement).
- **Era:** ~2012.

### California texting-driver fatal collision
- **Anchor:** `§8.p7`
- **canonical_cluster_id:** "California texting and driving fatal collision"
- **Frame in this lecture:** Closing aside. Woman texting at 70 mph rear-ended a stopped vehicle; the other driver died in the fire. Two months later the same woman was stopped again by state police for texting. Judge sentenced her to 5 years.
- **Era:** ~2011–2012.
