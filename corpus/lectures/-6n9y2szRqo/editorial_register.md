# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 11 (full). Section §1 is the worked example carried forward unchanged; §2–§10 follow the conventions established there. This register records editorial judgments — places where the editor chose what Tom meant on the page.*

## Conventions applied (from §1 worked example)

1. Student turns labeled `**Student:**`.
2. Physical-object teaching moves rendered as italicized stage directions in brackets.
3. Monetary values: `$150`, `$500`; rhetorical numbers spelled out.
4. Self-corrections smoothed to corrected form.
5. Backward-references to prior sessions cut unless they carry content.
6. "Okay" preserved as discourse marker that closes a thought; cut as mid-clause filler.
7. "You know" / "I mean" cut except where they invoke shared context.
8. Soft reordering permitted *within* a section, logged here. Tom's slips bracketed: `Ford [Forward]` first occurrence per section, `[Forward]` thereafter.

## §4 — Chaparral wide-flange beams

### §4.p1 — Stage direction
- Layer 2: "you can, I'll tell the whole story but you can feel by taking this neodymium magnet." Stage direction inserted; the "I'll tell the whole story" forward-reference cut as off-task.

### §4.p2 — Specificity restored
- Layer 2: "they dropped the imports to the United States to zero." L3 clarifies: "they dropped the imports of wide-flange beams to the United States to zero." Without the qualifier, a reader could think Chaparral eliminated all U.S. steel imports, which Tom did not say. This is editorial gloss based on context (the surrounding sentences all concern wide-flange beam production).
- **Flag:** This is a borderline case of "adding content." The qualifier is implicit in Tom's preceding sentence ("Chaparral rolled steel beams") but not explicit in p18. Editor judgment: clarification, not new content.

Actually you couldn't do that because you have to have certifications." L3 preserves the correction sequence — Tom retracts the "free 50 ksi" claim because of certification requirements. Reading: "you could order A36 from Chaparral, they'd ship you out of this pile. You order 50 ksi, they'd ship you out of the same pile… They got 50 ksi as a premium. Actually you couldn't simply ship one as the other, because you have to have certifications…" The retraction is preserved because it's substantive teaching content.

## Decisions that are NOT permitted at layer 3 (constitutional constraint)

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
