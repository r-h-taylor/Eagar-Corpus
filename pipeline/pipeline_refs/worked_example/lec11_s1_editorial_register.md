# Editorial Register: Layer 2 → Layer 3

*Lecture 11, §1. This register records every editorial judgment made in lifting the cleanup edit to the readable edition. Where the transformation log captures mechanical rules, this register captures **decisions**: places where an editor (Taylor / Claude) chose what Tom meant on the page.*

## Conventions established

- **Speaker attribution.** Student turns get a `**Student:**` label. The raw transcript has no speaker tags; attribution is inferred from context (Tom invites questions, then repeats a question back). Where attribution is uncertain, the register flags it.
- **Stage directions.** Physical-object teaching moves (Tom brings in scissors, magnets, I-beam castings) are preserved as italicized stage directions in brackets, e.g. `*[Tom produces a pair of surgical scissors.]*`. The pocket-patting and item-locating speech that surrounds them in layer 2 is cut.
- **Numerals.** Monetary values rendered as `$150`, `$500`, etc. Years as digits (`1990`, `1975`). Counts and rhetorical numbers spelled out (`fifty-thousand-dollar watches`, `ninety-eight, ninety-nine percent`). Productivity figures with units kept as decimals (`0.3` person-hours).
- **Self-corrections.** Mid-sentence corrections smoothed to the corrected form. Layer 2 preserves the false start; layer 3 reads as if Tom had written what he meant.
- **Filler removed.** "Um," "uh," "I mean," and "you know" as discourse fillers (no semantic load) are cut. "You know" preserved where it invokes shared classroom context. "Okay" preserved where it closes a thought rhetorically; cut where it's pure filler between clauses.

## Per-paragraph decisions

### §1.p1 — Student attribution
- Layer 2 `p1` opens with Tom's class-management "Okay does anybody have any questions?" The layer 3 section title already establishes the Q&A frame, so the opener is cut. The student's question is given a `Student:` label. Editorial judgment: this is a clear question-and-answer structure, not Tom thinking aloud, and a reader benefits from knowing whose voice is asking.

### §1.p2 — The energy-content lead
- **Cut:** "Uh one is the energy content" (Tom's first-attempt answer at the head of the section).
- **Reason:** Tom gives a brief lead, then pivots to "expensive is relative," then develops the energy answer fully in §1.p3. The lead at p2 functions as a placeholder he didn't return to; on the page, it reads as a fragmentary answer that contradicts the "expensive is relative" framing that follows. Cutting it lets the section's argument arrive in order: first the relativity point (p2), then the energy point (p3).
- **This is the most consequential edit in §1.** It is a soft reordering — not of paragraphs, but of which answer arrives first. Layer 2 preserves Tom's actual order. Layer 3 reflects an editorial inference about what Tom was building toward.
- **Numerals:** "five hundred watch" → "five-hundred-dollar watch"; "one fifty at Costco" → "$150 at Costco"; "five hundred at Costco" → "$500 at Costco"; "fifty thousand dollars" preserved spelled out (this is the rhetorical landing of the paragraph).

### §1.p3 — The productivity factor
- **Collapsed:** "1990 steel was one hour per ton, uh one person hour per ton" → "in 1990, steel was one person-hour per ton." Tom self-corrects mid-sentence; layer 3 reads the corrected form.
- **Filler cut:** "I mean this is, you're seeing a revolution" → "You're seeing a revolution." "We talked about that. I mean, I said you know" → "It used to be labor and then materials, but today it's materials and energy." Tom is orienting students who were present last week; on the page that orientation is dead weight.
- **Numerals:** "twenty-five years" spelled out; "0.3" preserved as decimal.

### §1.p4 — Processing as percentage of cost
- **Percent signs restored** at three points (already in layer 2; carried forward unchanged).
- **Cut:** "But then, we've talked about, so energy cost is one thing." → "So energy cost is one thing." Backward-reference to prior lecture removed; the section can stand on its own.
- **Cut:** "I mean I kind of said well, you know" → nothing. Pure filler.
- **Cut:** "and the rest of us digging the hole in the ground" — note: "us" is captioner / Tom slurring "is". Layer 2 preserves; layer 3 reads "the rest is digging the hole in the ground."

### §1.p5 — Market positioning
- **Cut:** "Are you down here in the twenty, the ships and automobiles" — Tom starts to say "twenty" (perhaps reaching for a dollar figure) and pivots. Layer 3 reads "Are you down here in the ships and automobiles."
- **Cut:** "okay" twice as filler.
- **Numerals:** "twenty thousand dollar a pound spacecraft" → "twenty-thousand-dollar-a-pound spacecraft" (hyphenated compound adjective). "Fifty thousand dollar watches" → "fifty-thousand-dollar watches"; "ten dollar watches" → "ten-dollar watches".

### §1.p6 — Beer and advertising
- **Cut:** "I mean when I worked in," → "My first big consulting job…" Tom restarts the sentence; layer 3 reads the completed version.
- **Numerals:** "thirteen cents a gallon" preserved (Tom's chosen rhetorical form); "fifty or sixty cents a gallon" preserved.
- **Cut:** "I don't know, today" → "Today" — hedge removed; Tom uses "I don't know" as a discourse marker, not as actual epistemic uncertainty.

### §1.p7 — Scissors as case
- **Cut entirely:** the pocket-patting sequence "I'll give you, oops did I, maybe I didn't, maybe I put them back somewhere else, I had my scissors here. I showed down here there."
- **Replaced with:** `*[Tom produces a pair of surgical scissors.]*` — the stage-direction convention.
- **Reason:** Tom's physical-object teaching moves are a core feature of the corpus and the reader needs to know one is happening. The live search for the scissors is not the move; the production of the scissors is. Layer 3 records the latter and discards the former. This is the prototype example for how the corpus handles all of Tom's physical-object asides going forward.
- **Self-correction smoothed:** "five hundred worth of health, and not health insurance, medical malpractice insurance" → "about five hundred dollars worth of medical malpractice insurance." The "*not* health" misdirection that Tom corrected mid-sentence is not preserved.
- **Captioner garbling fixed:** "the big cost is medical math, the application" → "The big cost is the medical application." ("Math" was a misrecognition of Tom's hesitation; the corrected reading is what he said.)

### §1.p8 — Closing
- **Collapsed:** "Other questions? That answer your question? There is no simple answer okay. Any other questions?" → "So again, the answer is, it depends on the industry. Any other questions?" The summary line "depends on the industry" appears in layer 2 immediately above; layer 3 moves it into p8 as the closing summary, paired with the single reissued question.
- **This is the second soft reordering in §1**, on a smaller scale than the p2 lead. Layer 2 preserves Tom's actual delivery order; layer 3 lets the summary close the section rather than open the close.

## Convention catalog (for use in §2–§5)

The following conventions are established by §1 and will apply consistently across the rest of the lecture and across the corpus:

1. Student questions get `**Student:**` attribution.
2. Physical-object teaching moves get italicized stage directions in brackets.
3. Monetary values: `$150`, `$500`; rhetorical numbers spelled out.
4. Self-corrections smoothed to the corrected form in layer 3; preserved in layer 2.
5. Backward-references to prior lecture sessions cut from layer 3.
6. Tom's "okay" preserved where it closes a thought; cut where it's mid-clause filler.
7. "You know" and "I mean" cut except where they invoke shared context.
8. Soft reordering (moving an answer earlier or a summary later within a section) permitted *within* a section, never *across* sections. Logged in the register every time.

## Decisions that are *not* permitted at layer 3

- Adding new sentences in Tom's voice (Feynman/Leighton/Sands could do this because Feynman was alive to ratify; we cannot).
- Reordering content across section boundaries.
- Merging cases that Tom kept separate, or splitting cases he handled as one.
- Removing factual errors Tom made on tape. (Captioner errors are corrected silently; Tom's slips are bracketed; Tom's genuine misstatements are preserved and footnoted in layer 3 only when they would actively mislead a reader.)
