# Transformation Log: Layer 1 → Layer 2

*Lecture 11, §1. Mechanical rules applied to the raw transcript to produce the cleaned-up middle layer. This log records every change so layer 2 is reproducible from layer 1.*

## Global rules applied to all paragraphs

1. **Line-broken delivery joined.** Auto-caption line breaks (typically 3–6 words per line) are merged into continuous sentences. The raw transcript breaks every ~3 seconds of speech onto a new line; layer 2 honors only true sentence boundaries.

2. **Sentence boundaries inserted.** Periods, commas, and question marks added where spoken pauses, falling intonation, and grammatical completion indicate sentence breaks. Capitalization at sentence starts.

3. **Captioner errors corrected.** Misrecognitions of words and proper nouns are corrected silently in layer 2; logged individually below.

4. **Tom's spoken slips of memory bracketed.** Where Tom misnames a person or thing, the correction appears in brackets on first use per section: `Ford [Forward]`, then `[Forward]` alone thereafter. (None in §1.)

5. **Paragraph breaks at natural pauses.** Inserted where Tom shifts topic, takes a beat, or arrives at a closing "okay" that ends a teaching unit. Eight paragraphs in §1.

6. **Preserved as-is in layer 2:** filler words ("um," "uh," "I mean," "you know," "okay"), false starts, mid-sentence pivots, self-corrections, pocket-patting, repetitions. These are speech artifacts but they are *Tom's speech artifacts*. Removing them is editorial and belongs in layer 3, not here.
